## Theme: How God Leads Those Who Believe in Him

*A guide to understanding and experiencing divine guidance as followers of Christ*

## üìñ Through His Word (The Bible)

**Scripture**: Psalm 119:105 (NIV)
> "Your word is a lamp for my feet, a light on my path."

**Supporting Scripture**: Joshua 1:8-9 (NIV)
> "Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips; meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do everything written in it. Then you will be prosperous and successful. Have I not commanded you? Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the Lord your God will be with you wherever you go."

**Prayer Focus**: Thank God that His Word is our primary guide, revealing His character, will, and promises. Pray for a hunger to study and meditate on Scripture daily. Ask the Holy Spirit to give you clarity, conviction, and direction through the Word. Commit to meditating on God's law so that you will prosper and succeed in His purposes.

**Example**: Joshua was commanded to meditate on God's law day and night so he would prosper and succeed in leading Israel.

**Scripture**: John 16:13 (NIV)
> "But when he, the Spirit of truth, comes, he will guide you into all the truth. He will not speak on his own; he will speak only what he hears, and he will tell you what is yet to come."

**Supporting Scripture**: Acts 16:6-7 (NIV)
> "Paul and his companions traveled throughout the region of Phrygia and Galatia, having been kept by the Holy Spirit from preaching the word in the province of Asia. When they came to the border of Mysia, they tried to enter Bithynia, but the Spirit of Jesus would not allow them to."

**Prayer Focus**: Thank God for the Holy Spirit, our internal guide and counselor. Pray for sensitivity to the Spirit's leading through conviction, peace, and wisdom. Ask the Spirit to redirect your plans when necessary, just as He did with Paul. Pray for discernment to recognize when the Spirit is keeping you from certain paths or opening new ones.

**Example**: The Spirit redirected Paul's plans, stopping him from entering certain regions to guide him to God's appointed destination.

## üôè Through Prayer and Seeking God's Will

**Scripture**: Proverbs 3:6 (NIV)
> "in all your ways submit to him, and he will make your paths straight."

**Supporting Scripture**: Proverbs 3:5 (NIV)
> "Trust in the Lord with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding."

**Prayer Focus**: Commit to acknowledging God in all your ways, trusting Him to make your paths straight. Pray for God to align your heart with His purpose. Ask for guidance through peace, scripture, or circumstances. Seek His counsel before making decisions, following David's example of inquiring of the Lord.

**Example**: David often sought God's counsel before making decisions in battle (1 Samuel 23:2, 30:8).

## üë• Through Godly Counsel and Fellowship

**Scripture**: Proverbs 15:22 (NIV)
> "Plans fail for lack of counsel, but with many advisers they succeed."

**Supporting Scripture**: Acts 13:2-3
> "While they were worshiping the Lord and fasting, the Holy Spirit said, 'Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them.' So after they had fasted and prayed, they placed their hands on them and sent them off."

**Prayer Focus**: Thank God for the wisdom of mature believers, mentors, pastors, and godly friends. Pray for discernment to recognize godly counsel that confirms what God is already stirring in your heart. Ask God to surround you with wise advisers who will help your plans succeed. Pray for humility to receive counsel and correction.

**Example**: Paul and Barnabas were commissioned for ministry after prayer and counsel from the church in Antioch.

## üö™ Through Circumstances and Open Doors

**Scripture**: Romans 8:28 (NIV)
> "And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose."

**Supporting Scripture**: Genesis 50:20 (NIV)
> "You intended to harm me, but God intended it for good to accomplish what is now being done, the saving of many lives."

**Prayer Focus**: Trust that God orchestrates events to move His people where He wants them. Pray for eyes to see God's hand in both open and closed doors. Thank God that He works all things together for good. Ask for faith to believe that even difficult circumstances are part of His divine plan. Remember that closed doors can be as much guidance as open ones.

**Example**: Joseph's trials in Egypt were part of God's plan to save many lives.

**Scripture**: Colossians 3:15 (NIV)
> "Let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts, since as members of one body you were called to peace. And be thankful."

**Prayer Focus**: Pray for the peace of Christ to rule as the deciding factor in your heart when making decisions. Ask God to replace confusion and anxiety with His perfect peace. ## ‚è≥ Through His Providence Over Time

> "The Lord had said to Abram, 'Go from your country, your people and your father's household to the land I will show you.'"

**Supporting Scripture**: Proverbs 3:5-6

**Example**: Abraham was told simply, "Go to the land I will show you." God didn't give every detail‚ÄîHe led Abraham step by step.

## üìù Action Points for This Week:

1. **Daily Scripture Meditation**: Read and meditate on Psalm 119:105 and Proverbs 3:5-6 each morning this week
2. **Listen to the Spirit**: Set aside quiet time daily to listen for the Holy Spirit's gentle promptings
3. **Seek Godly Counsel**: Identify one decision you're facing and seek wisdom from a mature believer
4. **Journal God's Leading**: Write down one way you've seen God guide you this week
5. **Practice Submission**: Before making any decision, pray "Not my will, but Yours be done"
6. **Trust in Closed Doors**: If you encounter a closed door this week, thank God that He may be redirecting you
7. **Peace Check**: Before making important decisions, pause and ask, "Do I have the peace of Christ about this?"

---

## üí° Reflection Questions:

1. **Word**: Am I regularly reading and meditating on God's Word for guidance?
2. **Spirit**: Am I sensitive to the Holy Spirit's promptings, or do I rush ahead with my own plans?
3. **Prayer**: Do I seek God's will in prayer before making decisions?
4. **Counsel**: Do I have mature believers in my life who can speak wisdom into my decisions?
5. **Circumstances**: Can I see God's hand in both the open and closed doors of my life?
6. **Peace**: Is the peace of Christ ruling in my heart, or am I driven by anxiety?
7. **Providence**: Am I patient with God's timing, or do I demand immediate answers?

---

## üåü Key Truths to Remember:

- **God's Word illuminates**: Scripture is our primary and most reliable source of guidance
- **The Spirit directs**: The Holy Spirit actively guides believers into truth and redirects when necessary
- **Prayer aligns**: Through prayer, God aligns our hearts with His purposes
- **Counsel confirms**: Godly advisers help confirm what God is already speaking
- **Providence orchestrates**: God works through circumstances, both favorable and challenging
- **Peace indicates**: Inner peace is often a sign of God's will; confusion rarely is
- **Time reveals**: God's guidance often unfolds gradually; trust the process

Part One

To Plan Or Not To Plan

Should Christians Plan Their Lives?

The Core Question

Should Christians plan their lives, or should we "let go and let God"?

Many believers struggle with whether planning conflicts with trusting God.

Three Philosophies of Life

The Fatalist

"Whatever will be, will be"

Passive. Abandons responsibility.

The Humanist

"I can be whatever I want"

Self-reliant. Excludes God.

The Biblical

"I choose what God wants"

Active partnership with God.

The Biblical View

"God has a plan for me but I'm not passive in that plan. I must be active. I must do something about it. I choose what God wants for my life."

Partnership between divine purpose and human action.

Jesus on Planning

"Do not worry about tomorrow..."

Matthew 6:31-34

Seems to discourage planning

"Count the cost before building..."

Luke 14:28-31

Encourages thoughtful planning

Resolving the Tension

  • Don't be preoccupied with what you need
    Don't let your life consist of your needs and ambitions
  • Make God's will your priority
    "Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness"
  • Plan adequately for the things you want
    Your life is more important than any building you construct

Wisdom from Proverbs 16

"A man's heart plans his way, but the LORD directs his steps."

Proverbs 16:9

We Make Plans

Set the direction we want to go

God Directs Steps

Opens doors, arranges relationships, creates favor

Why You Must Plan

"If you're going to build, you have to plan. Now if you plan to build a house, a building, don't you think your life is more important than a building?"

If we plan for buildings, shouldn't we plan for our lives?

Don't Plan for Favor

"Don't sit and say I'm waiting for favor... Favor happens when you are not looking. But you don't plan with favor. Favor is a bonus."

God's supernatural favor enhances your plan, but shouldn't replace it.

Part Two

From Here To There

The Journey of Life Planning

The Planning Journey

HERE

Where you are now

Your present reality

→

THERE

Where God wants you

Your destination

"That's what a life plan is all about. Moving you from where you are to where you ought to be."

The Upward Call

"Paul doesn't call it a forward call, but he calls it upward. There's a big difference between forward and upward."

FORWARD

Same level, easier path

UPWARD

Against gravity, requires effort

Your 10-year plan is an anti-gravity movement.

What Got Me Here?

External Forces

  • Country of birth
  • Family background
  • Economic conditions
  • Cultural factors

Internal Choices

  • Education decisions
  • Relationship choices
  • Habits formed
  • Values adopted
"Your life is not an accident. There's always a logic behind everybody's life."

Four Steps to Get There

From 1 Kings 17:3-4 (Elijah at Brook Cherith)

  • GET AWAY
    Make a move, take a step
  • TURN
    Change direction, break patterns
  • HIDE
    Set yourself apart, work privately
  • DRINK
    Receive fresh input, keep learning

Step 1: Get Away

"You can't move from here to there by staying here. You have to get away. You have to take a step."
  • Talk to that person
  • Register for that course
  • Apply for that opportunity
  • Small steps lead to big outcomes

Step 2: Turn

"You always end up in a place where you are headed."

What people call "generational curses" are often simply repeated patterns:

  • Eat different food = Different health outcomes
  • Make different marriage choices = Different relationships
  • Choose different financial habits = Different prosperity

You have the power to TURN.

Step 3: Hide

"Many of the important things we have to do must be done alone. Most of the things done in public are for show. The things that really change our lives are done privately."
  • Work on character when no one is watching
  • Pray intimately with God
  • Seek God's approval over people's applause

Step 4: Drink

"You're going to learn things that are different from what you have known. You're going to expose yourself to new knowledge, new information, new relationships, new concepts."
  • New books and learning
  • Different perspectives
  • Digital/tech skills
  • Diverse relationships

Plan or Be Shocked

"I don't want the next 10 years to shock any one of us. Please, be a fool now. Not there. Take all the right steps now."

Without Planning:

10 years later: "I've been a fool"

With Planning:

10 years later: "Wow, look at what I've achieved!"

Part Three

Becoming And Winning

Mindset Transformation

The Freedom Paradox

"For though I am free from all men, I have made myself a servant to all, that I might win the more."

1 Corinthians 9:19

"I am free, but I have made myself a servant."

True freedom is choosing what enslaves you.

Choose Your Slavery

Positive Slaveries

  • Christ and the Holy Spirit
  • The Word of God
  • Righteousness, integrity, honor
  • Wisdom and learning
  • Excellence and quality
  • Discipline and hard work

Negative Slaveries

  • Sin and comfort
  • Food and entertainment
  • Spending and materialism
  • Excuses and blame
  • Laziness and procrastination

Becoming IS Winning

"The greatest achievement is not what you get, it's becoming and doing what God wants for your life."

Part 1: Becoming

The person God wants you to be

Character transformation

Part 2: Doing

The things God wants you to do

Purposeful action

The Transformation Sequence

MINDSET

How you think

→

BEING

Who you become

DOING

What you do

→

HAVING

What you get

"The greatest change happens in your head before it comes into your hand."

"I Have Become"

"You are what you choose to be. I am making myself what I want to be."
  • Though I am not highly educated, I will make myself a specialist
  • Though I was born into poverty, I will work myself out
  • Though I didn't choose my mother, I can choose my lifestyle
  • Though I didn't choose my village, I can choose my values

Choice Over Circumstance

"The core of the Bible is that no matter what was imposed on you by life, you can choose differently."
  • Your origin doesn't determine your destiny
  • The greatest achievers often come from disruptive backgrounds
  • You didn't choose your starting point, but you choose your direction

Mindset Matters More Than Money

"If you have a poverty mindset, no matter how much money you are thrown into, if you are thrown into an ocean of money, you still come out poor, because it doesn't start with money. It starts with a mindset."

Change your thinking before changing your circumstances.

Measuring Success Correctly

Wrong Metrics

  • Money accumulated
  • Buildings constructed
  • Fame achieved
  • Material possessions

Right Metrics

  • Fulfillment: Internal satisfaction
  • Significance: External impact
  • Financial Freedom: Not controlled by others
"Don't measure your life by acquisitions. Measure it by fulfillment and significance."

Discipline as Life Routine

"I keep running. I keep fighting. I keep running. I keep fighting... That's my action. If I face it and it's tough, I keep running and I keep fighting. I don't quit."

Like your heart beats routinely, your discipline must become automatic.

  • Create non-negotiable routines
  • Don't wait for external accountability
  • Maintain standards when no one is watching

Life is Hard But Livable

"Life is hard. But it is livable. It is livable. We can overcome."

No matter what life has imposed on you, you can choose differently.

Based on Philippians 3:13-14

Prayer for Transformation

"God, transform my mindset before my circumstances. Help me understand that becoming the person You want me to be IS winning. Change my thinking so I can change my doing and having."

Based on Romans 12:2

Prayer for Chosen Discipline

"Lord, I choose to enslave myself to Christ, to righteousness, to excellence, and to discipline. Help me work like a servant even when I'm free. Let my discipline become as routine as my heartbeat."

Based on 1 Corinthians 9:27

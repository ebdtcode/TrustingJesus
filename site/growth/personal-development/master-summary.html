Executive Summary

This comprehensive sermon series guides believers in developing a Biblical approach to life planning. Pastor Otabil addresses the tension between divine sovereignty and human responsibility, providing a framework for creating a 10-year life plan grounded in Scripture while emphasizing personal accountability.

The series moves from philosophical foundations (why plan?) through practical methodology (how to plan?) to mindset transformation (who must I become?).

Part 1: To Plan Or Not To Plan

Core Question

Should Christians plan their lives, or should we "let go and let God"?

Three Philosophies of Life

The Fatalist View

"Whatever will be, will be"

  • Passive approach to life
  • Abandons personal responsibility
  • Common among religious people

Problem: Prevents improvement and change

The Humanist View

"I can be whatever I want to be"

  • "I am the master of my destiny"
  • Excludes supernatural influence
  • Predominant in self-help culture

Problem: God opposes human self-sufficiency

The Biblical View

"I choose what God wants for my life"

  • Combines divine purpose with responsibility
  • Active participation in God's plan
  • Makes choices based on Biblical values

Solution: Partnership between God and human action

Jesus's Teaching on Planning

Two Key Passages:

  • Matthew 6:31-34 - "Don't worry about tomorrow" (seems to discourage planning)
  • Luke 14:28-31 - "Count the cost before building" (encourages planning)

Three-Part Resolution:

  1. Don't be preoccupied with the things you need - avoid the "hustle" mentality
  2. Make God's will your priority - "Seek first the kingdom of God"
  3. Plan adequately for the things you want - your life is more important than any building

"The Biblical view says God has a plan for me but I'm not passive in that plan. I must be active. I must do something about it. I choose what God wants for my life."

Part 2: From Here To There

The Planning Journey

Central Concept: Moving from your current position to your desired destination

Strategic Questions:

  1. Where am I now? (Current reality assessment)
  2. Where do I want to be? (Vision and destination)
  3. How do I get there? (Strategy and pathway)

Five Pillars of Life Planning

  1. Self-Assessment: Know your strengths, weaknesses, gifts, and limitations
  2. Vision: What do you want to achieve? Who do you want to become?
  3. Goals: Break vision into specific, measurable targets with timelines
  4. Strategy: Identify resources, skills, relationships, and costs
  5. Execution: Start where you are, use what you have, do what you can

Biblical Examples

  • Joseph (Genesis 41) - 14-year strategic plan saved Egypt
  • Nehemiah (Nehemiah 1-2) - Rebuilt Jerusalem's walls in 52 days through planning + prayer
  • Jesus (Luke 9:51) - "Steadfastly set His face to go to Jerusalem"

"Planning is bringing the future into the present so you can do something about it now."

Part 3: Becoming And Winning

The Central Paradox

Paul's Declaration (1 Corinthians 9:19): "For though I am free from all men, I have made myself a servant to all"

Core Principle

Becoming IS winning. Who you become determines what you win. The kind of person who wins is the kind of person you choose to become.

Choosing Your Slavery

Everyone becomes enslaved to something. The question is: Will you be enslaved to constructive or destructive forces?

Positive Slaveries

  • Slave to Christ and the Holy Spirit
  • Slave to the Word of God
  • Slave to righteousness, integrity, honor
  • Slave to wisdom and learning
  • Slave to excellence and quality

Negative Slaveries

  • Slave to sin and comfort
  • Slave to food and entertainment
  • Slave to spending and acquisition
  • Slave to excuses and blame
  • Slave to laziness

The Transformation Sequence

MINDSET → BEING → DOING → HAVING

"The greatest change that must happen will not happen in your hands. It happens in your head before it comes into your hand."

Paul's Repeated Mindset Phrases

  • "I Became" (5 times) - Intentional self-transformation
  • "That I Might Win" (5 times) - Winning mindset permeates thinking

Measuring Success Correctly

Wrong Metrics

  • Money accumulated
  • Buildings constructed
  • Fame achieved
  • Material possessions

Right Metrics

  • Fulfillment: Internal satisfaction
  • Significance: Impact on others
  • Financial Freedom: Not controlled by others

"Don't measure the plan for your life by acquisitions. You measure it by fulfillment and significance."

Comprehensive Action Framework

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

  • Identify your current life philosophy (Fatalist/Humanist/Biblical)
  • Shift to Biblical view: "I choose what God wants"
  • Write personal mission statement
  • Evaluate where you are now (honest inventory)
  • Define who you want to be in 10 years
  • List what currently enslaves you (positive and negative)

Phase 2: Planning (Months 2-3)

Seven Life Areas to Address:

  1. Spiritual Growth
  2. Family & Relationships
  3. Career & Finance
  4. Health & Wellness
  5. Education & Skills
  6. Ministry & Service
  7. Legacy & Impact

Daily Disciplines

Morning Routine

  • Spiritual: Prayer and Scripture
  • Mental: Read/learn something new
  • Physical: Exercise/movement
  • Planning: Review goals and tasks

Evening Routine

  • Reflection: What went well?
  • Gratitude: Three things thankful for
  • Preparation: Set up tomorrow
  • Rest: Prioritize sleep

Key Takeaways

  1. Christians should plan - It's Biblical, but with God's guidance
  2. Three elements must align - Don't be preoccupied; prioritize God; plan adequately
  3. Planning is a journey - From current reality to desired destination
  4. Mindset precedes success - Change your thinking before changing circumstances
  5. Choose your slavery - Everyone serves something; choose wisely
  6. Becoming is winning - Character transformation equals true success
  7. Discipline is routine - Not occasional effort, but consistent practice
  8. Circumstances don't dictate destiny - You can choose differently
  9. Measure by significance - Fulfillment matters more than acquisition
  10. 10 years is coming - Whether you plan or not, time marches on

Final Challenge

"Whether you like it or not, 2034 is coming. The question is: who will you be when you get there?"

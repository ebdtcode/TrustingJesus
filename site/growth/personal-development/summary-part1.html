Core Theme

This foundational message addresses the question every Christian must answer: "To plan or not to plan?" Pastor Otabil examines three worldviews that shape how people approach life planning, establishing the theological groundwork for the entire series.

The Big Question

Should Christians plan their lives, or should we "let go and let God"? Many believers struggle with whether planning conflicts with trusting God.

Three Philosophies of Life

1. The Fatalist

"Whatever will be, will be"

  • Passive approach to life
  • Abandons personal responsibility
  • Common among religious people

Problem: Prevents improvement and change

2. The Humanist

"I can be whatever I want"

  • "Master of my destiny"
  • Excludes God entirely
  • Rooted in Garden of Eden rebellion

Problem: God opposes human self-sufficiency

3. The Biblicist

"I choose what God wants"

  • Active partnership with God
  • Divine purpose + human action
  • The ideal Christian position

Goal: This is the mindset to adopt

Jesus on Planning: Resolving the Tension

Matthew 6:31-34

"Do not worry about tomorrow..."

Seems to discourage planning

Luke 14:28-31

"Count the cost before building..."

Encourages thoughtful planning

Three-Part Resolution

  1. Don't be preoccupied with the things you need - avoid the "hustle" mentality that consumes your life
  2. Make God's will your priority - "Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness"
  3. Plan adequately for the things you want - your life is more important than any building you construct

Biblical Wisdom: Proverbs 16

Proverbs 16:2-3 - Motives and Commitment

  • We determine what we want - "All the ways of a man appear pure in his own eyes"
  • God examines and corrects our motives - "But the Lord weighs the spirit"
  • Commitment brings establishment - "Commit your works to the Lord"

Proverbs 16:9 - Human Plans, Divine Direction

  • What Man Does: "A man's heart plans his way" - We should initiate plans
  • What God Does: "The Lord directs his steps" - God shows us what to do

How God Directs:

  • Opening doors we never saw
  • Directing us into new relationships
  • Rearranging people around us
  • Leading into unexpected paths

Power Quotes

"The Biblicist view says God has a plan for me but I'm not passive in that plan. I must be active. I must do something about it. I choose what God wants for my life."

"Whether you plan or not, you will grow. But what kind of grown do you want to be?"

"The fact that you're not 100% sure does not mean don't plan."

"Sometimes sympathy can be your greatest enemy in life. Champions are made by coaches who have no sympathy for your tiredness but still push you because there is a target to achieve."

Actionable Steps

Immediate Self-Assessment

  1. Identify Your Current Philosophy - Are you a fatalist, humanist, or biblicist?
  2. Evaluate Your Priorities - Is your life preoccupied with needs, or occupied with God?
  3. Examine Your Motives - What deep drives are shaping your goals?

Relational Assessment

  1. Audit Your Relationships - Who are your sympathizers vs. encouragers?
  2. Evaluate for Your Season - Which relationships need to change?
  3. Surround Yourself - Find people who push you forward

Becoming And Winning

Part 3 - Comprehensive Summary

Pastor Mensa Otabil
35:37

Core Theme

The Central Truth

Becoming IS winning. Who you become determines what you win. The kind of person who wins is the kind of person you choose to become.

This part focuses on mindset transformation rather than strategic planning because "most of the time, people put a lot of things on paper but they don't do it." What you put on paper won't happen by itself. You make things happen.

The Central Paradox: Freedom and Chosen Slavery

1 Corinthians 9:19

"For though I am free from all men, I have made myself a servant to all"

Who I Am

"I am free"

What life has given to me naturally. Paul was a free Roman citizen - unrestrained, not bound by obligation.

What I Have Made Myself

"I have made myself a servant"

The effort I've put in to become something else. Paul chose to be enslaved.

"I am free to do whatever I want, but I choose to be a servant. What kind of mindset is that?"

Choosing Your Slavery

Universal truth: Everybody is a slave to something. You must choose what kind of slave and whose slave you want to be.

Destructive Slaveries

  • Slave to sin
  • Slave to food (sugar, carbs)
  • Slave to football
  • Slave to fun
  • Slave to spending

Warning sign: You know you're a slave when you're doing something you know is not good for you but you keep doing it.

Constructive Slaveries

  • Slave to Christ
  • Slave to the Holy Spirit
  • Slave to the Word of God
  • Slave to righteousness, integrity, honor
  • Slave to wisdom and learning
  • Slave to excellence and quality

Marking Your Own Paper

"You get to a point in life where nobody marks your paper for you. You mark your own paper."

When no one is overseeing you, you must:

  • Choose your own slavery
  • Set your own standards
  • Push yourself

"I study because I am a slave to learning. I'm a slave to quality. I'm a slave to excellence. I'm a slave to integrity. I'm a slave to honor. I have enslaved myself because if you don't enslave yourself, you're always going to require somebody else to oversee your life."

Two Key Phrases from Paul

"I Became As" (5x)

Demonstrates intentional self-transformation

You are what you choose to be. You are making yourself what you want to be.

"That I Might Win" (5x)

Demonstrates winning mindset

Purpose drives all transformation. Clear definition guides all action.

The connection: I'm going to win if I become. Becoming is winning.

What Winning Really Means

  1. Become the person God wants me to be (self-actualization, character transformation)
  2. Do the things God wants me to do (purposeful action, meaningful achievement)

Measuring Success Correctly

Wrong Metrics

  • Money accumulated
  • Buildings constructed
  • Fame achieved
  • Material possessions

Right Metrics

  • Fulfillment: Internal satisfaction
  • Significance: Impact on others
  • Financial Freedom: Not being controlled

"You can be famous and miserable. You can be rich and just miserable. The greatest achievement is not what you get - it's becoming and doing what God wants for your life."

The Transformation Sequence

MINDSET → BEING → DOING → HAVING

"The greatest change that must happen will not happen in your hands. It happens in your head before it comes into your hand."

On poverty mindset: "If you have a poverty mindset, no matter how much money you are thrown into - if you are thrown into an ocean of money - you still come out poor, because it doesn't start with money. It starts with a mindset."

Paul's Two Routines

1. Repeated Thoughts

The thought loop: "I must become, I must win. I must become, I must win."

  • Marriage problems: "I must become a better husband so I can win"
  • Leadership challenges: "I must become a better leader so I can win"
  • Any struggle: "I must become better. I must improve. I must learn more."

"It's so easy to complain without becoming."

2. Repeated Actions

The action loop: "I keep running. I keep fighting."

  • If it's tough: I keep running and I keep fighting
  • I don't quit because I keep running and I keep fighting
  • It's called routine - the running routine, the fighting routine

Life Can Be Overcome

"The core of the Bible is that no matter what was imposed on you by life, you can choose differently."

  • You didn't choose your mother, but you choose your lifestyle
  • You didn't choose your village, but you choose your values
  • You didn't choose your poverty, but you choose your pursuit

"Life is hard. If anybody tells you the opposite, tell them they haven't lived yet. It's hard, but it is livable. We can overcome."

Power Quotes

"The person who makes things happen is you. It's you. It's not what you have on paper."

"I must choose whose slave I become. Everybody is a slave to something."

"You are what you choose to be. I am making myself what I want to be."

"Many of us are pursuing things. But in life, we should pursue being. Because if you pursue being, being will get the things."

"Don't measure the plan for your life by acquisitions. You measure it by fulfillment and significance."

Actionable Steps

Mindset Assessment

  1. Identify your current slaveries - What are you enslaved to that is destroying you?
  2. Choose your constructive slaveries - What will you commit to being enslaved to?
  3. Adopt the "I must become" mentality - Stop blaming, start becoming

Routine Development

  1. Establish repeated thoughts - Create your thought loop ("I must become, I must win")
  2. Establish repeated actions - What will you keep running at? Keep fighting for?

Achievement Reframe

  1. Redefine success - Shift from acquisitions to significance
  2. Ask the right question - Not "What do I want to get?" but "Who do I need to become?"

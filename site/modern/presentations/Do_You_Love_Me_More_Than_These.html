Do You Love Me More Than These?

A Call To Radical Discipleship

"So when they had eaten breakfast, Jesus said to Simon Peter, 'Simon, son of Jonah, do you love Me more than these?' He said to Him, 'Yes, Lord; You know that I love You.' He said to him, 'Feed My lambs.'"

John 21:15

Jesus Is Looking for "A Few Good Men"

"Jesus is looking for committed disciples who will answer His call in the day of His power. Not merely attendees, but radical followers who will say YES to His commission."
This powerful message calls believers to examine the depth of their love for Jesus Christ and challenges the church to embrace radical discipleship in this critical hour.

A Post-Resurrection Encounter

  • Peter had denied Jesus three times during His trial
  • After the resurrection, Jesus meets the disciples by the Sea of Galilee
  • They had fished all night and caught nothing
  • Jesus told them to cast the net on the right side - miraculous catch
  • Jesus prepared breakfast for them on the shore
  • Then came the threefold question: "Do you love Me?"

Foundations of Radical Discipleship

1. The Call to Willing Troops

Psalm 110:3 - In the Day of God's Power

"Your people shall be volunteers in the day of Your power; in the beauties of holiness, from the womb of the morning, You have the dew of Your youth."

Psalm 110:3 (NKJV)

God Is Seeking Willing Volunteers

  • The word "people" actually means "troops" - we are in spiritual warfare
  • We live in the "day of His power" - particularly in these last days
  • Jesus Christ is Commander-in-Chief recruiting volunteers
  • Many are called, but few are chosen
  • Those who are chosen are those who actively CHOOSE to respond
  • God is looking for troops who will stand for righteousness

2. Love as the Foundation

John 21:15-17 - Three Questions

"He said to him the third time, 'Simon, son of Jonah, do you love Me?' Peter was grieved because He said to him the third time, 'Do you love Me?' And he said to Him, 'Lord, You know all things; You know that I love You.' Jesus said to him, 'Feed My sheep.'"

John 21:17

Everything Flows from Love

  • Jesus didn't ask Peter about theology or preaching ability
  • Jesus didn't ask about his past failures - He asked about LOVE
  • All Christian service must flow from genuine agape love for Christ
  • "Do you love Me MORE than these?"
  • What are "these"? Other people, possessions, positions, pursuits?
  • Jesus Christ must hold the supreme place in our affections

3. From Failure to Restoration

Through Love

  • Peter denied Christ three times
  • Jesus gave him three opportunities to affirm love
  • Past failures do not disqualify us from future service
  • God is more interested in the condition of our hearts
  • Restoration comes through returning to our first love
  • Re-establishing Jesus as Lord brings restoration

4. Love Expressed Through Service

John 21:15-17 - The Commission

Three Affirmations

  • "Do you love Me?"
  • "Do you love Me?"
  • "Do you love Me?"

Three Commissions

  • "Feed My lambs"
  • "Tend My sheep"
  • "Feed My sheep"
Love is demonstrated through practical obedience and sacrificial service to God's people.

Love Is Not Merely Emotional

  • Love for Christ must be practical, not just sentimental
  • Those who truly love Jesus care for what He cares about
  • His sheep - His church - His kingdom
  • Our love is proven by our willingness to serve others in His name
  • Feed the lambs (young believers)
  • Tend the sheep (care for and protect)
  • Feed the sheep (mature believers need nourishment too)

5. The Cost of Radical Discipleship

John 21:18-19 - The Prophecy

"Most assuredly, I say to you, when you were younger, you girded yourself and walked where you wished; but when you are old, you will stretch out your hands, and another will gird you and carry you where you do not wish." This He spoke, signifying by what death he would glorify God. And when He had spoken this, He said to him, "Follow Me."

John 21:18-19

Jesus Doesn't Hide the Cost

  • Jesus prophetically told Peter about his martyrdom
  • Radical discipleship may cost us everything
  • Our lives, our rights, our comfort, our preferences
  • "Follow Me" - not to ease, but to a cross
  • The call to discipleship is a call to die to self daily
  • Living wholly for Christ, regardless of personal cost

6. Focus on Your Own Calling

John 21:20-22 - What About Him?

"Then Peter, turning around, saw the disciple whom Jesus loved following, and said to Jesus, 'But Lord, what about this man?' Jesus said to him, 'If I will that he remain till I come, what is that to you? You follow Me.'"

John 21:20-22

Don't Compare Your Calling

  • Peter asked about John's future - Jesus redirected him
  • "What is that to you? You follow Me!"
  • Don't compare your calling with others
  • Don't be distracted by what God is doing in someone else's life
  • Each disciple has a unique path and calling
  • Our responsibility: follow Jesus faithfully, wherever He leads

üí° Practical Applications

Living Out Radical Discipleship

How to Apply This Message

  • 1. Examine Your Love: Regularly ask yourself, "Do I truly love Jesus more than anything or anyone else?" Let this be a heart check.
  • 2. Choose to Be Chosen: Recognize that God is calling you to radical discipleship. Make the conscious decision to fully surrender to His lordship.
  • 3. Serve His Sheep: Demonstrate your love for Christ by caring for His people. Get involved in ministry and mentorship.

How to Apply This Message

  • 4. Count the Cost: Understand that following Jesus may cost you everything. Make the decision now that He is worth it.
  • 5. Stay Focused on Your Call: Don't be distracted by what God is doing with others. Keep your eyes on Jesus.
  • 6. Be Willing in the Day of His Power: This is an urgent hour. Stand up, step forward, and say, "Here I am, send me."

üôè Prayer Points

Scripture-Based Prayers

1. Prayer for Supreme Love for Christ

Heavenly Father, we cry out for hearts that burn with undivided love for Your Son, Jesus Christ. Like Peter, we have failed You, denied You, and placed other things before You. We ask You to restore our first love and to make Jesus the supreme treasure of our hearts. Lord, we declare with Peter, "You know all things; You know that we love You!" Let our love for Christ be greater than our love for comfort, possessions, reputation, or even our own families. Consume us with holy passion for Jesus that compels us to serve You with abandoned devotion.

Based on Matthew 6:33

2. Prayer for Willing Hearts

Lord of Hosts, we recognize that we are living in the day of Your power, and You are calling for willing volunteers to serve in Your army. We respond to Your call and say, "Here we are, send us!" Make us troops who are willing to stand for righteousness, to advance Your kingdom, and to fulfill the Great Commission. Remove every hesitation, fear, and compromise from our hearts. We choose to be chosen. We volunteer to be among the "few good men" who will serve You with excellence and total commitment in this generation.

Based on Psalm 110:3

3. Prayer for Faithful Shepherding

Good Shepherd, You have called us not only to love You but to demonstrate that love by caring for Your people. Give us Your heart for the sheep - for the lambs who are young in faith, for the weak who need tender care, and for the mature who need continual feeding on Your Word. Raise up faithful shepherds, teachers, and mentors in our midst who will feed Your flock with truth, protect them from wolves, and lead them to green pastures. Help us to see every opportunity to serve others as an opportunity to express our love for You.

Based on 1 Peter 5:2

4. Prayer for Grace to Embrace the Cost

Lord Jesus, You never promised us an easy path. You told Your disciples that following You would cost them everything, even their lives. We ask for grace to count the cost and to embrace it willingly. Deliver us from the spirit of comfort and complacency that weakens the church. Give us the spirit of the martyrs who loved not their lives unto death. Whether You call us to literal martyrdom or to daily die to our own desires, give us strength to say, "Not my will, but Yours be done."

Based on Matthew 16:24

5. Prayer for Restoration from Past Failures

Merciful Father, we thank You that our past failures do not disqualify us from future service. Just as You restored Peter after his three denials, restore every believer who has fallen, failed, or denied You. Speak words of recommissioning over those who are paralyzed by guilt and shame. Remind us that Your mercies are new every morning and that You specialize in resurrection and restoration. Turn our mourning into dancing and our ashes into beauty as we return to You with our whole hearts.

Based on Psalm 51:17

6. Prayer for Undistracted Focus

Faithful Lord, deliver us from the trap of comparison and competition in the body of Christ. When we are tempted to look at what You are doing in someone else's life and ask, "What about them?" remind us of Your words to Peter: "What is that to you? You follow Me!" Help us to run our own race with perseverance, to steward our unique calling with faithfulness, and to celebrate what You are doing in the lives of our brothers and sisters without envy or distraction. Keep our eyes fixed on Jesus, the author and finisher of our faith.

Based on Hebrews 12:1-2

7. Prayer for the Church to Arise

God of Revival, we pray for a mighty awakening in Your church. Shake us from spiritual slumber, religious routine, and lukewarm Christianity. Raise up a generation of radical disciples who will turn the world upside down for Jesus Christ. Let Your church be known not for our buildings or programs, but for our extravagant love for Jesus and our sacrificial service to others. Pour out Your Spirit upon us and thrust us into the harvest fields as laborers who are willing to do whatever it takes to see Your kingdom come and Your will be done on earth as it is in heaven.

Based on Matthew 9:37-38

Declaration of Commitment

Questions for Reflection

  • Do I truly love Jesus more than anything or anyone else?

  • Am I willing to count the cost and embrace radical discipleship?
  • How am I demonstrating my love for Christ by serving His sheep?
  • Am I comparing myself to others instead of focusing on my calling?
  • Have I truly answered His call to be a willing volunteer?

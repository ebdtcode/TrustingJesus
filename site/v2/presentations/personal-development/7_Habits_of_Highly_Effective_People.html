Habits 1-3

Private Victory

From Dependence to Independence

Habit 1

Be Proactive

"Between stimulus and response lies a person's ability to choose how to react."

  • Take responsibility for your life and choices
  • Focus on your Circle of Influence, not Circle of Concern
  • Use proactive language: "I choose" vs "I have to"
  • Respond based on values, not emotions
Action Steps

Habit 1: Apply It

This Week

Monitor your language. Notice reactive phrases ("I can't," "I have to," "If only"). Replace them with proactive alternatives.

Circle of Influence

Draw two circles. List concerns in the outer, what you control in the inner. Focus energy on the inner circle.

30-Day Challenge

Work only on your Circle of Influence. Watch it grow as you focus your efforts there.

Habit 2

Begin with the End in Mind

"All things are created twice: first mentally, then physically. Before you act, act in your mind first."

  • Define your personal mission statement
  • Identify your core values and principles
  • Visualize the outcomes you want in life
  • Align daily actions with your overarching purpose
Action Steps

Habit 2: Apply It

Funeral Exercise

Imagine your funeral. What would you want family, friends, and colleagues to say about you? Write it down.

Mission Statement

Draft a personal mission statement defining who you want to be and what you want to accomplish.

Role Review

Identify your key roles (parent, spouse, professional). Define what success looks like in each.

Habit 3

Put First Things First

"The key is not to prioritize your schedule, but to schedule your priorities."

Q1: Urgent + Important

Crises, deadlines

DO
Q2: Not Urgent + Important

Planning, growth

SCHEDULE
Q3: Urgent + Not Important

Interruptions

DELEGATE
Q4: Not Urgent + Not Important

Time wasters

ELIMINATE
Action Steps

Habit 3: Apply It

Weekly Planning

Spend 30 minutes every Sunday planning your week. Schedule Quadrant 2 activities first.

Time Audit

Track how you spend time for one week. Categorize by quadrant. How much is in Q2?

Say No

Practice saying "no" to Q3 and Q4 activities so you can say "yes" to what matters most.

Habits 4-6

Public Victory

From Independence to Interdependence

Habit 4

Think Win-Win

"Win-Win isn't about being nice; it is a character-based code for human interaction and collaboration."

Scarcity Mindset

  • If you win, I lose
  • Competition and hoarding
  • Fear-based decisions

Abundance Mindset

  • Enough for everyone
  • Collaboration and sharing
  • Trust-based relationships
Action Steps

Habit 4: Apply It

Identify Relationships

Think of a relationship where you tend to think Win-Lose. What would Win-Win look like?

Abundance Practice

This week, share credit, celebrate others' wins, and find solutions that benefit everyone.

Win-Win or No Deal

In your next negotiation, seek solutions where both parties benefit. If not possible, agree to "No Deal."

Habit 5

Seek First to Understand

Then to Be Understood

"Most people do not listen with the intent to understand; they listen with the intent to reply."

  • Practice empathic listening - listen to understand
  • Diagnose before you prescribe
  • Reflect feelings and meaning, not just words
  • Then present your ideas in their terms
Action Steps

Habit 5: Apply It

Empathic Listening

In your next conversation, focus entirely on understanding. Don't plan your response. Just listen.

Reflect Back

Practice saying "So what you're feeling is..." before sharing your perspective.

One-on-One Time

Schedule dedicated time with someone important. Spend that time only listening.

Habit 6

Synergize

"The whole is greater than the sum of its parts. Synergy is the highest activity of life."

  • Value differences - don't just tolerate them
  • Seek third alternatives - not your way or my way
  • Build on diverse strengths
  • Create innovative solutions through collaboration
Action Steps

Habit 6: Apply It

Value Diversity

Identify someone who thinks differently. Ask: "What can I learn from their perspective?"

Third Alternative

When you disagree, ask: "Are we willing to search for a solution better than either has proposed?"

Team Synergy

In your next team project, create space for diverse ideas. Combine perspectives into something new.

Habit 7

Sharpen the Saw

Continuous Renewal and Growth

Habit 7

The Four Dimensions

"Give me six hours to chop down a tree and I will spend the first four sharpening the axe."

💪 Physical

Exercise, nutrition, rest

📚 Mental

Learning, reading

🙏 Spiritual

Values, meditation

🤝 Social

Relationships

Action Steps

Habit 7: Apply It

Weekly Renewal

Schedule one hour per week for each dimension: Physical, Mental, Spiritual, Social.

Daily Minimum

Establish a daily "sharpening" routine - 15 min reading, 30 min exercise, 10 min meditation.

P/PC Balance

Review: Are you producing results at the expense of your capacity to produce? Restore balance.

To Plan Or Not To Plan

Developing A Plan For My Life

Pastor Mensa Otabil | ICGC Christ Temple

A 10-Year Life Planning Framework (2024-2034)

The Fundamental Question

"Whether you plan or not, you will grow... but what kind of grown do you want to be?"

  • Every person has a philosophy of life
  • Your philosophy determines your approach to planning
  • The next 10 years are coming whether you plan or not

Three Views of Life

How do you approach life?

The Fatalist
The Humanist
The Biblicist
View 1

The Fatalist View

"Whatever will be, will be"

  • Life is already determined by fate or destiny
  • Passive approach - not doing anything about life
  • Abandons personal responsibility
  • Prevents growth and improvement

The Problem

Fatalism leads to passivity and stagnation

View 2

The Humanist View

"I am the master of my destiny, the captain of my ship"

  • No place for any external spiritual force
  • Rooted in Garden of Eden rebellion
  • Prevalent in Western self-help culture
  • Excludes God from life planning

The Problem

God opposes prideful self-sufficiency

View 3

The Biblicist View

"I choose what God wants for my life"

  • God has a plan, but I must actively participate
  • Combines divine purpose with personal responsibility
  • Neither passive nor self-sufficient
  • Partnership between God's will and human action

The Goal

Develop a life plan from a Biblicist mindset

Jesus Christ on Planning

An Apparent Contradiction

Don't Worry

"Do not worry saying what shall we eat or what shall we drink... do not worry about tomorrow for tomorrow will worry about its own things."

Matthew 6:31-34 (NIV)

Plan Ahead

"For which of you intending to build a tower does not sit down first and count the cost whether he has enough to finish it?"

Luke 14:28-31 (NIV)

Key Insight

"If you plan to build a house... don't you think your life is more important than a building?"

The Resolution: Three Principles

  • 1. Don't be preoccupied with things you need
  • 2. Make God's will your priority - "Seek first the Kingdom"
  • 3. Plan adequately for the things you want

"Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you."

Matthew 6:33 (NIV)

Biblical Wisdom on Planning

Proverbs 16

Motives and Commitment

"All the ways of a man are pure in his own eyes, but the LORD weighs the spirits. Commit your works to the LORD and your thoughts will be established."

Proverbs 16:2-3 (NIV)

  • We determine what we want
  • God examines and corrects our motives
  • Commitment brings establishment

Human Plans, Divine Direction

"A man's heart plans his way, but the LORD directs his steps."

Proverbs 16:9 (NIV)

What We Do

Plan our way

What God Does

Direct our steps

How God Directs Our Steps

  • Opening doors we never saw
  • Directing us into new relationships
  • Rearranging people around us
  • Leading us into unexpected paths

"I make my plan but God will order my steps. God will show me what to do."

Relationships and Progress

"At a certain point in your life, you don't need sympathy. You need people who say 'hey, let's stop, let's make it.' You need encouragers."

  • Sometimes sympathy can be your greatest enemy
  • Champions are made by coaches who push despite tiredness
  • Certain relationships may not serve your current season

Divine Sovereignty & Human Responsibility

Purpose God has a plan We must discover and choose it
Direction God directs steps We must plan our way
Motives God weighs the spirit We must commit our works
Results God establishes thoughts We must initiate action

Self-Assessment Questions

  • What philosophy has been governing my life?
  • Am I waiting passively or actively partnering with God?
  • What deep drives are shaping my goals?
  • Who in my life encourages growth vs. sympathizes with stagnation?
  • Am I willing to make plans even without 100% certainty?

Action Steps

Identify Your Philosophy

  • Fatalist? "Whatever will be will be"
  • Humanist? "I am the master of my fate"
  • Biblicist? "I choose what God wants for me"

Adopt the Biblicist Mindset

Believe God has a plan AND accept responsibility to actively pursue it

Prayer Focus

"Father, You know the end from the beginning. You are the Alpha and the Omega. We consciously bring ourselves into alignment with Your will. Help us, Lord, that as we build a life plan we'll do it with the right mindset so that the future of our lives will be greater than our past. In Jesus' name, Amen."

The Choice Is Yours

The next decade is coming whether you plan or not

"The fact that you're not 100% sure does not mean don't plan."

The Foundation

The Secret — The Power of Thought

Andrew Carnegie believed any person could achieve greatness if they understood the philosophy of success and followed specific steps to achieve it. He challenged Napoleon Hill to spend 20 years codifying this philosophy.

"Whatever the mind can conceive and believe, it can achieve."

The Core Insight

  • Thoughts are things — when mixed with definiteness of purpose, persistence, and burning desire, they become powerful forces capable of attracting their physical equivalent
  • The secret is woven into every chapter of the book — it is not stated directly because it works more effectively when discovered through personal readiness
  • Success comes to those who become success-conscious; failure comes to those who allow themselves to become failure-conscious
  • Every person who wins in any undertaking must be willing to burn their ships and cut all sources of retreat — only then do you have the state of mind known as a burning desire to win

The Edwin Barnes Story

  • Barnes arrived at Edison's laboratory looking like a tramp, but with the mind of a partner
  • He didn't say "I'll try" — he said "I will go into business with Edison"
  • He waited five years in Edison's organization, holding the vision every single day
  • His opportunity came when others rejected Edison's dictating machine — Barnes saw fortune where others saw failure
  • The lesson: desire backed by faith knows no such word as impossible
Principle 1

Desire — The Starting Point of All Achievement

Not a hope, not a wish, but a keen, pulsating desire that transcends everything else. The starting point of all achievement is desire — and you must know exactly what you want.

The 6 Steps to Turning Desire into Gold

  1. Fix in your mind the exact amount of money you desire — definiteness of purpose
  2. Determine exactly what you intend to give in return for the money you desire
  3. Establish a definite date by which you intend to possess the money
  4. Create a definite plan for carrying out your desire and begin at once, whether or not you are ready
  5. Write out a clear, concise statement combining all four elements above
  6. Read your written statement aloud twice daily — once before sleeping, once upon waking. As you read, see, feel, and believe yourself already in possession

Key Principles

  • Burn all bridges behind you — leave yourself no possible way of retreat
  • Every failure brings with it the seed of an equivalent advantage
  • Dreams are not born of indifference, laziness, or lack of ambition — they are born of a burning obsession
  • The person who desires riches in the same spirit a drowning person desires air — that person is almost certain to acquire them

"There is one quality which one must possess to win, and that is definiteness of purpose — the knowledge of what one wants, and a burning desire to possess it."

Principle 2

Faith — Visualization & Belief in the Attainment of Desire

Faith is a state of mind that can be induced through auto-suggestion. It is the head chemist of the mind — when mixed with thought, the subconscious instantly translates it into its spiritual equivalent.

How Faith Works

  • Faith is the head chemist of the mind — when mixed with thought, the subconscious mind instantly picks up the vibration and translates it into its spiritual equivalent
  • Any impulse of thought repeatedly passed to the subconscious mind is eventually accepted and acted upon
  • Thoughts magnetized with emotion act as a seed — when planted in the subconscious, they germinate and multiply
  • The subconscious mind makes no distinction between constructive and destructive thoughts — it acts on whatever it receives
  • This is why negative thought patterns are so dangerous — the mind translates them into physical reality just as readily as positive ones

The Self-Confidence Formula

  1. I know I have the ability to achieve my definite purpose in life
  2. My dominating thoughts will eventually reproduce themselves in outward action and transform into physical reality
  3. I will devote 30 minutes daily to concentrated thought on the person I intend to become
  4. I have clearly written down a description of my definite chief aim and will never stop trying until I develop the self-confidence to attain it
  5. I fully realize that no wealth or position can long endure unless built upon truth and justice; I will engage in no transaction that does not benefit all whom it affects

"Riches begin in the form of thought. The amount is limited only by the person in whose mind the thought is put into motion."

Principle 3

Auto-Suggestion — The Medium for Influencing the Subconscious Mind

Auto-suggestion is the agency of communication between the conscious thinking mind and the subconscious. It is the bridge — the only known bridge — through which you can voluntarily plant ideas in the subconscious.

The Mechanism

  • Repetition of thought is the only known method of voluntarily planting ideas in the subconscious
  • You must write out your definite chief aim, commit it to memory, and repeat it aloud daily
  • Mere words read from a page have no influence on the subconscious — they must be emotionalized with feeling and belief
  • Your subconscious mind recognizes and acts only upon thoughts that have been well-mixed with emotion or feeling
  • The dominating thoughts which you voluntarily place in your subconscious, and clothe with emotion, constitute the motivating forces that direct every movement and action

Practical Application

  • Go to a quiet place, close your eyes, and repeat aloud your written statement of the amount of money you intend to accumulate and the time limit for its accumulation
  • As you do this, see yourself already in possession of the money
  • Concentration, combined with desire, creates a powerful force that can overcome lack of self-confidence or any other weakness
  • By taking inventory of your mental assets and liabilities, you can discover and overcome weaknesses through applied auto-suggestion

"You are what you are because of the dominating thoughts that you permit to occupy your mind."

Principle 4

Specialized Knowledge — Personal Experiences or Observations

Knowledge is only potential power. It becomes power only when organized into definite plans of action and directed to a definite end. The missing link in education is that institutions fail to teach how to organize and use knowledge.

The Henry Ford Lesson

  • When Ford was called "ignorant" in court, he replied: "I have a row of electric push-buttons on my desk. By pushing the right button, I can summon any person I need to answer any question"
  • An educated person is not one who has an abundance of general knowledge — but one who has so developed their mind that they may acquire anything they want
  • The word "educate" derives from the Latin word educo meaning to draw out, to develop from within
  • Ford surrounded himself with people who could supply the knowledge he needed — this is the power of the Master Mind

Sources of Knowledge

  1. Your own experience and education — the foundation
  2. Experience and education of others through your Master Mind group
  3. Colleges, universities, and formal training institutions
  4. Public libraries — the repository of all human knowledge
  5. Specialized courses and self-directed research and study

Critical Insight

  • Successful people never stop acquiring specialized knowledge related to their major purpose
  • The person who stops studying because they've "finished school" is forever hopelessly doomed to mediocrity
Principle 5

Imagination — The Workshop of the Mind

The imagination is literally the workshop where all plans are fashioned. The impulse, the desire, is given shape, form, and action through the aid of the imaginative faculty of the mind.

Two Forms of Imagination

  • Synthetic Imagination — arranges old concepts, ideas, and plans into new combinations. Creates nothing new, but works with existing material of experience, education, and observation. This is the faculty used most frequently
  • Creative Imagination — the faculty through which new ideas, "hunches," and inspirations come. It operates only when the conscious mind is vibrating at an extremely rapid rate, as when stimulated by a strong emotion like desire. It connects you to Infinite Intelligence and to the subconscious minds of others
  • Both faculties grow stronger with use, like muscles

The Enchanted Kettle

  • An old country doctor sold a kettle, a paddle, and a secret formula to a young clerk for $500 — the clerk's entire life savings
  • What the clerk really purchased was an idea. He added a secret ingredient the doctor never knew: imagination
  • That idea became Coca-Cola, a fortune that surpassed the miraculous performance of Aladdin's lamp
  • Billions of dollars of wealth flowed from a single idea — proof that ideas are the starting point of all fortunes

"Ideas can be transmuted into cash through the power of definite purpose, plus definite plans."

Principle 6

Organized Planning — The Crystallization of Desire into Action

You must have practical plans to carry out your desire for riches. When one plan fails, replace it immediately with a new plan. Temporary defeat is not permanent failure — it is simply a signal to make new plans.

Attributes of Leadership

  1. Unwavering courage based on knowledge of self and occupation
  2. Self-control — one who cannot control themselves can never control others
  3. A keen sense of justice — no leader can command respect without fairness
  4. Definiteness of decision — those who waver show uncertainty
  5. Definiteness of plans — a leader who moves by guesswork is like a ship without a rudder
  6. The habit of doing more than paid for — one of the penalties of leadership
  7. A pleasing personality — slovenliness and carelessness repel followers
  8. Sympathy and understanding for followers
  9. Mastery of detail — efficient leadership requires mastery of details
  10. Willingness to assume full responsibility for the mistakes of followers
  11. Cooperation — great leaders lead by consent and with cooperation

The QQS Formula

  • Quality of service — render the best you are capable of, constantly improving
  • Quantity of service — render all the service you can at all times, going the extra mile
  • Spirit of service — cultivate the habit of agreeable, harmonious conduct that encourages cooperation

Critical Warning

  • There are over 30 major causes of failure — study them and make sure none stand between you and achievement
  • Most people who fail have the habit of quitting when overtaken by temporary defeat
  • A quitter never wins and a winner never quits — write this somewhere you will see it every day
Principle 7

Decision — The Mastery of Procrastination

Analysis of several hundred people who had accumulated fortunes revealed that every one of them had the habit of reaching decisions promptly. People who fail to accumulate money have the habit of reaching decisions very slowly, if at all, and of changing them quickly and often.

The Law of Decision

  • Definiteness of decision always requires courage — sometimes very much of it
  • Successful people reach decisions promptly and change them slowly, if and when they change them at all
  • People who fail reach decisions slowly and change them frequently and quickly
  • Procrastination — the opposite of decision — is a common enemy that must be conquered
  • The value of decisions depends upon the courage required to render them
  • Tell the world what you intend to do, but first show it

Lessons from History

  • The signing of the Declaration of Independence required a definite decision by 56 men who risked their lives
  • The decision to translate desire into action was what separated the Founding Fathers from the masses
  • Those who reach decisions promptly and definitely know what they want and generally get it
  • The leaders in every walk of life decide quickly and firmly

"The world has a habit of making room for the person whose words and actions show that they know where they are going."

Principle 8

Persistence — The Sustained Effort Necessary to Induce Faith

Persistence is to the character of a person what carbon is to steel. The basis of persistence is willpower. Without persistence, you will be defeated before you even start.

8 Factors That Cultivate Persistence

  1. Definiteness of purpose — knowing what you want is the first and most important step
  2. Desire — it is comparatively easy to maintain persistence when pursuing intense desire
  3. Self-reliance — belief in your ability to carry out a plan encourages you to persist
  4. Definiteness of plans — organized plans, even imperfect ones, encourage persistence
  5. Accurate knowledge — knowing your plans are sound encourages persistence; guessing destroys it
  6. Cooperation — sympathy, understanding, and cooperation with others tend to develop persistence
  7. Willpower — concentrating your thoughts on making plans to attain your definite purpose
  8. Habit — persistence is the direct result of habit; the mind absorbs daily experiences

4 Steps to Develop Persistence

  1. A definite purpose backed by a burning desire for its fulfillment
  2. A definite plan expressed in continuous action
  3. A mind closed tightly against all negative and discouraging influences, including the negative suggestions of relatives, friends, and acquaintances
  4. A friendly alliance with one or more persons who will encourage you to follow through with both plan and purpose

"There may be no heroic connotation to the word persistence, but the quality is to a person's character what carbon is to steel."

Principle 9

Power of the Master Mind — The Driving Force

Power is essential for the accumulation of money, and power is defined as "organized and intelligently directed knowledge." No individual may have great power without availing themselves of the Master Mind.

The Master Mind Defined

  • Definition: "Coordination of knowledge and effort, in a spirit of harmony, between two or more people for the attainment of a definite purpose"
  • No two minds ever come together without thereby creating a third, invisible, intangible force which may be likened to a third mind
  • When a group of individual brains are coordinated in a spirit of harmony, the energy of each brain forms an affinity with all the other brains — together they create something greater than the sum of the parts

Two Characteristics

  • Economic — surrounding yourself with the advice, counsel, and personal cooperation of people who willingly help you plan and achieve your goals
  • Psychic — when minds are blended in harmony, a spiritual connection emerges that elevates everyone involved to a higher level of insight and creativity

Historical Examples

  • Andrew Carnegie attributed his entire fortune to the power of his Master Mind group — a team of approximately 50 people whom he assembled for the specific purpose of manufacturing and marketing steel
  • Henry Ford began his career poor, uneducated, and illiterate. Within 10 years of aligning with Edison, Firestone, and other great minds, he became one of the wealthiest men in America
  • Mahatma Gandhi wielded tremendous power — more than 200 million people moved in unison at his request — drawn entirely from the principle of the Master Mind

"Energy is nature's universal set of building blocks, out of which she constructs every material thing in the universe — including people. The mind is energy."

Principle 10

The Mystery of Sex Transmutation — Channeling Creative Energy

Transmutation means the changing or transferring of one element or form of energy into another. The emotion of sex brings into being a state of mind that can be channeled into extraordinary creative, professional, or financial achievement.

The 10 Mind Stimuli

  1. The desire for sex — the most powerful of all stimuli
  2. Love
  3. A burning desire for fame, power, financial gain, money
  4. Music
  5. Friendship between people of the same or opposite sex
  6. A Master Mind alliance
  7. Mutual suffering — as experienced by people who are persecuted
  8. Auto-suggestion
  9. Fear
  10. Narcotics and alcohol — destructive and dangerous stimuli

Key Insights

  • The majority of people never learn that sexual desire has other possibilities beyond physical expression
  • People of the greatest achievement are those who have highly developed sexual natures and who have learned the art of transmutation
  • Scientific research reveals that most people do not achieve greatly until after age 40 — not from slowed activity but because they have not yet learned to channel creative energy
  • The combination of love, sex, and romance can lift a person to the status of a genius
Principle 11

The Subconscious Mind — The Connecting Link

The subconscious mind is the connecting link between the finite mind of a person and Infinite Intelligence. It is the intermediary through which one may draw upon the forces of Infinite Intelligence at will.

7 Positive Emotions

Desire Faith Love Sex Enthusiasm Romance Hope

7 Negative Emotions

Fear Jealousy Hatred Revenge Greed Superstition Anger

Critical Principles

  • The subconscious mind works day and night. You cannot entirely control it, but you can voluntarily hand it any plan, desire, or purpose you wish transformed into concrete form
  • Positive and negative emotions cannot occupy the mind at the same time — one or the other must dominate. It is your responsibility to make sure that positive emotions constitute the dominating influence
  • Form the habit of applying and using the positive emotions. Eventually they will dominate your mind so completely that the negatives cannot enter
  • The subconscious mind is more susceptible to influence by impulses of thought mixed with feeling or emotion than by those originating solely from reasoning
  • You may voluntarily plant in your subconscious any plan, thought, or purpose you desire — through the method described in the chapter on auto-suggestion
Principle 12

The Brain — A Broadcasting & Receiving Station for Thought

The brain is both a broadcasting station and a receiving station for the vibration of thought. Through the medium of the ether, the brain can communicate with other brains. This principle holds the key to understanding the creative power of the mind.

The Communication Framework

  1. The subconscious mind is the "sending station" — it transmits thought vibrations outward to other minds
  2. Creative Imagination is the "receiving set" — it picks up thought vibrations from outside sources and from Infinite Intelligence
  3. Auto-suggestion is the medium by which you voluntarily send thoughts from your conscious mind to your subconscious
  4. Your subconscious connects to every other subconscious mind through Infinite Intelligence — everything is energy in different forms

How It Works

  • When the brain vibrates at a rapid rate — stimulated by one or more of the 10 mind stimuli — it becomes far more receptive to thought from outside sources
  • Thoughts charged with strong emotion are broadcast more powerfully and are received more clearly by other brains
  • What we call intuition, hunches, deja vu, and foreknowledge are actually thoughts received from the subconscious minds of others through this interconnection
  • The emotion of sex stands at the head of the list of stimuli that most effectively increase brain vibration and receptivity

"Everything in the universe is made of time, space, energy, or matter. Because matter is just energy in a different form, then everything is actually different parts of the same thing."

Principle 13

The Sixth Sense — The Door to the Temple of Wisdom

The Sixth Sense is the portion of the subconscious mind referred to as the Creative Imagination. It is the apex of the philosophy — the point at which all other principles converge. It defies description to anyone who has not mastered the other twelve principles.

Understanding the Sixth Sense

  • Through the Sixth Sense, Infinite Intelligence may and will communicate voluntarily, without any effort from or demands by the individual
  • It is described as the "guardian angel" — a medium of contact between the finite mind and Infinite Intelligence
  • This principle can only be understood and applied by first mastering the other twelve principles — it is the culmination of the entire philosophy
  • The Sixth Sense does not operate unless you nurture and stimulate it through the dedicated application of all other principles

Hill's "Invisible Counselors" Technique

  • Each night before sleep, Hill would close his eyes and imagine sitting at a council table with great historical figures — Emerson, Edison, Darwin, Lincoln, Carnegie, and others
  • He asked each imaginary counselor for the qualities he most admired in them: faith, courage, creative thinking, persistence
  • Over time, these imaginary meetings became so realistic that the characters took on distinct personalities and offered surprising insights
  • The technique trains the mind to receive flashes of inspiration and creative ideas — it cultivates the Sixth Sense through disciplined imagination

"Through the aid of the Sixth Sense, you will be warned of impending dangers in time to avoid them, and notified of opportunities in time to embrace them."

The Obstacles

The Six Basic Fears — Ghosts That Block Success

Before you can put the philosophy of success to work, you must prepare your mind to receive it. That preparation requires an understanding of and confrontation with six forms of fear — plus a seventh evil: susceptibility to negative influences.

1. Poverty
Destroys ambition, imagination, desire, and the will to act. Leads to indifference, indecision, doubt, worry, over-caution, and procrastination. The most destructive of all fears.
2. Criticism
Robs initiative, destroys imagination, breeds self-consciousness and inferiority. Causes lack of poise, personality weakness, extravagance, and lack of ambition. Nearly as universal as the fear of poverty.
3. Ill Health
The fear of disease, even without cause, often produces the physical symptoms of the disease feared. 75% of doctor visits are hypochondria — imaginary illness born from negative auto-suggestion.
4. Loss of Love
The most painful of all fears. Breeds jealousy, fault-finding, gambling, and reckless behavior. Probably plays more havoc with body and mind than any of the other basic fears.
5. Old Age
Grows from the thought that old age brings poverty and loss of freedom. Causes premature slowing down, killing of initiative, and the false belief that you are "slipping" because of age.
6. Death
Can be conquered by accepting death as an inescapable event. Life is energy — energy cannot be destroyed, only transformed. "Death is mere transition." A busy person seldom has time to fear death.

The 7th Evil: Susceptibility to Negative Influences

  • More subtle and more deadly than any of the six fears — it is the habit of leaving your mind open to the negative influence of others
  • Protect yourself with willpower: recognize that you are by nature lazy, indifferent, and susceptible to all suggestions that reinforce your weaknesses
  • Deliberately seek the company of people who influence you to think and act for yourself
The Master Key

The Master Key to Riches

The culmination of the entire philosophy in its purest, most actionable form.

"You have absolute control over only one thing — and that is your thoughts. This is the most significant and inspiring of all facts known to humankind. It reflects our divine nature."

The Final Principles

  • Kill the habit of worry by reaching a general blanket decision that nothing life has to offer is worth the price of worry

The Call to Action

  • Or you may neglect to make your life what you want, and you will be adrift on the seas of circumstance
  • The master key is intangible, but it is powerful: the privilege of creating in your own mind a burning desire for a definite form of riches
  • There is no penalty for using this key, but there is a price if you do not use it: the price is failure

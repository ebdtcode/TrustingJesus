Sermon

What Will You Do With What God Gave You?

Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Matthew 25:14-30 • 1 Thessalonians 2:4

Entrusted With the Gospel

“But as we have been approved by God to be entrusted with the gospel, even so we speak, not as pleasing men, but God who tests our hearts.”

1 Thessalonians 2:4 (NKJV)

The Call

God has entrusted every believer with the gospel—the responsibility of taking it to the whole world.

Three Commitments

1. Prioritize the Message

The gospel must be first in our time, talents, and treasure.

2. Personalize the Mission

The Great Commission is your responsibility, not just the pastor’s.

3. Participate Fully

God is not looking for part-time missionaries.

Prioritize the Message

  • The gospel cannot be something we “get to when we get to it”
  • It must be first on our schedule—in our time, talent, and treasure
  • It is the power of God unto salvation

“The gospel has to be prioritized. It has to be first.”

Personalize the Mission

  • When Jesus said “Go into all the world,” He was speaking to all of us
  • Don’t delegate your calling to someone else
  • Your mission field begins right where you are

“This responsibility is my responsibility.”

The Parable

The Parable of the Talents

Matthew 25:14–30

Entrusted With His Goods

“For the kingdom of heaven is like a man traveling to a far country, who called his own servants and delivered his goods to them. And to one he gave five talents, to another two, and to another one, to each according to his own ability.”

Matthew 25:14–15 (NKJV)

Two Types of Believers

⚖

Active

Working, contributing, investing in the kingdom

⛔

Passive

Consuming, procrastinating, burying their resources

Which group will you belong to?

The Danger of Procrastination

“Procrastination is disobedience in slow motion.”

— Bishop Darlingston Johnson

  • The faithful servants immediately went to work
  • Good intentions without action are unfaithfulness
  • If not now, when? If not you, who?

Work While It Is Day

“I must work the works of Him who sent Me while it is day; the night is coming when no one can work.”

John 9:4 (NKJV)

  • People are not going to hell seasonally—they go every day
  • The harvest is ripe every day
  • You’ll rest when you go to heaven

God’s Measure of Faithfulness

  • It’s not the amount you have that matters
  • God looks at what you do with what you have
  • The servant with two talents received the same commendation as the one with five

Faithfulness is measured by obedience, not abundance.

Five Loaves and Two Fish

  • The boy didn’t have much—but he gave what he had
  • When you place your best in the Master’s hand, He multiplies it
  • Don’t wait until you have enough—give what you have now

The Principle

It’s not how much you have. It’s what you choose to do with what you have.

Stewards, Not Owners

  • Nothing we have belongs to us—it is the Lord’s
  • We are managing someone else’s time, treasure, and talents
  • One day we will give an account of our stewardship

“Try earning money without a brain. The money isn’t yours.”

Promise and Warning

“Well done, good and faithful servant; you were faithful over a few things, I will make you ruler over many things. Enter into the joy of your lord.”

Matthew 25:21 (NKJV)

The Faithful

Greater responsibility, greater rulership, eternal reward

The Unfaithful

Loss of reward, joy, influence, and significance

Eternal Perspective

  • What he has given us is of great value—yet nothing compared to what He plans to give
  • 70 years of time is little compared to eternity
  • If you are faithful with earthly things, He will entrust you with eternal things

“If you’re not faithful in this, who will entrust you with true riches?”

Prayer for Faithful Stewardship

  • Lord, help me prioritize Your gospel in my time, talent, and treasure
  • Deliver me from procrastination and the comfort of inaction
  • Give me courage to take what I have—little or much—and place it in Your hands
  • Let me live as a faithful steward, accountable to You alone

Living It Out

  • Evaluate: Am I prioritizing, personalizing, and fully participating?
  • Stop procrastinating—start using your resources today
  • Remember: you are a steward, not an owner
  • Live for His eternal approval, not earthly applause

Not Home Yet

“A missionary returned home after years of faithful service. There were no crowds, no bands. He prayed, ‘Lord, no one was here to welcome me.’ And the Spirit whispered: ‘But you’re not home yet.’”

— Bishop Darlingston Johnson

What will matter is not the applause of earth, but the approval of heaven.

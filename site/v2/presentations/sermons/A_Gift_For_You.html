Study Notes

Sermon Summary

A Gift For You — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview & Key Scriptures

Central Message — Jesus Christ is God’s personal gift to every individual. He is not merely a gift to the world in general, but a gift with your name on it. Like a Christmas present that must be unwrapped and enjoyed, Jesus must be personally received, appropriated, and celebrated.

Luke 2:10–11 (NKJV) — “Then the angel said to them, ‘Do not be afraid, for behold, I bring you good tidings of great joy which will be to all people. For there is born to you this day in the city of David, a Savior who is Christ the Lord.’” — The primary text of the sermon. The angel’s announcement to the shepherds reveals that the good news is both universal (“all people”) and deeply personal (“unto you”).

Isaiah 55:11 (NKJV) — “So shall My word be that goes forth from My mouth; it shall not return to Me void.” — Used to affirm that God always keeps His promises. What He speaks will come to pass, no matter how long the silence lasts.

Genesis 3:15 — The first messianic promise: the seed of the woman would crush the serpent’s head. For 5,000 years, prophets declared “He will come,” and at the fullness of time, “He will come” became “He has come.”

Numbers 23:19 — “Has He said it? Will He not do it?” — Referenced to reinforce the certainty and reliability of God’s spoken word through seasons of waiting and silence.

Summary 1 of 5

Main Teachings (Part 1)

God Chooses the Excluded — The most important announcement in history was not made in a king’s palace or a beautiful temple, but in a quiet field at midnight to shepherds — people who were rejected, unclean, and at the lowest rung of society. God does not value people by titles, education, possessions, or status. In His eyes, every person has equal worth.

“Unto You” — The Personal Gift — If the angel had only said “good news for all people,” the shepherds would have excluded themselves because years of rejection had conditioned them to believe they were not part of “all people.” God specifically added “unto you” so they would know they were included. Many believers today disqualify themselves from God’s promises through self-evaluation and comparison with others.

Unwrap the Gift — Using the illustration of his granddaughter Anna eagerly unwrapping a Christmas gift, Bishop Johnson teaches that Jesus must be personally received, taken possession of, and enjoyed. Many Christians lack joy because they haven’t “unwrapped” Jesus — they haven’t discovered how big the gift truly is. God provides help through the Holy Spirit, pastors, and fellow believers. The greater the revelation of who Jesus is, the greater the joy.

Don’t Respond to God with Fear — The shepherds’ first response was fear, echoing Adam and Eve hiding in the garden. From Genesis to the disciples on the stormy sea, people consistently respond to God with fear when they should respond with joy. God does not come to harm us but to save us, protect us, and arm us with truth.

Summary 2 of 5

Main Teachings (Part 2)

Our Faith Is Rooted in History — Christianity is not based on myths, fables, philosophy, or someone’s dream. On a specific day during King Augustus’s reign, in Bethlehem, a baby was born. He grew up in Nazareth, walked Israel’s streets for 33 years, performed miracles, was crucified, died, and rose on the third day. There were eyewitnesses — women at the empty tomb, disciples who touched Him, over 500 who saw Him alive. Jesus is a real Savior providing real salvation for real people with real needs.

God Always Keeps His Word — For approximately 5,000 years, from Genesis to Malachi, the prophets declared “He will come.” Then came 400 years of silence between Malachi and Luke — no prophets, no fresh word from God. But silence does not mean God has forgotten. At the fullness of time, over 400 prophecies were fulfilled to the letter. God’s word never returns void; it is impossible for His word not to come to pass.

A “This Day” God — We can look back at yesterday and see evidence of God’s faithfulness. We can look forward to tomorrow and anticipate His promises. But our God is a “this day” God. This day He is your Savior. This day He is answering prayer. This day He is working miracles, giving breakthroughs, guiding steps, and being faithful. We serve a right-now God and an on-time God.

The Gift Is God Himself — The word “Lord” in Luke 2:11 is the translation of Yahweh — God. In Christ, the giver is God and the gift is God. God has not merely given us a gift; God has given us Himself. If God has given you God, He has given you the answer, the breakthrough, the healer, the strength — everything you need.

Summary 3 of 5

Principles & Warnings

Principle: Equal Worth in God’s Eyes — God does not evaluate people by titles, education, wealth, or outward accomplishments. The shepherds — society’s rejects — were just as valuable to God as kings. You are somebody because you are God’s child.

Principle: God’s Presence Transforms Any Place — When God shows up where you are, that place becomes the most important, most holy, most exalted place. You are the temple of the Holy Spirit — God lives in you, making you an exalted place.

Principle: God’s Word Is Certain — No word of God is void of power. It is impossible for the word that God speaks not to come to pass. Silence does not mean God has forgotten — it means the fullness of time has not yet arrived.

Warning: Don’t Think Yourself Superior — Never think you are better than someone because of your position, title, education, car, house, or clothing. God sent His most important message to shepherds, not kings.

Warning: Don’t Think Yourself Inferior — Never look at someone else and determine your value based on what they have and you lack. Don’t let the opinions of men define who you are. Stand straight, lift your head, and know that God loves you equally.

Warning: Don’t Exclude Yourself from God’s Promises — Many believers disqualify themselves from what God has freely given because they are looking at themselves rather than at the Giver. Stop excluding yourself based on self-evaluation, comparison, or what others have said about you.

Warning: Don’t Doubt During Seasons of Silence — If God has spoken to you and now there is silence, do not start to doubt or question His faithfulness. Go back to what is written. Remind yourself of the promises. The word remains true across millennia.

Summary 4 of 5

Practical Takeaways & Call to Action

Receive the Gift Personally — Understand that Jesus is God’s gift specifically to you. Your name is on it. Take Him personally, give God praise, celebrate Him, and thank Him for what He has done.

Unwrap Jesus — Don’t leave the gift unopened. Ask the Holy Spirit to help you discover the fullness of who Jesus is. The more you unwrap, the more you’ll be surprised at how big the gift actually is. If you lack joy, it’s because you haven’t unwrapped Him.

Stop Living for the Approval of Men — Some are exhausting themselves working to impress others and earn human validation. Rest in the truth that God already likes you, loves you, and values you. Let that be enough.

Hold Fast to God’s Promises in Silence — When you have heard from God but now face silence, go back to what is written. Remind yourself of the Scriptures. Trust that His word was true in Genesis and it is true today. Has He said it? He will do it.

Live in the “This Day” — Don’t only worship God for what He did yesterday or what He will do tomorrow. Recognize that this day He is your Savior, this day He is working in your life, this day He is being faithful.

Believe, Act, and Proclaim — Like the shepherds: believe the good news, go and see for yourself, act upon it, and then tell the whole world. Jesus is not just a gift for you — He is a gift for all people. Proclaim it.

And therefore, around 5,000 years the prophets kept saying, “He will come.” In Genesis, He will come. In Exodus, He will come. In Leviticus, He will come. In Numbers, He will come. Throughout the prophets, He will come. In Isaiah, He will come. In Jeremiah, He will come. All the way to Malachi, He will come. And so from Genesis to Malachi, almost 5,000 years. But then there was silence, because from the time of Malachi to the time of Luke — 400 years of silence. No prophets speaking on behalf of God, no public pronouncement. All they had to go on was what He had said.

And so for 5,000 years, “He will come.” Then 400 years of silence — but that didn’t change anything. He was still going to come. And then all of a sudden, “He will come” changes. On that night the angel appears, and He who was coming has come. The word is fulfilled. The prophecy has come to pass. What God said He would do, God has done. Over 400 prophecies fulfilled to the letter. At the fullness of time, God brings to pass the word that He has spoken.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Angels On Call — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview & Key Scriptures

Central Theme — Angels are real spiritual beings created by God, assigned to serve believers who are heirs of salvation. They are present with us right now and are far more active than most Christians realize.

Hebrews 1:14 (NKJV) — “Are they not all ministering spirits sent forth to minister for those who will inherit salvation?” — The foundational verse: angels are sent by God specifically to serve every child of God, not just a select few. This is a covenant blessing and your inheritance.

Psalm 103:20 (NKJV) — “Bless the Lord, you His angels, who excel in strength, who do His word, heeding the voice of His word.” — Angels respond to the spoken word of God. They heed the “voice” of His word—not the word on the page or in your mind, but the word declared aloud.

Proverbs 18:21 (NKJV) — “Death and life are in the power of the tongue.” — Your words give life either to God’s will or to the enemy’s schemes. Speak God’s word to empower angelic enforcement.

2 Kings 19:35 — One angel killed 185,000 Assyrian soldiers in a single night when King Hezekiah prayed. Demonstrates the extraordinary power of just one angel acting on God’s command.

Matthew 26:53 — Jesus said He could ask the Father and receive 12 legions (72,000) of angels instantly. Yet He chose not to ask—showing that prayer activates angelic ministry, and without prayer, even available angels remain still.

Daniel 10 — Daniel prayed and stood in faith for 21 days. The angel told him, “The minute you opened your mouth, I was dispatched.” Prayer based on God’s word triggers immediate angelic response, even when the answer is delayed.

Romans 8:37 — “We are more than conquerors through Christ.” Referenced in the context of declarations believers should make to activate angelic ministry.

Summary 1 of 5

Three Roles of Angels

1. Heaven’s Worshipers — Worship is the most important function of angels. They behold God and worship Him continually. They are keenly aware that they are never to be worshipped themselves. Even when John the Apostle fell before an angel in Revelation, the angel immediately said, “Don’t do that! Worship God!” Angels always point to God and direct all worship to the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

2. Heaven’s Warriors — Angels fight battles in the unseen spiritual realm so that believers cannot be overwhelmed in the seen realm. God mercifully keeps us unaware of most of these battles. Some of these battles concern our future and destiny. One angel alone killed 185,000 soldiers (2 Kings 19:35)—demonstrating their immense power. Bishop Johnson declared: “I believe I got at least two, so don’t mess with my angels!”

3. Protective Services — Angels provide daily protection and physical ministry. They strengthened Jesus after His 40-day fast and in the Garden of Gethsemane. An angel fed Elijah, and he traveled 40 days on the strength of that food. Angels minister to us physically when we are weary, tired, and in need. “You think the President is the only one with secret service? You don’t know whose children you are!”

Summary 2 of 5

How Angels Operate: Spiritual Laws

Principle: Angels Operate Legally — Angels do not act independently, emotionally, or based on need alone. They are servants under authority, governed by spiritual laws. They wait for legal authority before acting—like a well-trained army that will not move until the command is given.

Principle: The Spoken Word Creates Legal Authority — Angels heed the “voice” of God’s word (Psalm 103:20). Not the word on the page, not the word in your mind—the word must be spoken aloud. When you voice God’s word, you create the legal environment that gives angels authority to act on your behalf.

Principle: Prayer Activates Angelic Ministry — Jesus could have called 72,000 angels but chose not to ask. The reason those angels did not move was simple: Jesus did not ask. No prayer means no angelic ministry—not because it is unavailable, but because the legal conditions have not been met. Daniel prayed and an angel was dispatched the moment he opened his mouth.

Principle: Inheritance Must Be Received — Not every believer experiences the same level of angelic activity. It is not that some are more righteous or more holy—those who experience more angelic ministry are more aligned with the spiritual laws that angels obey. The inheritance is available to all, but it must be actively received through faith, prayer, and declaration.

Summary 3 of 5

Warnings & The Power of Words

Warning: Negative Words Create Legal Ground for Demons — When you speak words not aligned with God’s word, angels cannot enforce them. Worse, your negative words can create a legal environment for demons to operate. If you say “I’m always sick,” the angels have no legal authority to act—but demons will gladly enforce that declaration.

Warning: Do Not Give Life to Fear — Satan puts thoughts in your mind, then waits for you to give them life with your words. Death and life are in the power of the tongue. When you wake from a bad dream and declare, “The devil is after me,” you are giving life to that curse and creating legal ground for the enemy.

Warning: Silence Keeps Angels Still — If you keep your mouth silent about God’s word, angels will be still. Many believers have an “angelic balance” in their account that goes unused because they are not giving voice to God’s promises. No voice means no withdrawal of angelic ministry.

Warning: Do Not Confuse Your Angels — Speaking contradictory things—declaring God’s word one moment and then speaking fear, doubt, and negativity the next—confuses the legal environment. Speak consistently and in alignment with God’s word.

Summary 4 of 5

Practical Takeaways & Prophetic Encouragement

Declare God’s Word Daily — Take promises from Scripture and speak them aloud every day. Declare: “The Lord is my refuge and my fortress.” “No weapon formed against me will prosper.” “My God will supply all my needs according to His riches in glory.” Each declaration gives angels legal authority to enforce God’s will.

Pray Believing Prayers Based on the Word — The combination of prayer and God’s spoken word creates the conditions for maximum angelic activity. No prayer equals no angelic ministry. Pray consistently and back up your prayers with Scripture declarations.

Resist Negative Speech — Do not give life to fears, anxieties, and negative thoughts with your words. When Satan puts a thought in your mind, refuse to voice it. Instead, open your mouth and declare God’s word over the situation.

Acknowledge and Thank God for Angels — Become conscious of the angelic presence in your life. Thank God for the ministry of angels. Recognizing and acknowledging their activity increases your awareness of and alignment with their ministry.

Don’t Believe the Lie — Do not believe you are alone, under-resourced, or without help. That lie keeps your angels at attention but unable to act. Fill your heart, mind, and mouth with the reality that angels are present and actively working on your behalf.

Remember: Jesus Is Greater — As great as angels are, there is One far greater. Your greatest gift is Jesus Christ. Learn from the angels to bow before Him. Angels always point to Jesus—so should we.

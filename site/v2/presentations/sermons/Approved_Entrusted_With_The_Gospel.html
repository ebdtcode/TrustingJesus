Approved by God to Be Entrusted with the Gospel

Bishop Darlingston Johnson

1 Thessalonians 2:4-9 • 1 Corinthians 4:2 • Matthew 9:37-38

Approved, Then Entrusted

"But as we have been approved by God to be entrusted with the gospel, even so we speak, not as pleasing men, but God who tests our hearts."

1 Thessalonians 2:4 (NKJV)

Notice the order: approved first, then entrusted.

The Question That Will Matter

At the end of your life, the question will not be: Did you build a comfortable life? Did you accumulate enough wealth? Did you retire well?

The Real Question

Did you faithfully advance the gospel? Were you a steward of the gospel during your lifetime?

Many Children, Few Laborers

"The harvest truly is plentiful, but the laborers are few."

Matthew 9:37 (NKJV)

  • Jesus did not say the harvest is small — He said the laborers are few
  • God has many children, but very few He can rely on or depend on
  • Too many willing to be consumers but unwilling to be contributors

The Consuming Child

You bathed him, clothed him, fed him, paid all his bills. But today, if something important needed to be done, he would be the last person you would entrust it to.

The Tragedy in the Kingdom

You love them, but you can't depend on them. That is the tragedy — God has many children who delight in His grace but few who desire to contribute to the responsibilities of the kingdom.

Key Truth

Gift and Responsibility

The Gospel Is a Gift to Celebrate

Salvation is totally, completely by grace. Thank God!

The Gospel Is a Responsibility to Carry

Stewardship is determined by your faithfulness (1 Corinthians 4:2)

Grace and Faithfulness

"Moreover it is required in stewards that one be found faithful."

1 Corinthians 4:2 (NKJV)

  • Salvation is by grace — faithfulness is not required for salvation
  • Stewardship is by faithfulness — faithfulness is required for responsibility and reward
  • God didn't just save you to make it to heaven — He saved you for something far greater
The Heart Test

God Looks at the Heart

"For the Lord does not see as man sees; for man looks at the outward appearance, but the Lord looks at the heart."

1 Samuel 16:7 (NKJV)

The approval is based on whether or not you pass the heart test. What is God looking for? A heart that is faithful.

Three Marks of a Faithful Heart

1. Right Motive

Desiring God's approval far more than man's applause. Not flattering words, not seeking glory from men.

2. Total Sacrifice

Willing to give not just your best but your all. Paul said: "I didn't just give you the gospel — I gave you my life."

3. Hard Work

Willing to work hard and work long. Paul spelled faithfulness W-O-R-K. God is looking for laborers.

Our Response

Three Words to Remember

What must we do with the gospel?

1. Prioritize the Message

  • Getting this gospel to the world is the greatest responsibility we have
  • Your time and how you use it reveals the priorities of your life
  • You have 168 hours every week — can you find a few to disciple someone?
  • If we make no time to serve, to disciple, to advance the gospel — we are not prioritizing it

2. Personalize the Mission

  • Stop saying "they" should do this — start saying "I" will do this
  • When it comes to rights, you say "we." When it comes to responsibility, you say "they."
  • At the end of your life, you will give account for your stewardship
  • Ask yourself: Does my use of time, talent, and treasure reflect gospel priority?

3. Participate Fully

"If you have not been faithful in the unrighteous mammon, who will commit to your trust the true riches?"

Luke 16:11 (NKJV)

  • Pay the price with your time, your gifts, and your treasure
  • Money is not the mission, but money is the fuel for the mission
  • When only 20% serve and give, we row. When all participate, we fly.

Stewardship Is a Decision

Faithfulness is not seasonal. People don't go to hell in seasons. Stewardship cannot depend on your emotions.

  • I decide to follow Jesus
  • I decide to be faithful, to serve, to participate fully
  • I decide to seek the approval of God, not the applause of men
  • I decide to be generous in my giving and faithful in my stewardship
Response

Prayer Points

From the sermon on Gospel Stewardship

Prayer: A Heart God Can Trust

Father, You test the heart before You entrust responsibility. Search our hearts and expose every area where we have been unfaithful. Remove the desire for man's applause and replace it with a hunger for Your approval. Give us hearts You can trust — hearts that are faithful, dependable, and motivated by love for You alone.

1 Thessalonians 2:4; 1 Samuel 16:7; Psalm 139:23-24

Prayer: From Consumer to Contributor

Lord, forgive us for being consumers of Your grace without being contributors to Your kingdom. We have celebrated the gift but neglected the responsibility. Transform us from spectators to laborers, from takers to givers. Make us sons and daughters You can depend on — not just children who eat at Your table but workers in Your harvest field.

Matthew 9:37-38; 1 Corinthians 4:2; Luke 16:11-12

Prayer: Prioritize, Personalize, Participate

Father, help us to prioritize the gospel above comfort and convenience. Help us to personalize the mission — to stop saying "they" and start saying "I." Help us to participate fully with our time, our talents, and our treasure. Where we have withheld, convict us. Where we have been lazy, stir us. Let this church fly, not row, for the sake of the gospel.

1 Thessalonians 2:8-9; Romans 12:1; 2 Timothy 4:5

Prayer: Faithful Stewardship

Lord, we make a decision today — not a seasonal emotion but a permanent commitment. We decide to follow Jesus. We decide to be faithful stewards. We decide to work hard and work long for the sake of the gospel. At the end of our lives, may we answer this question with confidence: "I faithfully advanced the gospel You entrusted to me."

1 Corinthians 4:2; 2 Timothy 4:7; Matthew 25:21

Approved by God

I have decided to follow Jesus — no turning back

Choosing Obedience

Obedience Over Convenience

Bishop Darlingston Johnson • Palm Sunday

Matthew 21:12–17

The Temple Cleansed

"My house shall be called a house of prayer, but you have made it a den of thieves."

Matthew 21:13 (NKJV)

Palm Sunday Pattern

Sunday: Hosanna! → Friday: Crucify Him! — The crowd shows up for blessing but disappears when the call is to sacrifice.

The Crowd Then and Now

  • The crowd expected a political deliverer — physical prosperity and freedom
  • When Jesus' agenda didn't match theirs, they disappeared
  • They showed up for getting but vanished when called to give, serve, and sacrifice
  • When Jesus hung on the cross — none of them was present

Called to Conquest, Not Comfort

The Army Analogy

Some enlist in the kingdom for the benefits — the uniform, the respect — but when deployment comes, they say: "I didn't sign up for war."

God's Call

God hasn't called us to comfort — He's called us to conquest. That requires worship, work, and war.

The Example

Setting Your Face Toward Jerusalem

Jesus chose obedience over convenience

A Deliberate Choice

  • Jesus didn't drift into Jerusalem — it was not an accident of history
  • He knew what awaited him: betrayal, arrest, beating, mockery, crucifixion
  • He set his face — fixed his mind, gathered courage, steeled himself
  • With eyes wide open, he chose to go

"What does it profit a man to gain the whole world and forfeit his soul?"

Matthew 16:26 (NKJV)

Why?

Why Jesus Still Went

Three reasons for the journey to Jerusalem

1. Love for the Father

  • His love for the Father superseded his desire to avoid pain
  • There are some things worth living for — and some things worth dying for
  • His desire to be obedient overrode his natural instinct for self-preservation

"I appeal to you therefore, brothers, by the mercies of God, to present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to God."

Romans 12:1 (ESV)

2. Love for You

  • Having loved his own, he loved them to the end
  • His love for you is so extreme that no suffering could overcome it
  • If it were necessary today, his love is still strong enough — he would do it again

"Who shall separate us from the love of Christ? Shall tribulation, or distress, or persecution, or famine, or nakedness, or danger, or sword?"

Romans 8:35 (ESV)

The Enemy's Strategy: Doubt

The Lie

"Did God really say...?" The enemy's number one strategy is to make you doubt God's love — just as he did with Adam and Eve.

The Truth

When you start believing the lie, you walk in disobedience. Don't let the enemy cause you to question God's love.

3. The Place of Obedience — and Resurrection

  • Jerusalem was the place of total obedience — this was God's will
  • It was the place of purpose — what he was born for
  • Not to go would have been a denial of who he was and what he was called to do
  • But it was also the place of his resurrection and ascension

There Is a Third Day

"If we suffer, we shall also reign with him."

2 Timothy 2:12 (KJV)

Prophetic Word

If you are in your Jerusalem because you chose obedience over convenience — that place of suffering shall become the place of your resurrection and your ascension. When you embrace the cross of purpose, you receive the crown of authority.

Warning

Jerusalem, Not Rome

Don't let the world's voices redirect you

Stay on the Narrow Road

  • As Jesus rode in, the crowd's celebration could have changed his mind
  • The voices, the sights, the sensations — "Why not Rome instead of Calvary?"
  • People start well on God's path, then listen too much to the world and take an exit

"There is a way that seems right to a man, but its end is the way of death."

Proverbs 14:12 (NKJV)

Daily Life

Worship and Work Every Day

Sunday worship must produce Monday obedience

Personal and Corporate Worship

  • Jesus went to the temple on Sunday to look, then returned Monday to act
  • His personal prayer life was powerful — but incomplete without corporate gathering
  • If Jesus needed the temple, you are not the exception
  • We don't just worship God one day — worship and work every day of the week
The Challenge

Cleanse the Temple

Your body is the temple of God

You Are the Temple

"Do you not know that your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit within you, whom you have from God? You are not your own, for you were bought with a price."

1 Corinthians 6:19–20 (ESV)

  • The temple was designed for communion — but had been repurposed for commerce
  • Not sinful things, but necessary things that displaced the sacred
  • How have you repurposed your body, your time, your talents?

Overturn the Tables

"The kingdom of heaven suffers violence, and the violent take it by force."

Matthew 11:12 (NKJV)

  • Between Palm Sunday and Easter — cleanse the temple
  • Be violent with sin, unforgiveness, bitterness
  • Don't pet it — throw it out!
  • After cleansing, the blind and lame came — and Jesus healed them

Prayer Points

  • Against Crowd Christianity — Lord, deliver me from showing up only for blessings. Make my Hosanna on Sunday translate into faithful obedience every day of the week.
  • For Holy Resolve — Help me fix my face toward Jerusalem. May my desire for obedience always supersede my desire for comfort and convenience.
  • Against the Lie of Doubt — I reject the enemy's whisper that you don't love me. Your love took you to the cross — nothing can separate me from it. (Romans 8:35)

Prayer Points

  • For Those in Their Jerusalem — Lord, for everyone suffering because they chose obedience: there is a third day. Their place of pain shall become the place of resurrection and ascension. (2 Timothy 2:12)
  • Against Worldly Deviation — Guard my mind from voices that call me off your path. Keep me on the narrow road that leads to life. (Proverbs 14:12)
  • Temple Cleansing — Holy Spirit, come into my temple this week. Overturn what needs overturning. Drive out what has displaced you. Make me a house of prayer, fully consecrated for your glory. (1 Corinthians 6:19–20)

Declaration

We will not be the crowd that shouts Hosanna on Sunday and disappears by Friday.

We fix our faces toward Jerusalem.

We choose obedience over convenience.

We embrace the cross knowing the crown follows.

We will cleanse the temple and worship every day.

In our Jerusalem, we will experience resurrection.

To the glory of God the Father, through Jesus Christ His Son, by the power of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

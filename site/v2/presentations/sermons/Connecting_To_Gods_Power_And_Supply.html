Connecting To God's Power And Supply

Part 2: The Power Behind the Promise

Bishop Darlingston Johnson • Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney

Philippians 4:19 • April 27, 2026

Last Week: God's Assurance Policy

"And my God shall supply all your need according to his riches in glory by Christ Jesus."

Philippians 4:19 (KJV)

Not just a promise — a divine decree.

The Language of Decree

  • "Shall" — the strongest way to say you will do something
  • When a king establishes a law, he uses "shall"
  • "Thou shalt..." — God established the Ten Commandments this way
  • Philippians 4:19 carries that same force — a law God enforces

What This Decree Covers

Eternal

Covers earth and heaven — we are eternal beings

Geographic

America, Africa, Asia, Europe — wherever you live

Covenantal

For those who are in Christ — not a universal promise

By Christ Jesus

Jesus is the source, channel, and qualifier of supply

A Constant in a Changing World

Don't Let Circumstances Determine Your Outlook

The economy goes up and down. Jobs come and go. But this decree is a constant. Your outlook should be based on the law, not the present condition.

"Job or no job — my God shall supply all my need."

Why It Matters to God

Reason 1 of 2

"If ye then, being evil, know how to give good gifts unto your children, how much more shall your Father which is in heaven give good things to them that ask him?"

Matthew 7:11 (KJV)

God is a Father. It is His pleasure, His joy, to supply your needs.

Why It Matters to God

Reason 2 of 2

"But seek ye first the kingdom of God, and his righteousness; and all these things shall be added unto you."

Matthew 6:33 (KJV)

Freed for Kingdom Work

God meets your needs so you don't have to worry about them — so you can participate in what He is doing on the earth.

Today's Question

"What is my responsibility?"

Is there anything we must do to experience this promise in its fullness?

The Big Idea

"Connect to the power
behind the promise."

The promise is dependent on power. The power needs a connection.

Illustration 1

The Power Company

The power is delivered. You decide whether to plug in.

Wired for Power

  • The power company has wired your house
  • Power is already in the wires — in the wall
  • But the company didn't reserve the right to turn on your TV
  • That authority rests in your hands
  • No connection — no operation. Even with full power available.

"It Was the Cable Box"

The Verizon Story

I tried one remote. Then another. Pushing buttons. Nothing. I called Verizon to complain. The lady asked, "Sir, could you check if the cable box is plugged in?"

It was Pastor Chris — she had unplugged it. The problem wasn't Verizon. The problem wasn't the TV. It was a connection problem.

"No connection. No operation."

The Power Is Already In You

"Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us..."

Ephesians 3:20 (KJV)

God has delivered the power that raised Christ from the dead to your address. The power is not in heaven — it is in you.

The Problem Is Never God

Not the Power Company

God is perfect in all His ways

Not the Power

The power is delivered — it lives in every believer

It's a Connection Problem

"From time to time — check the connection."

Don't Assume You're Connected

  • Anger that hardens into bitterness
  • Habitual doubt and complaint
  • Walking in our own strength — "I don't need God"
  • Click — nothing. The connection broke and we didn't know.

Like a weak cell signal — nothing's wrong with the phone. Move to where the connection is strong.

Part 2

How Do I Connect?

The Bible doesn't say "connection." It says "agreement."

Agreement Is Connection

"Can two walk together, except they be agreed?"

Amos 3:3 (KJV)

God told Israel: "I am not changing My will, My word, or My ways. You must move and align with Me."

Four Dimensions of Alignment

Will

Not my will, but Yours

Words

Speak what He has said

Ways

Truth, love, generosity, kindness

Work

My work aligned with His kingdom

The Faucet and the Cup

The Faucet Doesn't Move

The faucet is flowing. You hold the cup. If you want water, the faucet is not moving to the cup — the cup must move to the faucet.

God will not align with you. You must align with Him.

The Lord's Prayer Pattern

"Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name. Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done... Give us this day our daily bread."

Luke 11:2-3 (KJV)

"Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done" is not a request. It is a declaration of surrender — and it must come before the petition.

Surrender Precedes Supply

First — Declaration of Surrender

"Your kingdom come. Your will be done." (alignment)

Then — Petition for Provision

"Give us this day our daily bread." (request)

"Before you request power, you must first surrender to power."

Christ's Pattern in Gethsemane

"O my Father, if it be possible, let this cup pass from me: nevertheless not as I will, but as thou wilt."

Matthew 26:39 (KJV)

The reason Jesus resurrected on Sunday was because on Friday, He said "Not My will." Surrender unlocked resurrection power.

Surrender — Then Authority

"Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you."

James 4:7 (KJV)

The Sequence Is Fixed

Submit first. Then resist. Once you have surrendered to God's will, you can demand that lack, sickness, and contradicting circumstances submit to that same will.

High Priest of Our Confession

"Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our profession, Christ Jesus."

Hebrews 3:1 (KJV)

Confession (homologia) means "to say the same thing" — to agree. Jesus is the High Priest of our agreement with the Father.

Advocate of Your Agreement

When You Speak God's Word

Jesus advocates, applies His blood, joins your prayers

When You Speak Doubt or Fear

You "tie His hands" — pull the plug on His ministry

"He cannot advocate for disagreement."

Believe and Speak

"We having the same spirit of faith, according as it is written, I believed, and therefore have I spoken; we also believe, and therefore speak..."

2 Corinthians 4:13 (KJV)

"For all the promises of God in him are yea, and in him Amen, unto the glory of God by us."

2 Corinthians 1:20 (KJV)

"Amen" = "I agree." Every "Amen" activates the High Priest.

The Primary Purpose of Prayer

"To overcome our resistance,
not to overcome His."

Prayer is for alignment, not persuasion. When we are aligned, requests are answered quickly.

Hold Fast to Your Confession

"Let us hold fast the profession of our faith without wavering; for he is faithful that promised."

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Connecting To God's Power And Supply — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview

Building on Part 1, which established Philippians 4:19 as a divine decree of supply, Bishop Johnson turns to the believer's responsibility: connecting to the power behind the promise.

The sermon's central thesis: the promise is dependent on power, and that power has already been delivered to every believer — the same power that raised Christ from the dead. The believer's responsibility is not to acquire power but to connect to the power already within. Connection happens through one word: agreement.

Where promises are not manifesting, the problem is never God. It is always a connection problem.

Recap — Philippians 4:19 is a decree, not just a promise. Eternal, geographic, covenantal, and "by Christ Jesus."

Why God wants needs supplied (1) — He is a Father. A natural, fatherly delight to give good gifts (Matthew 7:11).

Why God wants needs supplied (2) — To free us for kingdom work. "So you can participate in what He is doing on the earth."

My responsibility = connect to the power behind the promise. The promise is dependent on power; that power needs a connection.

Power Company illustration. Power is in the wires; you decide whether to plug in. The Verizon/cable box anecdote: "The problem was simply a connection problem."

The power is already inside you (Eph. 3:20). Delivered to your address at salvation. Not in heaven — in you.

Check the connection. Don't assume. Bitterness, anger, doubt — "click, nothing." Cell-signal illustration: nothing's wrong with the phone; move to where the signal is strong.

Summary 3 of 7

Main Teachings (Part 2 of 2)

How to connect — AGREEMENT (Amos 3:3). Alignment of will, words, ways, work with God's.

Faucet & cup illustration. The faucet doesn't move; the cup must move to it. God will not align with you — you must align with Him.

Lord's Prayer pattern. "Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done" (declaration of surrender) precedes "Give us this day" (petition). Verse 2 is imperative mood — a demand of alignment, not a request.

Surrender precedes supply. Christ's pattern in Gethsemane: Friday's "Not My will" enabled Sunday's resurrection.

Declare surrender — then demand surrender. Once aligned with God's will, you can demand circumstances submit to that same will. "Lack, leave my life."

James 4:7 sequence. Submit first. Then resist the devil — and he will flee.

Jesus is High Priest of our CONFESSION (Heb. 3:1). "Confession" = "to say the same thing." His advocacy is activated by our agreement with the Father's words.

Primary purpose of prayer. "To overcome our resistance, not to overcome His." Prayer is for alignment, not persuasion.

Summary 4 of 7

Biblical Principles

The Decree Principle — Philippians 4:19 is law, not hope. God enforces it.

The Delivery Principle — The power has already been delivered to your address. There is no shortage.

The Connection Principle — "No connection, no operation." Availability does not equal experience.

The Agreement Principle — Connection happens through agreement: will, words, ways, work.

The Movement Principle — God will not move; you must. You are the cup.

Surrender Precedes Supply — Declaration of surrender must come before petition for provision.

Surrender Empowers Authority — Submission to God is what makes resistance of the enemy effective.

The Confession Principle — Confession means agreement. To agree verbally with God's Word activates Christ's high priestly ministry.

The High Priest Principle — Jesus is High Priest of agreement, not of doubt. His advocacy is activated by our alignment.

Prayer's True Purpose — To overcome our resistance, not God's. Alignment, not persuasion.

Kingdom Freedom Principle — God supplies needs so we are free from anxiety to advance His kingdom.

Summary 5 of 7

Warnings & Practical Takeaways

Warnings

• Do not blame God when promises seem unfulfilled — the problem is never God.

• Do not let circumstances determine your outlook — the decree is constant.

• Do not speak your own mind out of alignment with God's Word. "Some of you should not speak your mind, because your mind is crazy."

• Do not allow anger to harden into bitterness — a silent connection-breaker.

• Do not attempt to resist the enemy without first submitting to God.

• Beware of "self-reliance Christianity" — "I don't need God."

• Do not skip surrender and go straight to petition in prayer.

Practical Takeaways

• Periodically check the connection. Don't assume.

• Align will, words, ways, and work with God's.

• Speak the Word; say "Amen" to His promises — activate the High Priest.

• Declare surrender before making petition in prayer.

• Once surrendered, exercise authority — demand circumstances align.

• Hold fast to your confession when circumstances fluctuate.

• Move toward God; don't wait for God to move toward you.

Prayer Points

Moving from Fake to Real Worship

Prayer for Genuine Hunger

Father God, I ask for a genuine hunger for Jesus that goes beyond religious formality. Like the wise men who left Persia and traveled far to meet the King, give me a heart that is not satisfied with secondhand information about Jesus. I don't want to be a casual seeker; I want to be a serious seeker hungry for truth. Holy Spirit, stir up within me an insatiable appetite for the presence of Jesus. Every encounter with Jesus should make me hunger for more.

Matthew 2:2; John 14:6

Prayer for Focused Pursuit

Lord Jesus, in a world with countless distractions—ambitions, goals, dreams, relationships, money, status—help me to identify Your star and focus exclusively on You. The wise men saw many stars in the heavens, but they ignored all the others when they identified His star. Father, I confess that I have allowed many things to absorb my attention. Today I choose to fix my eyes on Jesus. When I fix my eyes on You, it brings peace, hope, power, and true fulfillment.

Hebrews 12:1-2; Matthew 6:21

Prayer for Complete Surrender

Heavenly Father, the defining mark of true worship is surrender. When the wise men found Jesus, they bowed down. I present my body as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to You. I confess that there are areas where I have been troubled by Your kingship because I know it demands that I surrender control. Today, I choose to abdicate the throne of my life. I surrender my time, treasure, gifts, finances, relationships, career—every single area. You are King; I'm not. You are Lord; I'm not.

Romans 12:1; Matthew 2:11

Prayer for Transformation, Not Just Information

Father God, I confess that like Herod, there have been times when I inquired and wanted information about You, but never truly intended for that information to transform me. Today I repent of this fake worship. Transform me by the renewing of my mind. Real worshipers bow and surrender, inviting You to have Your way. I don't want to remain the same. I want to change more and more into the image of Christ. Let the information concerning Jesus produce transformation in my life.

Romans 12:2; James 1:22

Prayer to Stop Managing God

Heavenly Father, worship without surrender is simply an attempt to manage You. I confess that I have been guilty of trying to manage You instead of worship You. I have said, "God, bless my plans, but don't change them. God, heal me, but don't touch this part." I have made You a consultant instead of recognizing You as King. I cannot worship the person I'm trying to manage. Today I stop negotiating with You because You do not negotiate Your kingship. I bow and surrender to You. I step off the throne because You are King.

John 4:23-24; Matthew 2:11

Prayer for Personal Conviction

Lord Jesus, I don't want to be like those who worship only because others are doing it—those who are part of the crowd but have no personal relationship or personal convictions. I want my worship to flow from deep personal conviction and intimate relationship with You, not from what's popular or who's around me. Whether anyone else worships or not, whether friends stay or leave, let me worship You because I know You personally and am motivated from within by the Holy Spirit.

John 4:23-24

Prayer for Obedience That Demonstrates Love

Father, Jesus declared, "These people honor Me with their lips, but their heart is far from Me." I don't want to be guilty of honoring You with words while my heart remains far from You. Jesus said, "If you love Me, keep My commandments." True worship will be demonstrated in my works of obedience. I will show my faith by my works. Let my worship be real, flowing from my heart and evidenced in my obedience, my love for others, and my works of righteousness.

John 14:15; Matthew 15:8; James 2:18

Prayer for Honoring God with Our Treasures

Lord, the wise men didn't come to Jesus empty-handed. They brought their gifts and worshiped Him with their treasures—gold, frankincense, and myrrh. Jesus, You said, "Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also." Our whole life is to be a gift to You because You gave Your whole life for us. Father, I present my body as a living sacrifice. I honor You not just with words but with the gift of my surrendered heart, my time, my talents, and my treasures.

Matthew 2:11; Matthew 6:21; Romans 12:1

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Fake Worship — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview & Key Scriptures

Central Text — Matthew 2:1–12 (NKJV). The story of the wise men and King Herod responding to the birth of Jesus provides a lens for examining genuine versus counterfeit worship.

Big Idea — “The revelation of Jesus as King always demands a response, and that response will determine whether we are fake or real in our worship.”

Supporting Scriptures — John 14:6 (The Way, Truth, Life) • Isaiah 6 (Angels crying “Holy, holy, holy”) • Hebrews 12:1–2 (Eyes fixed on Jesus) • Romans 12:1–2 (Living sacrifice, transformation) • Matthew 6:21 (Where your treasure is) • John 4:23–24 (Worship in spirit and truth) • Matthew 15:8 (Honor with lips, heart far away) • John 14:15 (If you love Me, keep My commandments) • James 2:18 (Faith shown by works)

Two Responses, Same Message — The wise men and Herod both received the same revelation about the same King. One response was joyful, leading to surrender, giving, and transformation. The other was fearful, leading to resistance, pretence, and destruction. Both were called “worship.”

Summary 1 of 5

Marks of Real Worship (The Wise Men)

1. Genuine Hunger — Real worship begins with a serious, personal hunger to know, meet, and experience the King—not casual religion or secondhand information. The wise men asked, “Where is He who has been born King?”

2. Focused Pursuit — Among countless stars, the wise men identified His star and fixed their gaze exclusively on it. Real worshipers ignore competing distractions—ambitions, status, possessions—and keep their eyes on Jesus. “When you fix your eyes on Jesus it brings peace, hope, power, and true fulfillment.”

3. Costly Sacrifice — The journey from Persia was long, expensive, and inconvenient. True worshipers are willing to pay any price, reorder their entire lives, and set no limits on pursuit of the King.

4. Honoring with Treasures — They brought gold, frankincense, and myrrh—their substance. “Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.” Our whole life is to be a gift to Jesus because He gave His whole life for us.

5. Complete Surrender — They fell down and worshiped Him. “Anything short of surrender comes short of worship.” The defining mark of real worship is bowing down—surrendering self, substance, and control to the King.

Summary 2 of 5

Five Signs of Fake Worship (Herod)

1. Troubled by Jesus’s Kingship — Fake worshipers love Jesus as Savior, Healer, and Forgiver, but struggle when He is declared King and Lord. Kingship demands they abdicate the throne of their own lives, surrender control, and can no longer be “the decider in chief.”

2. Information Without Transformation — Herod inquired about Christ but never intended for that knowledge to change him. Desiring information without willingness to be transformed is a clear indicator of fake worship. “If you want to remain the same and you simply want information, you are not a real worshiper.”

3. Delegating the Hard Work — Herod sent the wise men to do the seeking while he waited for a report. Fake worshipers want worship to be easy—they delegate prayer, fasting, Bible study, and personal seeking to others.

4. Worshiping Only with the Crowd — “I will come and worship Him too.” Many worship only because others do. They lack personal relationship, personal convictions, and inward motivation. When friends leave, they leave.

5. Simply Pretending — Herod had no intention of worshiping. Many in churches pretend to worship Jesus while actually worshiping fame, popularity, power, possessions, or themselves. “These people honor Me with their lips, but their hearts are far from Me.”

Summary 3 of 5

Principles & Warnings

Worship Without Surrender Is Management — “Worship without surrender is simply an attempt to manage God.” We sing, pray, and attend church while keeping control. We want God close enough to bless us but not close enough to confront or change us. We reduce God from King to consultant.

You Cannot Worship What You Manage — “You cannot worship the person you’re trying to manage. You can either bow and surrender to God or you can try to negotiate with Him, but God does not negotiate His kingship.”

Don’t Separate Salvation from Submission — Don’t desire forgiveness without authority, heaven without obedience, or grace without lordship. True worship requires willingness for God’s Word to produce transformation.

True Worship Is Demonstrated in Works — “Show me your faith by your works” (James 2:18). If you love Jesus, keep His commandments. True worship flows from the heart and is evidenced in obedience, loving one another, and works of righteousness.

He Is Trustworthy — Jesus is both a great King who deserves surrender and a humble servant King who rules with grace and mercy. “He loves you more than you love yourself.” You can surrender because He is trustworthy.

Summary 4 of 5

Practical Takeaways & The Three-Part Invitation

The Invitation

1. Stop trying to manage God — that is fake worship.

2. Step off the throne — He is King — that is real worship.

3. Lay down control at His feet and trust Him — that is real worship.

Self-Examination Questions

• Is my heart genuinely hungry for an encounter with Christ, or am I satisfied with secondhand information?

• What “star” am I following? Is Jesus truly the focus of my life?

• Am I willing to pay the price of pursuit—time, effort, sacrifice?

• What areas of my life am I still refusing to surrender to His kingship?

• Am I seeking information about God without allowing it to transform me?

• Do I delegate the hard parts of worship to others?

• Is my worship motivated from within, or do I only worship with the crowd?

The Measure of Worship — “Worship is not measured by how loudly we sing but by how deeply we surrender.”

When we worship Him in spirit and in truth, when we’re real worshipers like the wise men, we bow, we surrender. And in bowing and surrendering, we’re inviting Him to have His way with us, which means any information we receive or desire is used to produce change and transformation in our lives. If you want to remain the same and you simply want information, you are not a real worshiper.

True worship requires a willingness for the information that we are receiving to produce transformation in our lives. So if you come to church on a weekly basis and you get information, but when that information requires transformation you’re not willing to allow that transformation to take place—because you want to stay the same, you love where you are, you love what you’re doing—that is not real worship.

Here’s another thing about Herod, again revealing fake worship. What did Herod do when he heard that a child had been born? The Bible says he sent the wise men to go find Him in Bethlehem, and then come and tell him, and then he said, “I will go and worship Him too.” So “you all go do the hard work, you spend the time, you invest the energy, you make the effort, you do all of that to seek Him, and then when you have found Him, come tell me, and then I will go and worship.”

In other words, they don’t want to experience the hard parts of worship. They only worship when they are able to do so without much effort, without much time. Make it quick, make it sweet, let’s do it, let’s enjoy. For them, worship is just enjoying—come on, let’s sing, let’s enjoy ourselves. But when it’s time to seek Him, when it’s time to set aside time to pray, to read, to fast, to wait upon the Lord—things that the flesh does not like—they delegate it to other people.

“These people honor Me with their lips, but their hearts are far from Me.” Jesus said, “If you say you love Me, keep My commandments.” If you’re not keeping His commandments, you’re only pretending to worship Him—with songs, with music, with dance, but those are just words with no substance. So let’s stop the pretence. If we’re worshiping Jesus, let’s worship Him for real. Worshiping Him for real means we are worshiping from our hearts, with our works. We’re not just going to love Him with words but through our obedience. His commandment is that we love one another.

So here is the end of the matter: worship without surrender is simply an attempt to manage God. Worship without surrender is not worship at all. We sing, we pray, we attend, but we still keep control. We’re still on the throne. We want God close enough to bless us, but not close enough to confront us or change us. We’ve made God a consultant, not the King. You cannot worship the person you’re trying to manage. God does not negotiate His kingship.

So here’s the invitation: One, stop trying to manage God—that’s fake worship. Two, step off the throne—He is King. Three, lay down control at His feet and trust Him. Because at the end of the day, worship is not measured by how loudly we sing, but by how deeply we surrender. In Jesus’s name. Amen.

The Fierce Attack

  • Moabites, Ammonites, and people of Mount Seir attacked Judah
  • Only 30 miles away — imminent danger
  • Despite military might, Jehoshaphat was fearful

Key Insight

Fear, like faith, comes by hearing. What we allow ourselves to hear shapes our spiritual posture.

Facing Fear Head-On

Pastor Babu's testimony from a crusade in India

  • Translating for a Singapore team — threats came on day 2
  • His daughter asked: "Dad, if you die today, will you go to heaven?"
  • His father said: "Son, you go. If something happens, we'll see you in heaven."
  • He forgave everyone, prayed in tongues, obeyed God's instruction to stay
  • That night was the most powerful night — many saved and healed

Part 3

The Prophetic Word

The battle belongs to the Lord

Stand Still and See

"You will not need to fight this battle. Position yourselves, stand still and see the salvation of the Lord, who is with you."

2 Chronicles 20:17 (NKJV)

  • The word "salvation" here is Yahweh — meaning Jesus Himself
  • When you stand still on the Word, you see Jesus as your deliverer

"If God is for us, who can be against us?"

Romans 8:31 (ESV)

Worship Before Victory

  • Jehoshaphat fell on his face — total surrender
  • He sent the worship team before the army — not after victory
  • They sang: "Praise the Lord, for His mercy endures forever"
  • The worshipers went out without weapons — only instruments

The Principle

Victory is already declared in heaven. The worship goes first because the battle is already won.

The Lord Set Ambushes

"And when they began to sing and to praise, the Lord set ambushes against the people of Ammon, Moab, and Mount Seir… and they were defeated."

2 Chronicles 20:22 (NKJV)

The enemies destroyed each other — your problem will be killed by itself

When you praise, God fights for you in the unseen realm

Victory

Enforcing the Victory

Victory is not fought — it is enforced

What To Do With Victory

1. Enjoy It

The victory was won on the cross — receive it with joy

2. Express It

Declare God's faithfulness in your daily life

3. Enforce It

Tell the problem: victory is already here — enforce it by faith

The Valley of Baraka

"So the realm of Jehoshaphat was quiet, for his God gave him rest on every side."

2 Chronicles 20:30 (NKJV)

  • 3 days collecting spoils — abundance beyond measure
  • The valley was renamed Valley of Baraka (Valley of Blessings)
  • You need not work for rest — you work from rest
  • They returned to Jerusalem with joy

Prayer: Against Complacency & For Courage

Prayer Focus

Father, we repent of every alliance formed with what is contrary to Your Spirit. Restore in us a holy alertness. Where fear has entered, let faith arise. We choose to hear Your Word above every other voice. We declare that the Spirit who raised Christ from the dead lives in us, and He is greater than the spirit of fear.

"For God gave us a spirit not of fear but of power and love and self-control."

2 Timothy 1:7 (ESV)

Prayer: Freedom from the Past

Prayer Focus

Heavenly Father, we declare that the blood of Jesus cleanses from all sin. We break the power of condemnation and shame over every believer. Every person paralyzed by past failures or wounds — we declare your past is absolutely washed clean. There is now no condemnation for those in Christ Jesus.

"As far as the east is from the west, so far does He remove our transgressions from us."

Psalm 103:12 (ESV)

Prayer: Worship Over Worry

Prayer Focus

Father, we choose to lead with worship. Let our praise go before us into every situation. As we lift our voices to declare that Your mercy endures forever, we trust You are setting ambushes against every force arrayed against us. The battle belongs to the Lord. We do not need to fight — we enforce what Christ has already won.

"But thanks be to God, who gives us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ."

1 Corinthians 15:57 (ESV)

Prayer: Rest, Joy & Enforced Victory

Prayer Focus

Lord, we receive the truth of Jehoshaphat's story: the spoils are already prepared, the valley of blessings is already named. We declare we do not work for rest — we work from rest, from the finished work of Jesus. Teach us to enjoy the victory, express it, and enforce it. Let the joy of the Lord be our strength today.

"Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."

Matthew 11:28 (ESV)

Give Me Your Isaac

Faith, Obedience, and the Sacrifice That Releases Grace

Pastor Maima Moore • Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney

Genesis 22:1-18 • June 8, 2026

The Test

"Take now your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah, and offer him there as a burnt offering on one of the mountains of which I shall tell you."

Genesis 22:2 (NKJV)

Isaac was the promised son — the most precious possession, the son through whom every promise would come. And God asks for him.

"We Will Go Yonder and Worship"

"Stay here with the donkey; the lad and I will go yonder and worship, and we will come back to you."

Genesis 22:5 (NKJV)

Notice what Abraham calls the hardest act of obedience of his life: worship.

The Altar and the Knife

"Abraham built an altar there… bound Isaac his son, and laid him on the altar… And Abraham stretched out his hand and took the knife to slay his son."

Genesis 22:9-10 (NKJV)

Obedience was not a feeling or a song — it was the knife raised in full surrender.

"Now I Know That You Fear God"

"Do not lay your hand on the lad… for now I know that you fear God, since you have not withheld your son, your only son, from Me."

Genesis 22:12 (NKJV)

The test was never about Isaac's death — it was about Abraham's heart.

Jehovah Jireh — The Lord Will Provide

"Abraham lifted his eyes… and there behind him was a ram caught in a thicket by its horns… And Abraham called the name of the place, The-Lord-Will-Provide."

Genesis 22:13-14 (NKJV)

"God will provide for Himself the lamb." Abraham's obedience met God's provision.

The Oath of Blessing

"By Myself I have sworn… blessing I will bless you, and multiplying I will multiply your descendants… and your descendants shall possess the gate of their enemies. In your seed all the nations of the earth shall be blessed, because you have obeyed My voice."

Genesis 22:16-18 (NKJV)

The blessing was sealed with two words: "you have obeyed."

Movement One

What Is Your Isaac?

Identify the specific area where God is calling you to obey

Isaac Is What God Asked You to Do

More Than "Your Most Precious Thing"

"When the Lord spoke to me, He said: Isaac is simply that which I have asked you to do — the thing our God has commanded you to do. That is your Isaac."

God said "your only son" — not Ishmael, the work of the flesh. The command was specific. So is yours.

Your Isaac Is Specific to You

"My Isaac is specific to me; your Isaac is specific to you." For some it is…

  • Cutting off social media and getting into prayer
  • A call to the mission field — "this place is not your place"
  • A business God told you to start — or a career you chose for money
  • The tithe — "give Me My ten percent; that's Isaac"
  • A book delayed, or a sin God says, "Give it to Me"

Two Services, One Voice

The Holy Ghost Speaking to the House

"We did not confer. Pastor Obi preached my message this morning in the first service. So he was not preaching, I am not preaching — it is the Holy Ghost talking to this church."

On the first Sunday of June 2026, God came to the house with one word: Give Me your Isaac.

Movement Three

Sacrifice Releases Grace

Blessings and breakthroughs follow the surrendered Isaac

Mount Moriah and the Covenant

Moriah was the very mountain where God would later offer His own Son. Why ask Abraham to offer, when God knew the sacrifice would be Jesus?

Covenant Partnership

"For God to do what God needs to do, His covenant partner must do his part. When Abraham obeyed, he released grace for God to send the ultimate sacrifice — that's why he said, 'God Himself will provide a lamb.'"

It Must Cost You Something

"I will not offer burnt offerings to the Lord my God with that which costs me nothing."

2 Samuel 24:24 (NKJV) — David at the threshing floor

And here is the mystery: Araunah's threshing floor stood on Mount Moriah. David understood — sacrifice ushers in and releases grace.

The Sacrifice of Praise

"By Him let us continually offer the sacrifice of praise to God… But do not forget to do good and to share, for with such sacrifices God is well pleased."

Hebrews 13:15-16 (NKJV)

When you give God your Isaac, it becomes a sweet-smelling savor in His nostrils. Disobedience — not the cross — is what hinders the grace.

Grace Released in the Prison

Paul and Silas, in the Philippian jail, did not mumble or grumble — they gave God a sacrifice of praise. And grace broke in: a sudden, massive shaking that rattled the prison doors and set every prisoner free.

Acts 16:25-34 — the offering came first; the breakthrough followed.

The Evidence

Faith That Reverences God

Why the surrendered Isaac proves the fear of the Lord

Abraham's Great Faith

"By faith Abraham… offered up Isaac… concluding that God was able to raise him up, even from the dead."

Hebrews 11:17-19 (NKJV)

By obeying God, we reject fear and trust that He will do what He has promised. The church says, "We believe You."

Evidence That You Fear God

Your Isaac Is Not Lord — God Is

"All the things you were doing before were just talk — but by the fact that you gave Me Isaac, it is evidence that you fear Me, that you reverence Me, that I am Lord in your life."

A nest egg you trust for security; a comfort you will not leave — these can quietly become your Isaac.

"Can This Money Go?"

Two people — neither in Harvest, neither even in this state — texted Pastor Maima within days, sending several thousand dollars each toward their InvestBeyond pledges. "Don't doubt me — it is God who told me to do this."

"Then I heard the Holy Ghost say, 'Can this money go?' — and I knew it was a prophetic illustration for the giving of Isaac, and it was to this house. It had nothing to do with me."

God Has Spoken Three Times

At a solemn assembly, a minister prophesied over a young man: "Learn all you must learn from here, because God will take you to the nations" — a prophetic parable of Isaac released to the nations.

In 2023, God said "Go deeper" three times. Now in June 2026 He has spoken three times again — through Pastor Obi, through Pastor Maima, through the illustration — "Give Me Isaac: your faith and obedience."

The Promised Harvest

"Blessing I will bless you… and your seed shall possess the gate of their enemies… in your seed all the nations of the earth shall be blessed."

Genesis 22:17-18 (NKJV)

Doors opened, nations blessed, laborers sent, resources raised, mighty exploits accomplished — "the ball is in our court."

Response

Prayer Points

Surrendering our Isaac to the Lord

1. Reveal My Isaac

For Honest Discernment

Lord, show me my Isaac. What have You told me to do that I have not done, that I have been excusing or delaying? Search my heart and name the specific area where You are calling me to obey — even if it is difficult, costly, or uncomfortable.

2. Worship Through Obedience

For a Surrendered Heart

Father, teach me that worship is more than a song. I bring myself as a living sacrifice. Let me never claim to worship You while unwilling to obey You. Make my obedience a sweet-smelling savor — a pleasing sacrifice in Your sight.

3. Courage to Lay It Down

For Faith Over Fear

Lord, I will not offer You that which costs me nothing. Give me Abraham's faith to trust that You can provide and even raise the dead. Loosen my grip on every nest egg, comfort, and security I have made into an Isaac, and help me release it in obedience.

4. Families on the Same Page

For Households That Obey Together

God, unite husbands and wives to give You their Isaac together. Remove every hindrance between us, and let our homes become altars of faith and obedience so that grace is released over our children and their children.

5. Harvest's Corporate Isaac

For the Building of His House

Lord, as a house we release our faith and obedience — our Isaac of InvestBeyond — to build Your church. Release abundant grace: send laborers, raise and deploy resources, accomplish mighty exploits, and bless the nations until no one lives or dies without Christ.

Covenant Declaration

We Give God Our Isaac

We, the people of Harvest-Olney, covenant with the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit that on this first Sunday of June 2026, we release our faith and obedience to God by giving Him our Isaac for the building of His church.

We enter a spiritual transaction that releases God's grace for generational blessing. We declare that our children, their children, and all generations of Harvest-Olney until Jesus comes will be blessed because of our faith and obedience.

We declare that InvestBeyond — our Isaac — will thunder in the heavens: that there was a people who sacrificed, who gave their time, talent, and treasure for the work of God; and that our obedience will bless the nations of the earth, as grace is released for laborers to be sent, resources raised, and mighty exploits accomplished, in the name of Jesus.

Discussion & Reflection

From the Harvest small-group guide — Genesis 22:1-18

Church prayer line • 7:30–8:30 PM on Zoom

1. Identify Your Isaac

What is your "Isaac" — the specific area where God is calling you to obey Him, even if it is difficult, costly, or uncomfortable? (Genesis 22:1-2; Hebrews 11:17-19)

Why the command was so difficult — Isaac was the promised son, the very means of every promise; God asked Abraham to surrender not just something precious but the apparent guarantee of his future. Obedience meant trusting that God could provide or even raise the dead (Hebrews 11:17-19).

Personal and specific — God said "your only son" — not Ishmael. "My Isaac is specific to me; your Isaac is specific to you." Isaac is not merely your most precious thing; it is the particular thing God has asked you to do.

Modern-day Isaacs — Cutting off social media for prayer; a call to the mission field; a business God said to start (or a career chosen only for money); the tithe; a delayed book; a besetting sin to surrender; a nest egg trusted for security; a comfort zone you will not leave. The question: "What have You told me to do that I haven't done, that I'm excusing?"

Discussion 1 of 3

2. Obedience Is Worship

"I cannot claim to worship God when I am not willing to obey God." How does obedience demonstrate true worship? (Genesis 22:5; Hebrews 13:15-16)

Songs vs. a life of worship — "We can sing, dance, and rejoice — that is good, but worship is more than a song." Singing engages the lips; living worship brings the self: "Today I've brought myself. I am the sacrifice." True worship is a surrendered heart, not a good set of music.

Why Abraham called it "worship" — Genesis 22:5: "the lad and I will go yonder and worship." He labeled the hardest act of obedience of his life as worship, because obeying God's costly command is the truest worship — reverence, trust, and surrender expressed in action.

Obedience as a pleasing sacrifice — Hebrews 13:15-16 joins the "sacrifice of praise" to "doing good and sharing," for "with such sacrifices God is well pleased." When you give God your Isaac, it becomes a sweet-smelling savor in His nostrils — worship He receives.

Discussion 2 of 3

3. Blessings Released

What blessings or spiritual breakthroughs can be released when we give God our Isaac? (Genesis 22:15-18; 2 Samuel 24:24; Acts 16:25-34)

After Abraham obeyed — God swore by Himself: "Blessing I will bless you… your seed shall possess the gate of their enemies… in your seed all nations shall be blessed, because you have obeyed." Obedience unlocked an oath-bound, generational blessing.

Sacrifice and future generations — In covenant, the partner's obedience "releases grace." David refused to "give the Lord that which costs nothing" (2 Sam 24:24) on the same Mount Moriah; Paul and Silas's sacrifice of praise shook the prison and freed everyone (Acts 16:25-34). Costly obedience opens heaven for those who come after.

Effect on family, church, community, ministry — Our obedience positions children and children's children for blessing, and releases laborers, resources, and "mighty exploits" for the harvest field.

Harvest's corporate Isaac — InvestBeyond — A house giving its Isaac of faith and obedience "thunders in the heavens," releasing grace so that no one lives or dies without Christ. The ball is in our court.

Give Me Your Isaac — Pastor Maima Moore

Key points, principles, scripture, and takeaways

Overview

Anchor text — Genesis 22:1-18, the binding of Isaac, climaxing in Jehovah Jireh and the oath of blessing.

Preacher & setting — Pastor Maima Moore, second service at Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney, first Sunday of June 2026. The same morning, Pastor Obi (Obadiah Swen) preached "Loving Jesus" in the first service with no coordination — taken as the Holy Ghost confirming one word to the house.

Big idea — "Give Me your Isaac." Your Isaac is not only your most precious possession but the specific thing God has commanded you to do. Surrendering it is true worship and releases grace and generational blessing.

Application — Identify your Isaac (individual, family, and corporate), worship through obedience, and give it — including Harvest's corporate Isaac of InvestBeyond.

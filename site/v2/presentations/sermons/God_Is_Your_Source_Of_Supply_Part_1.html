God Is Your Source of Supply

Part 1: The Law of Divine Provision

Bishop Darlingston Johnson • Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney

Philippians 4:19 • April 19, 2026

A World on Edge

  • Middle East disruption — oil, fuel prices, inflation climbing
  • Families worried about jobs, bills, the cost of living
  • Anxiety has become the normal posture of the world
  • But the believer has an option

Two Ways to Respond

"Seek ye first the kingdom of God and his righteousness, and all these things shall be added unto you."

Matthew 6:33 (KJV)

The same circumstances. Two entirely different responses.

Don't Take the Thought

"Take no anxious thought for your life... your heavenly Father knoweth that ye have need of all these things."

Matthew 6:25, 32 (paraphrase)

Anxious Thoughts Will Come

You cannot stop them from coming. But you do not have to take them. Taking is an act of will — refuse it.

The Foundation

"And my God shall supply all your need according to his riches in glory by Christ Jesus."

Philippians 4:19 (KJV)

Six phrases. Each one carries the weight of heaven.

The Big Idea

"When God is your God,
your needs are His responsibility."

This is not a quote. This is a covenant reality.

Part 1

God Is Your Source

Not your job. Not the economy. Not your business.

Source vs. Channel

The Source — God Himself

Unchanging. Inexhaustible. Never absent.

The Channels — Jobs, Businesses, the Economy

They come and go. God has channels you don't even know about.

"Don't mistake the source for the channel."

The Child in the Grocery Store

A Picture of Trust

A five-year-old watches his parents fill the cart with food. Never once does it cross his mind: How will we pay for this? Can we afford it?

  • He's a child — provision is not his responsibility
  • He trusts his parents completely — not himself
  • What if we had that same attitude toward our Father?
Part 2

"My God" — The Covenant

There is a world of difference between "God" and "my God."

The Husband Analogy

  • Everyone can say "Bishop is a husband" — and it gives them no claim on him
  • Only his wife can say "you are my husband" — with legal and moral claim
  • Knowing about God yields nothing — "the devils also believe, and tremble" (James 2:19)
  • But when you say "my God, my Father" — covenant binds Him to respond

David Knew This

"The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want."

Psalm 23:1 (KJV)

The Faithful Provider

God is described in Scripture as faithful — a faithful Father, faithful husband, faithful provider. There is no way He will fail to carry out His covenant obligation.

Part 3

"Shall" — The Decree

Not a promise. A pledge. A law.

The Weight of "Shall"

I may

A possibility. No guarantee.

I will

An intent. A statement of will.

I SHALL

A decree. A binding obligation. An established kingdom law.

When a King Decrees a Law

He says "shall." So does God.

  • "Thou shall not worship any other God"
  • "Thou shall not steal"
  • "Thou shall not commit adultery"
  • "My God shall supply all your need"

Same grammar. Same weight. Same certainty.

The Word Fulfills Itself

"So shall my word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return unto me void, but it shall accomplish that which I please."

Isaiah 55:11 (KJV)

  • "Let there be light" — light had to appear
  • When God says "I shall supply," the word carries its own power to fulfill itself
  • You don't need to ask how. The decree will accomplish it.

"DJ, Your Need Is Met"

A Personal Testimony from College

Walking away from the finance office, unable to register, no money for the new semester — the Spirit of God whispered:

"DJ, whenever you have a need, the need is met."

"From the 70s to now — never forsaken, never begging bread."

As Sure as Sunrise

"I have been young, and now am old; yet have I not seen the righteous forsaken, nor his seed begging bread."

Psalm 37:25 (KJV)

A Fixed Law

The same God who causes the sun to rise every morning has decreed that your needs shall be met — by the same word of power.

Part 4

The Coverage

Who is covered • What is covered

Life Assurance, Not Life Insurance

Life Insurance

Provides for your family after you die

Life Assurance (God's Policy)

Covers you while you live. Activates now, not at death.

Who Is Covered?

  • Not the unbeliever — the policy requires being a child of God
  • Believers walking by faith — actively trusting God as source
  • The Philippians gave out of their own need (Phil 4:15-16) — that generosity was the evidence of their faith
  • Fear in time of need blocks access — faith that gives keeps the policy in force

Part 5

"According to His Riches"

Not out of His riches. According to.

The Key Preposition

Out of His riches

A small portion of what He has — like a poor man stretching fifty dollars

According to His riches

Proportional to His wealth — a billionaire doesn't give you a secondhand Corolla

God supplies according to how much He has — not how much you need.

Three Tiers of His Wealth

1. Earthly Riches

Silver, gold, oil, diamonds — discovered and undiscovered (Psalm 24:1; Psalm 50:10–12)

2. Cosmic Riches

Two trillion galaxies — and everything in them belongs to Him

3. Unsearchable Riches of Christ

Spiritual wealth so vast it cannot be measured (Ephesians 3:8)

The Checkbook Is Always in the Black

  • God never has to balance His account
  • His giving does not diminish His supply — it is continuously replenished
  • Will His check ever bounce? Never.
  • Don't limit yourself to your account — your Father draws from another

Five Loaves. Two Fish. Surplus.

He Was Not Supplying from the Loaves

Jesus was not blessing them according to five loaves and two fish. He was blessing them according to His riches in glory. Every time they came back, there was more. The supply replenished instead of diminishing.

The natural is the channel. The riches of glory are the source.

Forty Years in the Wilderness

  • Over three million people
  • No farms. No grocery stores. No natural infrastructure.
  • Manna every morning. Quail in the evenings. Water from a rock.
  • Clothes and shoes did not wear out

When natural channels are closed, God supplies supernaturally.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

God Is Your Source of Supply — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview

In the face of economic uncertainty — rising inflation, disrupted oil markets, widespread financial anxiety — Bishop Johnson anchors believers in the bedrock promise of Philippians 4:19. He argues this verse is not merely a promise but a divine decree and established kingdom law, binding God Himself to supply the needs of every child who truly calls Him "my God."

The central revelation of the message: when God is your God, provision is not your problem — it is His covenantal responsibility.

This is Part 1 of a multi-part series. Part 2 will teach how to tap in to the supply — beginning with the principle: "When God makes a decree, you must agree."

Summary 1 of 5

Key Scriptures

Philippians 4:19 — "My God shall supply all your need according to his riches in glory by Christ Jesus." Primary text; treated as decree and kingdom law.

Matthew 6:33 — "Seek ye first the kingdom..." Believers have an option different from the world.

Matthew 6:25, 32 — "Take no anxious thought..." Anxious thoughts come, but you do not have to take them.

Psalm 23:1 — "The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want." David's model of covenant confidence.

Psalm 24:1; Psalm 50:10–12 — The earth and its fullness are God's. Earthly riches available to the Source.

Ephesians 3:8 — "The unsearchable riches of Christ." Spiritual wealth that cannot be measured or exhausted.

John 6 (feeding of 5,000) — Jesus supplied according to His riches, not the five loaves.

Deuteronomy / Wilderness narrative — God provided supernaturally for 40 years without natural infrastructure.

Philippians 4:15–16 — The Philippians' repeated generosity from their own need — the faith that activates the policy.

Summary 2 of 5

Main Teachings

1. God is your source — not your job. Channels come and go. Source does not. If one channel closes, God has others you don't know about.

2. "My God" creates covenant. Knowing about God gives you nothing. "My God" binds Him by moral and legal obligation to His children (husband/wife analogy).

3. "Shall" is a decree, not a suggestion. Shall > will > may. Kings and God use "shall" to establish laws. Philippians 4:19 is a fixed kingdom law.

4. God's Word carries power to fulfill itself. Just as "let there be light" produced light, "I shall supply" produces supply. You don't need to ask how.

5. Coverage is comprehensive. Spiritual, physical, material, health, financial — everything for life and godliness.

6. It covers believers walking by faith. The Philippians gave from their own need — their generosity was the evidence of trust.

7. According to His riches. Three tiers: earthly, cosmic, and unsearchable riches of Christ. He gives proportional to His wealth, not your need.

8. Personal testimony. "DJ, whenever you have a need, the need is met" — a personal revelation of a universal biblical truth.

Summary 3 of 5

Principles & Memorable Quotes

Key Principles:

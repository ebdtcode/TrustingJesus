Grace Giving

Genesis 12:1-3 • Malachi 3:10 • Luke 6:38

God's Grace Reached Down

"Now the Lord said to Abram, 'Get out of your country, from your family and from your father's house, to a land that I will show you. I will make you a great nation; I will bless you and make your name great; and you shall be a blessing.'"

Genesis 12:1-2 (NKJV)

Pure Grace

Abraham was born into a family of idol worshipers. God, in His pure grace, reached down and said, "I want you."

Chosen by Grace

"Your fathers, including Terah the father of Abraham... dwelt on the other side of the River in old times; and they served other gods."

Joshua 24:2 (NKJV)

  • God chose one man out of a family, a tribe, a country of idol worshipers
  • Some of you are the only one in your family who follows God
  • It is nothing but total grace that you are here today — saved, delivered, made a child of God
How Tithing Began

Abraham Meets Melchizedek

"Then Melchizedek king of Salem brought out bread and wine; he was the priest of God Most High. And he blessed him and said: 'Blessed be Abram of God Most High... And blessed be God Most High, who has delivered your enemies into your hand.' And he gave him a tithe of all."

Genesis 14:18-20 (NKJV)

Why Did Abraham Give?

  • He looked back at where he came from — a family that didn't know God
  • He recognized God's blessing and prosperity in his life
  • With 318 servants, he defeated five kings and recovered everything
  • Out of a heart of love, appreciation, and thanksgiving

The Heart of Grace Giving

"How can I withhold from Him who has been so gracious to me? I must give back to God."

The Patriarchs: A Pattern of Blessing

Abraham

Self-employed, blessed beyond measure, 318 servants — gave the first tithe out of gratitude

Isaac

Sowed during famine, reaped a hundredfold — "the Lord blessed him" (Genesis 26:12)

Jacob

Left home with nothing, vowed "I will surely give You the tenth" — returned 20 years later, abundantly blessed

400 Years Before the Law

Tithing was practiced for 400 years before Moses codified it in the Law. Grace giving precedes legal obligation.

Key Truth

Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob all tithed — not because the law required it, but because grace compelled it.

The Tithe Is the Lord's

"And all the tithe of the land, whether of the seed of the land or of the fruit of the tree, is the Lord's. It is holy to the Lord."

Leviticus 27:30 (NKJV)

  • The tithe is the Lord's — it belongs to Him
  • The tithe is holy — you don't play with holy things
  • It must be first — God is Most High, not somewhere down on the list

A Blessing on Your House

"The best of all firstfruits of any kind... shall be the priest's; also you shall give to the priest the first of your ground meal, to cause a blessing to rest on your house."

Ezekiel 44:30 (NKJV)

The Promise

There is a way to have a blessing resting on your house: be faithful with the tithe.

The Challenge

Should People Cheat God?

"'Will a man rob God? Yet you have robbed Me! But you say, "In what way have we robbed You?" In tithes and offerings. You are cursed with a curse, for you have robbed Me, even this whole nation.'"

Malachi 3:8-9 (NKJV)

God prefaced this with: "I am the Lord, I do not change" (Malachi 3:6)

God's Extraordinary Invitation

"'Bring all the tithes into the storehouse... Try Me now in this,' says the Lord of hosts, 'If I will not open for you the windows of heaven and pour out for you such blessing that there will not be room enough to receive it. And I will rebuke the devourer for your sakes.'"

Malachi 3:10-11 (NKJV)

Put Me to the Test

This is the only place in Scripture where God says "Test Me." He stakes His reputation on this promise.

The Umbrella of Protection

  • To the faithful tither, there is an umbrella of protection over you
  • Under that umbrella, your work will be blessed
  • God will defend you and fight off disease and destruction
  • Even those who rise against you — God will cause you to be blessed

Personal Testimony

"A boss who hated me said, 'I'll be here long after you're gone.' Two weeks later, he was fired. God defends His faithful tithers."

Jesus Said

You Should Tithe

"Woe to you, teachers of the law and Pharisees, you hypocrites! You give a tenth of your spices... But you have neglected the more important matters of the law — justice, mercy and faithfulness. You should have practiced the latter, without neglecting the former."

Matthew 23:23 (NIV)

Jesus said: "You should tithe."

Look at What's Happening to You

"You have planted much, but harvested little. You eat, but never have enough. You drink, but never have your fill. You put on clothes, but are not warm. You earn wages, only to put them in a purse with holes in it."

Haggai 1:6 (NIV)

A Warning

Your desire for things will always exceed your income. Unless you learn to control that desire, you will always be in financial difficulty.

Faithful in Little, Faithful in Much

"If you are faithful in little things, you will be faithful in large ones. But if you are dishonest in little things, you won't be honest with greater responsibilities."

Luke 16:10 (NLT)

  • When you are faithful with the tithe, it sanctifies the remaining 90%
  • God will not share His secrets with people who steal from Him
  • Financial faithfulness unlocks spiritual revelation
  • You cannot serve God and money (Luke 16:13)

The Tithe vs. The Offering

The Tithe — Faithfulness

The first 10% already belongs to God. Returning it is not giving — it is being faithful with what is His. You cannot give God what is already His.

The Offering — Generosity

What you give above and beyond the tithe is your true gift. This is where Luke 6:38's promise of pressed down, shaken together, running over applies.

Give, and It Will Be Given to You

"Give, and it will be given to you: good measure, pressed down, shaken together, and running over will be put into your bosom. For with the same measure that you use, it will be measured back to you."

Luke 6:38 (NKJV)

Your Choice

A teaspoon or a shovel — the measure you use determines what comes back. God has made a plan for everyone to move from lack to abundance.

Why Grace Giving?

1. Gratitude

I am a witness to God's goodness — how can I withhold from Him?

2. Kingdom Expansion

Through faithfulness, I participate in souls being saved — "Thy kingdom come"

3. Eternal Treasure

What I give goes into an account that lasts throughout eternity

4. God's Unfailing Love

God loves you and wants you to be blessed — tithing is His plan for your provision

Prayer Points

From the sermon on Grace Giving

Prayer: Thanksgiving for God's Grace

Heavenly Father, just as You reached down in pure grace to choose Abraham from a family of idol worshipers, You have chosen us by Your unfailing love. Thank You for calling us out of darkness into Your marvelous light. Every good thing we have flows from Your gracious hand. We are witnesses to Your goodness.

Genesis 12:1-2; Joshua 24:2

Prayer: Faithfulness in Tithing

Lord God, the tithe is Yours — it is holy and set apart. We commit to bringing the firstfruits of our increase into Your storehouse. Remove from us any spirit of reluctance or selfishness. Give us hearts that joyfully prioritize You above all else. May we never rob You of what is rightfully Yours.

Malachi 3:10; Leviticus 27:30

Prayer: Financial Breakthrough

Father, we take You at Your Word — open the windows of heaven over our lives and pour out blessings we cannot contain. Rebuke the devourer on our behalf. Protect our income, our businesses, our health, and our resources. Break the cycle of planting much but harvesting little. Let the 90% go further under Your blessing than 100% ever could without You.

Malachi 3:10-11; Haggai 1:6

Prayer: Generosity and Contentment

Gracious God, we choose to be givers, not hoarders. As we give, may it be given back — good measure, pressed down, shaken together, running over. Break off any poverty mindset and spirit of lack. Deliver us from the lie that more possessions bring satisfaction. You are our portion and greatest treasure.

Luke 6:38; Luke 16:10-13

Prayer: Blessing on Our Houses

Lord, as we honor You with the best of our firstfruits, cause Your blessing to rest upon our houses. May our homes be sanctuaries of Your presence, peace, and provision. Let the umbrella of Your protection cover every member of our household. May our children walk in Your ways and experience covenant blessings.

Ezekiel 44:30; Malachi 3:11-12

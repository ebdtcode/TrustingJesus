Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Have I Not Called You — Pastor Chrys Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview & Context

Opening Intercession — The service opened with intercession for the USA: asking God for peace, civility, and unity across political lines; protection over leaders; wisdom for the President and officials; the Church to arise as light; and revival beginning in the House of God. “We push back darkness and believe God is at work as we pray in faith.”

Word Focus — God fulfills His purposes on earth through yielded men and women. From Isaiah’s “Here am I, send me” to Ezekiel’s “stand in the gap,” God seeks partners. The incarnation itself shows God working through human vessels.

Gideon’s Story (Judges 6–7) — Israel was oppressed by Midian; when they cried out, God called Gideon—a fearful, least-qualified man—“mighty man of valor,” promising, “I will be with you.” God uses the ordinary, adds His “extra” to our ordinary, and gets extraordinary results.

Summary 1 of 4

Key Applications

1. Reject Passivity — Cry Out to God — Don’t accept “whatever will be” resignation. Faith resists passivity. The children of Israel cried out, and God responded by sending deliverance through Gideon. Whatever you are going through, lift your voice and call on the Lord.

2. Pray and Develop Intimacy — Gideon sought confirmation and remained in God’s presence to receive step-by-step strategy. Intimacy with God births revelation, assurance, and divine blueprints. At the place of prayer, strategy for conquest is revealed, provision for vision is given, and the assurance of God’s presence comes alive. “You must pray. Give yourselves to prayer.”

3. Obey Even When God’s Strategy Seems Foolish — Jars, torches, trumpets; 300 men against an innumerable enemy. God reduced the army so Israel could never boast in their own strength. This preserves God’s glory and removes human boasting. “God chose the foolish things to shame the wise” (1 Corinthians 1:27).

Summary 2 of 4

Key Applications (continued)

4. Be Encouraged in Delay — If you’re discouraged because fulfillment hasn’t come after years of waiting, remember: God cannot lie. He qualifies the called. His presence goes with His word. Every time God gives you a word, that word comes with His presence and His power.

5. God Uses the Ordinary — God chose Moses the stutterer, Esther the orphan, fishermen as disciples. “God takes His extra and adds it to your ordinary and gets extraordinary.” When you feel you don’t have what it takes, you are the right candidate for God to use, because there is room for Him to work.

6. Say “Yes” Like Mary — “Be it unto me according to Your word.” Whatever God says, align your faith with His word. It doesn’t matter how long it takes—God has a plan for your life and that plan will come to fruition.

7. Others Are Connected to Your Call — The destiny of others is connected to what God has called you to do. That is why you cannot play with it, and that is why you must pray. When Bishop and the church began 35 years ago, it was just two people—but connections have connections, and God’s work has spread across the world.

Summary 3 of 4

Call to Respond

Salvation — For anyone who does not know Jesus: before you came alive, your days were already planned. Confessing Jesus as Lord makes Him Lord of your life. “If anyone will confess with their mouth that Jesus is Lord, that person will be saved” (Romans 10:9).

Rededication — For those who have grown cold: God still has that call upon your life. He is with you, just as He was with Israel even in their rebellion. The fire of the Holy Spirit can rekindle your heart and set your life ablaze once more.

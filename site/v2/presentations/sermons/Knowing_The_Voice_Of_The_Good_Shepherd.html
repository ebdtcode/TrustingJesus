The Shepherd & The Sheepfold

How Jesus protects, calls, and knows His sheep

Jesus Is The Door

  • Shepherds brought sheep to the sheepfold at night — sometimes 100 sheep from five different shepherds
  • One shepherd would lie across the gate to guard them
  • That is why Jesus said, "I am the door"
  • He keeps predators out and keeps wandering sheep in

People, we are protected. "I will never leave you nor forsake you." (Hebrews 13:5)

He Calls His Own By Name

  • In the morning each shepherd would call his own sheep
  • Only the sheep belonging to that shepherd would respond
  • The sheep ignored strangers — kept their heads down and grazed

How God Speaks Today

"God still speaks to His people. If you want to hear His voice, you can."

Way 1: Through His Word

The Primary Way

This is the "more sure word of prophecy" (2 Peter 1:19). Open your Bible and read.

  • Don’t only read — meditate
  • Be like a sheep: graze, rest, then chew the cud of the Word
  • "I found the Word and I ate it" — Jeremiah 15:16

Way 2: Holy Spirit’s Promptings

  • Especially at the place of prayer and worship
  • They don’t always come like Moses’ earthquake
  • Sometimes like Elijah: a still small voice (1 Kings 19:12)
  • If you are not sensitive, you will miss it

After you’ve prayed — please be quiet and let God speak.

Way 3: Audible Voice

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Knowing The Voice of The Good Shepherd — Pastor Chrys Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview & Key Scriptures

In this message from John 10, Pastor Chrys Johnson calls the church to recover the lost art of discerning the voice of the Good Shepherd in a world saturated with competing voices. Camping on John 10:27, she draws out four truths about our relationship with Christ — relationship, intimacy, discernment, and obedience — then exposes five voices that drown out the Shepherd and teaches five concrete ways God still speaks to His people today. The practical urgency: we are in the last days, AI has multiplied the voices, and if ever we needed to know His voice, it is now.

Key Scriptures:

John 10:1–5, 27–28 — The primary text. "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me… neither shall anyone snatch them out of my hand."

John 10:7, 9, 14, 16 — Jesus is the door of the sheepfold; other sheep not of this fold will also hear His voice.

Romans 8:1 — "There is therefore now no condemnation to those who are in Christ Jesus." The enemy’s voice of condemnation is exposed as a lie.

John 17:16; James 4:4 — In the world but not of it. Friendship with the world is enmity with God.

Philippians 4:8 — "Whatever things are true, noble… meditate on these things." The cure for the voice of our own thoughts.

Psalm 23:4; Psalm 46:10 — The Shepherd-king, "Be still and know that I am God." Audible voice testimony.

1 Thessalonians 5:16–18 — Rejoice evermore, pray without ceasing, in everything give thanks.

Psalm 103:7 — God made known His ways to Moses, His acts to Israel.

Hebrews 13:5 — "I will never leave you nor forsake you."

2 Peter 1:19; Jeremiah 15:16 — The more sure word of prophecy; "I found Your words, and I ate them."

1 Kings 19:12 — Elijah and the still small voice.

1 Samuel 3 — Samuel submits what he heard to Eli’s counsel.

Summary 1 of 5

Four Truths & Competing Voices

Four Key Truths from John 10:27

1. "My Sheep" — Relationship. Jesus is in relationship with us. He calls us His own.

2. "I Know Them" — Intimacy. Not just acquaintance but depth. We are known by Him and we know Him.

3. "They Hear My Voice" — Discernment. The ability to distinguish God’s voice from every other voice around us.

4. "They Follow Me" — Obedience. Hearing is not for passivity; it is so we can act on what we hear.

Five Voices Competing for Attention

1. The Enemy — Brings lies, fears, condemnation, and confusion. Satan is the accuser of the brethren, speaking contrary to what God says about us day and night. Answer: Romans 8:1 — no condemnation in Christ.

2. The World System — Normalizes what God calls sin. Pushes the envelope further each generation. What was once in the closet is now paraded. Sheep and lambs of God are not called to embrace the world’s values.

3. False Teachers — Distort the Word of God for their own gain. They sound spiritual but are not grounded in truth. A recent example: a preacher questioning Paul’s apostolic authority and calling his revelations "personal." They want to sound deep; God is simple.

4. Our Own Thoughts — Insecurities and past hurts influence our decisions and feelings. You are not your thoughts. You can’t stop the birds from flying over your head, but you can stop them from nesting in your hair. Put a "no vacancy" sign on your mind.

5. Circumstances — A fallen world. Every man experiences trouble. What the enemy means for evil, God uses for good. David walked through the valley but feared no evil because the Shepherd was with him. Rejoice in the midst.

Summary 2 of 5

The Shepherd & The Sheepfold

The Ancient Near East Picture — Shepherds in Palestine would graze their sheep by day, return to a shared sheepfold at night, and often five shepherds with twenty sheep each would use one enclosure — about a hundred sheep together. One shepherd stayed behind, lying down at the gate as the door, to keep predators out and wandering sheep in. This is why Jesus declared, "I am the door" (John 10:7, 9).

He Calls His Own By Name — In the morning, each shepherd would call his sheep, and only his sheep would respond. Other sheep ignored him with their heads down. Something in the pitch and cadence of the true shepherd’s voice made them look up and follow. A viral video illustration: three strangers tried and failed to call a flock; when the real shepherd spoke quietly, the sheep looked up and walked to him.

The Shepherd Initiated It — The shepherd’s chief responsibility is to get to know his sheep intimately — their tendencies, their weaknesses — and in that process the sheep come to know him. Our relationship with the Lord is the same: He initiated everything. Salvation from beginning to end is God’s idea. "Child of God, relax. The gospel we preach is done, done, done. He did it all."

Protection and Presence — We are in a sheepfold and He is watching over His Word concerning us. "I will never leave you nor forsake you" — and He cannot lie. In situations that cause fear, this promise holds.

Know His Ways, Not Just His Acts — Psalm 103:7 distinguishes the two. Israel saw what God did; Moses knew how God works. We need to know His ways — His cadence, His patterns — so that even in the valley we can say, "I know what God is doing," or at least, "I know I’m in His hand."

Summary 3 of 5

Five Ways God Still Speaks

1. Through His Word (Primary) — The more sure word of prophecy (2 Peter 1:19). Open your Bible and read; don’t only read, meditate. Be like a sheep that grazes, then stands under a tree chewing the cud: go back over the Word again and again. Be like the prophet who said, "I found the Word, and I ate it" (Jeremiah 15:16). Be a Berean — read it for yourself and let the Word validate what you are hearing.

2. The Holy Spirit’s Promptings — Especially at the place of prayer and worship. These promptings don’t always come like Moses’ earthquake on the mountain; they often come like Elijah’s still small voice (1 Kings 19:12). If you are not sensitive, you will miss them. Key practice: after you’ve prayed, be quiet and let God speak.

3. Audible Voice — Sometimes God speaks with an actual audible voice. Testimony: Bishop’s mother (Pastor Chrys’s mother-in-love) was told her sister and two daughters died in an accident. Weeping in the church, she suddenly heard a voice loud in her ear: "Be still and know that I am God" (Psalm 46:10). Her sister died, but the daughters survived with only scars on their faces.

4. Visions and Dreams — Sudden impressions where a name drops into your spirit, or images flash before your face. Every time Pastor Chrys responded to such impressions, the person she called was going through a crisis. Prophetic dreams that come to pass years later: her own salvation was first shown in a dream four years before it happened; the name "Harvest" for the church came through a vision of a golden field ripe unto harvest.

5. Ordinary Days — God speaks on completely normal days. Zacharias was doing his priestly duty on an ordinary day when the angel showed up (Luke 1). Bishop in Tulsa cited a random example in Bible study — "don’t sign a contract tomorrow" — and a pastor in the room had exactly that situation on his desk, obeyed, and later discovered the contract would have ruined him. Nothing special about the day. But God spoke.

Summary 4 of 5

Principles, Warnings & Takeaways

Timeless Principles

• God’s Word is simple — those who try to make it deep are often dodging it.

• A sheep that cannot distinguish its shepherd’s voice is in big trouble.

• Hearing exists for following — obedience is the point of discernment.

• Salvation is God’s idea from beginning to end; the Shepherd initiated everything.

• Know His ways, not just His acts (Psalm 103:7).

• God still speaks today — if you want to hear, you can train your spirit to hear.

• The primary way God speaks is still the written Word. Anything else confirms and never contradicts it.

• God uses men — godly counsel — to confirm what He speaks to you.

Warnings

• Against unfiltered voices — the enemy, the world, false teachers, your thoughts, and circumstances will all vie for your attention. Especially now with AI, you cannot believe everything you see and hear.

• Against shacking up — if you are not married, you have no business living together. The world has normalized it; God has not.

• Against false teachers who sound spiritual — if it’s not grounded in truth, reject it, no matter who is preaching. Even men questioning Paul’s apostolic authority.

• Against acting alone on dreams or visions — don’t run off to fulfill a dream without submitting it to spiritual leaders. The man who moved his family to Mexico without counsel paid a steep price.

• Against perfectionism in a fallen world — if you seek perfection you will get frustrated. Every man experiences trouble.

Practical Takeaways

• Open your Bible and meditate — chew the cud of the Word like a sheep.

• After you pray, be quiet — give God space to speak.

• Respond to sudden promptings — a name in your spirit, a flash before your face.

• When thoughts attack, post a "no vacancy" sign on your mind.

• Rejoice evermore — not for the trouble, but in the midst of it.

• Submit dreams and visions to godly counsel before acting.

• If you are not yet in the sheepfold, pray the sinner’s prayer today. If you don’t have a home church, find a Bible-believing church.

One of the most important responsibilities of the shepherd is to get acquainted with the sheep — to familiarize himself with each one, know their tendencies, know their weaknesses. And through that process the sheep get to know him. When you read the story, it is the shepherd who initiated this. Not the sheep. And even in our relationship with the Lord, it is He who initiated everything.

That statement Jesus made was a bold declaration. "My sheep know my voice. My sheep hear me and they follow me." It is a statement of faith. Because this thing called salvation, from beginning to end, is God’s idea. He originated it. It’s not us. Sometimes we think we are the ones — yeah, we’ll clap for you — but it’s not you.

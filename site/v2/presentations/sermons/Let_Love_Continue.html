God's Unfailing Love - Part 6

Let Love Continue

Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Hebrews 13:1-3

The Foundation Text

"Let brotherly love continue. Do not forget to entertain strangers, for by so doing some have unwittingly entertained angels. Remember the prisoners as if chained with them—those who are mistreated—since you yourselves are in the body also."

Hebrews 13:1-3 (NKJV)

Context: 12 Chapters of Theology

After revealing deep truths about Jesus...

  • His pre-incarnation and divine nature
  • His role as the Lamb of God
  • His superiority as High Priest
  • The new covenant replacing the old

The Question

If Jesus is truly Lord, what are you going to do about it?

Belief Must Lead to Behavior

"You believe that there is one God. You do well. Even the demons believe—and tremble!"

James 2:19 (NKJV)

The demons believe but their behavior does not correspond with their belief.

Philadelphia: Brotherly Love

The City of Brotherly Love

"Let brotherly love continue."

Hebrews 13:1

Key Insight

Not "let brotherly love begin" — but continue. It already exists in your heart as a believer.

Love Is Your New Nature

  • You have eternal life — your nature is now love
  • Being mean and selfish is now foreign to you
  • You share the very life of Christ
  • Love kept Jesus on the cross — not the nails

Starting vs. Staying in Love

It's easy to fall in love.

Ask the couples who are fighting today how easy it was...

It's challenging to stay in love.

Love Will Be Tested

By three things:

Time
Pressure
Disappointment

Time reveals what's real — like paint quality only shows after sun and rain.

"Let" = Your Choice

God has done everything to make this possible. It's hard but not impossible because you have His love inside you. But you have to make the choice.

Two Options

Let brotherly love continue OR allow resentment and unforgiveness to take over.

Why Relationships Fail

  • Marriages fail when someone decides: "I'm not letting love continue"
  • Friendships break when one person has "had enough"
  • People leave churches and find the next church also has people
  • Business partners become enemies

Continuing Means...

Being willing to love even when:

  • Someone talks mean to you
  • Someone betrays your trust
  • Someone disrespects you
  • Someone stabs you in the back
  • The usher wasn't nice to you

You Can Do It!

"I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me."

Philippians 4:13 (NKJV)

The True Meaning

This verse isn't just about running marathons — it's about being able to love like Christ in every situation, including suffering.

The Mark of True Disciples

"By this all will know that you are My disciples, if you have love for one another."

John 13:35 (NKJV)

Not by your gifting, preaching, singing, or intelligence — but by how you love one another.

Three Reasons to Let Love Continue

1. Obedience

The Lord said it — that's the only reason you need.

2. Gratitude

That's how Jesus loves you. How can you receive such love and not share it?

3. Humility

God loves that difficult person just as much as He loves you.

Verse 2

Love for the Stranger

"Do not forget to entertain strangers, for by so doing some have unwittingly entertained angels."

Hebrews 13:2 (NKJV)

Philoxenia: Love for Strangers

The opposite of xenophobia

Who is the "Stranger"?

  • People you don't know very well
  • People outside your circle
  • People different from you
  • Even people right here in church who aren't in your group

Don't Neglect the Stranger

"Neglect" doesn't mean intentional avoidance...

  • You become preoccupied with your own circle
  • Your schedule is optimized for your family, career, comfort
  • There's "no room left" for others
  • You passively drift away from hospitality

The Priest and the Levite

Too preoccupied to be interrupted

They had optimized their schedules to take care of the things they were concerned about — refusing to be interrupted by the stranger who had an urgent need.

The Garden Principle

You don't have to decide to grow weeds.

But if you want a particular fruit, you must plant it intentionally.

Application

Without intention, your life will grow "fences" — and everyone outside your fence, you don't care for.

Jesus Loved Strangers

  • When He died, we didn't know Him
  • We were alienated, far away from Him
  • He invited strangers into His heart
  • He made our problems His own
  • He died for strangers

Thank God — we're no longer strangers!

Entertaining Angels

Abraham, not knowing, simply showing hospitality to strangers, had dinner with angels and received information that saved his family.

Genesis 18

Don't underestimate how God uses simple acts of love and kindness to change human destiny.

Not Extra-Curricular

Loving and caring and reaching out to those both within and beyond your circle is not optional for the Christian. This is essential. This is how we live the Christian life.

The Final Accounting

"I was a stranger and you took Me in... Assuredly, I say to you, inasmuch as you did it to one of the least of these My brethren, you did it to Me."

Matthew 25:35, 40 (NKJV)

What If They Don't Love Back?

That's irrelevant.

What's relevant is: you did what the Lord wanted you to do.

The one you loved may not have appreciated it, but God was pleased.

Prayer

Father, I thank You that the love of God is shed abroad in my heart. I can let brotherly love continue. I choose to. I refuse to shut my bowels of compassion. I choose to let Your love, Jesus, flow through me — to my brothers, to my sisters, to the stranger, and to the suffering. May Christ be glorified. In Jesus' name, Amen.

Let Love Continue

The choice is yours

"Let brotherly love continue."

Hebrews 13:1

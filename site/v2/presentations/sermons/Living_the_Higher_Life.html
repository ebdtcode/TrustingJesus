Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Living the Higher Life — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Three Marks of Spiritual Growth

Faith in Christ — Not faith in yourself, not faith in something else, not faith in faith — but genuine confidence in who Jesus Christ is and what He has accomplished. This is the foundation God looks for in every believer.

Love for One Another — The New Testament emphasis on love centers on loving one another. It is a supernatural love that goes beyond preference and convenience, flowing from our union with Christ.

Everlasting Hope — A steadfast, anchoring hope that keeps us secure no matter what life brings. When these three qualities are active and growing in our lives, we are experiencing true spiritual maturity and growth.

Summary 1 of 4

Living from Above: Identity and Altitude

The Airplane Illustration — At 10,000 feet the turbulence disappears and it is smooth sailing. The weather below did not change — the altitude did. When we live from our position in Christ, we respond from peace rather than from the storms of life.

The Eagle Among Chickens — An eagle raised among chickens ate worms from the dust until it discovered its wings. Christians are eagles, not chickens. We are designed to live above the flesh, above sin, above this world system — soaring in the abundant life Christ came to give.

Embrace Your Identity — If we continue to think of ourselves as mere sinners and do not embrace our identity in Christ, we live far beneath our privileges. Our spiritual address is “in Christ,” seated with Him in heavenly places.

Summary 2 of 4

Christ Our Source: Complete in Him

Hold Fast to the Head — Colossians 2:19 reveals the key to the higher life: holding fast to Christ, the Head. He is both the authority over our lives and the source of everything we need to grow and thrive.

The Fullness of the Godhead — In Christ dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily (Colossians 2:9-10). Everything God is, Jesus is. Everything God has, Jesus has. Since we are joined to Christ, we lack nothing and are complete in Him.

The Light Bulb Illustration — A light bulb has everything it needs to shine except power. Connected to its source, it shines. Disconnected, it is still a light bulb but produces no light. We are the light bulb; Christ is the power source. Stay connected.

Guard Against Distractions — Satan cannot undo what God has done, but he can try to disconnect our minds from Christ. False philosophies, false humility, and mystical additions all aim to shift our focus away from the sufficiency of Christ alone. Grace points us right to Jesus.

Summary 3 of 4

Three Practical Steps for the Higher Life

1. Seek Things Above — Because above is better, and above is where you spiritually live. Your address is “in Christ” — seek the things that are where you are. You have access to all God is, all God has, and all God can do.

2. Set Your Mind on Things Above — Meditate on God’s Word. Use your mouth as a “remote control” — when negative or earthly thoughts come, click and change the channel. Speak the truth: “I am in Christ. My life is complete in Him. Everything He is, I am.”

3. Mortify the Deeds of the Flesh — The old man is already dead — crucified with Christ. But he left behind old furniture: fornication, lying, anger, jealousy, gossip. As the new occupant, discard what belongs to the old man and bring in new things from Christ. Put off the old, put on the new.

Pray for One Another — Even those who are growing need prayer. The enemy constantly tries to distract believers from their focus on Christ through philosophies, false ideas, and traditions of men. Pray that nothing the enemy tries will succeed in pulling us away from Jesus.

Guest Message

Loving Jesus

Four Expressions of a Heart That Loves Him

Pastor Obadiah Swen • Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney

Psalm 42:1-2 • June 8, 2026

The Cry of a Thirsty Heart

"I long to drink of You, O God, to drink deeply from the streams of pleasure flowing from Your presence. My longings overwhelm me for more of You. My soul thirsts, pants, and longs for the living God."

Psalm 42:1-2 (TPT)

David is not describing a duty — he is describing a thirst. The question for us: what does it actually mean to love Jesus?

A Professor and His Sleeping Students

Claudio Freidzon, used by God in a great Argentine revival, taught at a Bible school. One morning his students were asleep in class — they had been at a mighty crusade led by the evangelist Carlos Annacondia. Curious, he went, saw the miracles, befriended the man, and learned from him. His own church began to grow; soon he was even on television.

Success was multiplying — but something was still missing.

"You Have No Time for the Holy Spirit"

A Friend's Honest Word

"You tell me everything you are doing — but you do not have time for the Holy Spirit. You can do for God, but God is interested in you being with Him."

Claudio read Benny Hinn's Good Morning, Holy Spirit, sought prayer, was powerfully touched — and an aisle full of people fell under God's power. A revival was born. The question he leaves us:

Why not here? Why not us? Why not now?

The Best Thing You Can Do

Not to Impress — to Show You Jesus

"My intention today is not to impress you. My intention is to show you Jesus, and how important it is for you to devote your life to loving Him."

The best thing you can do — for yourself, your family, your friends, your community, your church, and your nation — is to fall in love with Jesus.

The Framework

Four Expressions of Loving Jesus

How a heart in love with Christ actually speaks

Four Things Love Says

1. Jesus, I love You
2. Jesus, I want You
3. Jesus, I surrender all to You
4. Jesus, I say yes
Expression One

Jesus, I Love You

Love that answers a love that came first

The Supreme Commandment

"You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your mind."

Matthew 22:37 (NKJV) • "…and with all your strength" — Mark 12:30

Heart, soul, mind, strength — nothing held back. This is the first and greatest commandment, and it is where everything else begins.

He First Loved You

"I have loved you with an everlasting love; therefore with lovingkindness I have drawn you."

Jeremiah 31:3 (NKJV)

"Behold what manner of love the Father has bestowed on us, that we should be called children of God!"

1 John 3:1 (NKJV)

You cannot truly love Him until you recognize you were loved first.

What Loving Jesus Means

"Everyone who has this hope in Him purifies himself, just as He is pure."

1 John 3:3 (NKJV)

  • To purify yourself — separate from everything that defiles
  • To recognize that He is God, He is Lord
  • To want to be like Him — to know His ways and His heart
  • To want to be with Him — in His presence

When You Love, You Tell Everyone

When you fall in love, you can't help it — you tell your friends, you want to spend every moment together, you can't get away from the person.

So It Is with Jesus

When you fall in love with Jesus, you have to let people know about Him. You want to spend time in His presence. You cannot get away from Him — you cannot, you cannot, you cannot.

You Cannot Delegate Your Quiet Time

"O God of my life, I'm lovesick for You in this weary wilderness… for Your tender mercies mean more to me than life itself."

Psalm 63 (TPT)

There is something about intimacy with God that you cannot delegate. You have to spend the time yourself. This is the voice of someone who is pursuing God.

Expression Two

Jesus, I Want You

Loving Him means wanting His power to live

You Need the Power from on High

"Behold, I send the Promise of My Father upon you; but tarry… until you are endued with power from on high."

Luke 24:49 (NKJV)

"You shall receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you; and you shall be witnesses to Me… to the end of the earth."

Acts 1:8 (NKJV)

To love Jesus, you need the Holy Spirit.

Boldness That Shakes the Place

"Grant to Your servants that with all boldness they may speak Your word, by stretching out Your hand… And… the place… was shaken; and they… spoke the word of God with boldness."

Acts 4:29-31 (NKJV)

Threatened to stay silent, Peter and John prayed for boldness instead. They loved the Lord — but they also wanted Him: the power to do what He had called them to do.

"Just Give Me One Hundred Souls"

At a Reinhard Bonnke crusade in Uhuru Park, Kenya, a divorced woman named Teresa — barred from ministry, depressed — wept in the back: "Lord, give Bonnke a hundred thousand; just give me one hundred souls." She pursued his meetings until he prayed for her, and the power of God hit her.

Back home she began a Bible study: 17 came, then 100, then 200, 400, 2,000, 4,000. They moved to a school, then a stadium. Eventually her monthly service in Uhuru Park drew more than two hundred thousand people.

It takes Jesus.

Expression Three

Jesus, I Surrender All to You

A living sacrifice that refuses to leave the altar

Your Genuine Expression of Worship

"Surrender yourselves to God to be His sacred, living sacrifices… For this becomes your genuine expression of worship… be inwardly transformed… through a total reformation of how you think."

Romans 12:1-2 (TPT)

Surrender is not a feeling — it is worship offered with your whole self.

The Seed Must Die to Bear Fruit

"Unless a grain of wheat falls into the ground and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it produces much grain."

John 12:24 (NKJV)

Your life was destined to produce fruit — but you are a seed. To produce the fruit, the seed has to die.

A Living Sacrifice Can Roll Off the Altar

The Danger of a Living Sacrifice

A dead sacrifice cannot move — but a living one can crawl off the altar. The most reasonable response to His mercy is to offer ourselves so that we can die.

  • Die to self and to pride
  • Die to gossiping and competing
  • Die to the lusts of the flesh and to sin
  • Die to everything that does not glorify God

Take Up Your Cross

"If anyone desires to come after Me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross, and follow Me."

Luke 9:23 (NKJV)

Where was Jesus going with His cross? To die. To follow Him is to be willing to die — and total surrender requires total dependence, which means a real life of prayer that expects God to answer.

Surrender Means He Is Lord

Wholehearted Obedience

You can't surrender to God and still want to do your own thing. You can't say, "That's just who I am." If you love God and surrender to Him, then He is Lord — Lord of all.

"He's Lord. He's Lord. He's Lord."

Abraham and a Willing Isaac

"Now I know that you fear God, since you have not withheld your son, your only son, from Me."

Genesis 22:12 (NKJV) • Hebrews 6:13-14; 11:17-19

Isaac was no toddler — the Bible calls him a lad, twelve or fourteen. No parent could bind him by force; he had to be willing. Because Abraham held nothing back, God swore by Himself. God could find no greater word for such love — and because Abraham was willing, Jesus could come.

Expression Four

Jesus, I Say Yes

Declaring the faithfulness of God

Nothing Is Too Difficult for Him

"Ah, Lord God! Behold, You have made the heavens and the earth by Your great power… There is nothing too hard for You."

Jeremiah 32:17 (NKJV)

You say yes to God by recognizing that He is God — lifting Him up for what He has done and for who He is.

Declare His Goodness

"Shout hallelujah to Yahweh! May every one of His devoted lovers hear my passionate praise to Him."

Psalm 111:1 (TPT)

You say yes because God is faithful — He has not failed us. So we declare and proclaim His goodness and His mighty works to the nations.

Loving Jesus Means…

Purity — we walk in purity before God
Power — we flow in the demonstration of the Holy Spirit
Prayer & Fasting — we live a life of prayer and fasting
Praise — we declare the goodness of God to the nations
Response

Prayer Points

Praying the four expressions into our lives

1. Jesus, I Love You

For a Heart That Answers His Love

Lord, You loved me first with an everlasting love. Stir in me a love that holds nothing back — heart, soul, mind, and strength. Make my quiet time undelegated and unhurried, and let me purify myself because I have this hope in You.

2. Jesus, I Want You

For the Power of the Holy Spirit

Father, I do not only want to work for You — I want to be with You. Endue me with power from on high. Give me boldness like Peter and John, and shake every place where I carry Your word. It takes Jesus — so fill me afresh.

3. Jesus, I Surrender All to You

For a Sacrifice That Stays on the Altar

Lord, I am a living sacrifice — keep me from rolling off the altar. Help me die to self, pride, gossip, competing, and the lusts of the flesh. Make me totally dependent, a person of prayer who expects You to answer, and surrendered in wholehearted obedience. You are Lord of all.

4. Jesus, I Say Yes

For a Life That Declares His Faithfulness

God, nothing is too difficult for You. I say yes — yes to purity, yes to Your power, yes to prayer and fasting, yes to praising You before the nations. Like Abraham, let me withhold nothing, and let my "yes" count.

5. Why Not Us? Why Not Now?

For Revival to Begin Here

Lord, what You did through Claudio Freidzon and through a praying woman in Kenya, do among us. Touch our lives, break off every hindrance, rebuke the spirit of anxiety, shame, and condemnation, and make us channels of Your grace, love, mercy, and power. Why not here? Why not us? Why not now?

Declaration

My Confession of Love

Jesus, I love You.

Jesus, I want You.

Jesus, I surrender all to You.

Jesus, I say yes.

"Your presence is heaven to me." My life will never be the same — because I said yes.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Loving Jesus — Pastor Obadiah Swen

Key points, principles, scripture, and takeaways

Overview

Anchor text — Psalm 42:1-2 (TPT). The thirst of David's soul for the living God frames the whole message: loving Jesus is not duty, it is desire.

Guest preacher — Pastor Obadiah Swen ("Pastor Obi"), preaching at Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney (host Bishop Darlingston Johnson and Lady Grace). His stated aim: not to impress, but to show Jesus and stir a fresh love for Him.

Big idea — The best thing you can do, for everyone connected to you, is to fall in love with Jesus. He maps that love in four expressions.

Four expressions — "Jesus, I love You" • "Jesus, I want You" • "Jesus, I surrender all to You" • "Jesus, I say yes."

Summary 1 of 6

Key Scriptures

Psalm 42:1-2; Psalm 63 (TPT) — The thirst and lovesickness of a heart pursuing God.

Matthew 22:37 / Mark 12:30 — Love God with all your heart, soul, mind, strength: the supreme commandment.

Jeremiah 31:3; 1 John 3:1-3 — He loved first; we are His children; this hope makes us purify ourselves.

Luke 24:49; Acts 1:8; Acts 4:29-31 — Wait for power; receive the Spirit; pray for boldness that shakes the place.

Romans 12:1-2 (TPT); John 12:24; Luke 9:23 — Living sacrifice and worship; the seed must die; deny self and take up the cross.

Genesis 22; Hebrews 6:13-14; 11:17-19 — Abraham and a willing Isaac; God swears by Himself over such love.

Jeremiah 32:17; Psalm 111 — Nothing too hard for God; declare His goodness and mighty works.

Summary 2 of 6

Main Teachings

Expression 1 — "Jesus, I love You" — We love Him because He first loved us. Real love wants to be with Him, become like Him, tell everyone, and purify itself. Intimacy cannot be delegated.

Expression 2 — "Jesus, I want You" — Loving Jesus means wanting the power of the Holy Spirit to live and to serve. "It takes Jesus." Illustrated by Claudio Freidzon's revival and Reinhard Bonnke's praying woman in Kenya.

Expression 3 — "Jesus, I surrender all to You" — Worship is offering yourself as a living sacrifice. The seed must die to bear fruit. A living sacrifice can roll off the altar, so we die daily to self — in total dependence and obedience, with Christ as Lord. Abraham held nothing back; Isaac was willing.

Expression 4 — "Jesus, I say yes" — Nothing is too difficult for God. We say yes by recognizing who He is and declaring His faithfulness to the nations.

Summary 3 of 6

Principles

Love answers love — You cannot love Jesus until you recognize He loved you first.

You can do FOR God yet miss being WITH Him — Activity is not intimacy; God wants you, not just your work.

Intimacy is non-transferable — You cannot delegate your quiet time.

It takes Jesus — Loving and serving Him both require the power of the Holy Spirit.

The seed must die to multiply — Surrender, not self-preservation, produces fruit.

Surrender = dependence = prayer + obedience — You can't make Him Savior and not make Him Lord.

Willingness moves heaven — Abraham's withheld-nothing obedience drew God's oath.

Summary 4 of 6

Warnings & Cautions

Busy for God, distant from God — You can build a growing ministry and still "have no time for the Holy Spirit." Guard the relationship, not just the results.

A living sacrifice can roll off the altar — Surrender is not a one-time event; the flesh keeps trying to climb down. Stay on the altar.

Praying without expecting — Don't pray for praying's sake; pray expecting God to answer.

"That's just who I am" — You cannot truly surrender while reserving the right to do your own thing. If He is Lord, He is Lord of all.

Summary 5 of 6

Practical Takeaways

Protect undelegated time with Jesus — Schedule quiet time you will not hand off to anyone or anything.

Move from doing-for to being-with — Audit your week: is there real time spent simply with the Holy Spirit?

Ask for power and boldness — Pray Acts 4 over yourself before you carry His word.

Name what must die — Identify the specific pride, gossip, competing, or lust to lay on the altar today.

Say a definite yes — "Jesus, I love You; I want You; I surrender all; I say yes" — then tell Him what you are saying yes for.

Declare His faithfulness — Make praise and testimony of His goodness a daily habit.

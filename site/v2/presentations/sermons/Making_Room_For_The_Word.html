The Word Works Series

Making Room For The Word

The Seed Is Full of Power — Your Part Is to Make Room

Bishop Darlingston Johnson • Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney

Luke 8:11 • James 1:21 • July 6, 2026

Same Sower, Same Seed — Four Soils

"Now the parable is this: The seed is the word of God."

Luke 8:11 (NKJV)

Good soil, good seed — good harvest. Good seed, bad soil — poor harvest. No matter how good the seed is, the quality of the soil is critical. Jesus used this to teach how important it is that we prepare our hearts for the Word.

The Word Is Alive

The Word of God is not just a source of information. Not just instruction. Not just doctrine — though doctrine is extremely important. The Scripture teaches that the Word of God is a living thing.

More Than Information

When the Word speaks to you concerning salvation, it is not just giving you information about salvation — it comes with the power to save you. The Word actually creates salvation.

The Source of the New Birth

"…having been born again, not of corruptible seed but incorruptible, through the word of God which lives and abides forever."

1 Peter 1:23 (NKJV)

God is the source of the new birth. Jesus is the source. The Holy Spirit is the source. And it is equally true that the Word is the source — God created us anew through the power of His Word.

Say It With Me

"The Word does not just inform — it creates."

The Word creates healing. The Word creates salvation. The Word creates faith, hope, and peace. It literally has the power to bring into being that which it is talking about. In every Word God speaks, He has compressed omnipotence and omniscience — everything necessary for that Word to fulfill itself.

Watching Over My Word

"…for I am watching over My word to perform it."

Jeremiah 1:12 (NASB)

Wait — if the Word already has the power, why does God have to watch over it? Not because He doubts His Word. He wanted to give young Jeremiah another reason not to doubt — "I Myself am involved in the process."

The Double Guarantee

The Check Has Funds

Like a $1,000 check with money in the bank — the Word already carries all the power it needs. No Word of His is void of power; no Word He speaks will return void.

Plus a Personal Guarantee

"And I guarantee you — you will get your money." God backs His Word Himself: "I will see to it that that Word comes to pass. So don't worry — go ahead and do what the Word says."

God Has Spoken by His Son

"…[God] has in these last days spoken to us by His Son… who being the brightness of His glory and the express image of His person, and upholding all things by the word of His power…"

Hebrews 1:2-3 (NKJV)

The word for us today is what God has spoken through Jesus. Understand the gospel, believe the gospel, hold on to the gospel — it is the power of God unto salvation (Romans 1:16).

The Word Sustains the Universe

Jesus is seated — and the whole universe, everything God created, and even the expansion of the universe is happening right now because of the power in the word He has spoken. It's the word that is sustaining the universe. It's the power in the word that is expanding it.

"The Word — it's where the power is."

2 Peter 1:3-4

Everything You Need Is Inside

All things that pertain to life and godliness

Already Given

"…as His divine power has given to us all things that pertain to life and godliness, through the knowledge of Him who called us by glory and virtue…"

2 Peter 1:3 (NKJV)

Love, joy, peace, faith, hope, wisdom, understanding, power, anointing — everything we need has already been given. Stop looking outside; start thanking God for what He has put inside.

A Millionaire Begging on the Streets

You can have a million dollars in the bank and die as a pauper begging on the streets — because you don't know you're a millionaire. Many believers don't know what they contain, what has been given to them.

How Do You Walk In It?

Through the knowledge of Him. And where does that knowledge come from? The Word. Without the Word you cannot know — and cannot walk in — what is already yours.

Partakers of the Divine Nature

"…by which have been given to us exceedingly great and precious promises, that through these you may be partakers of the divine nature…"

2 Peter 1:4 (NKJV)

Through your relationship with the Word, your faith in the Word, your understanding of the power of the Word — the Word makes you a partaker of His divine nature. Without the Word, I cannot partake of the divine nature.

I Am Living Forever

The Word that gave life to you is eternal — so that which it produces partakes of its nature. That which is incorruptible has produced what is incorruptible. "I see you 1,000 years from now. I see you 1 million years from now."

The Fruit of the Spirit Is Your Nature

Joy is your nature. Peace is your nature. Hope is your nature. Love is your nature. Not something you fight for — it's who you are spiritually. The more knowledge we have of these things, the more we walk in them.

Then Why Different Outcomes?

If the Word is that powerful, why don't we all experience the same level of outcome? What Jesus reveals through the parable: it has nothing to do with the Word, nothing to do with the Sower.

It has to do with the receptivity of the heart — the condition of the heart. Not all hearts are equally prepared.

The Best Medicine

"He sent His word and healed them, and delivered them from their destructions."

Psalm 107:20 (NKJV)

The Word is the best medicine for your spirit, your emotions, your mind — even your body. If you need to see the doctor, see the doctor. But don't neglect the power that is in the Word.

The Word That Heals

Billy Bimba

He came too close to dying, too many times — but he had a revelation of the Word and refused to let go: "I will live and not die, and I will declare the glory of God" (Psalm 118:17).

Dodie Osteen

Diagnosed with terminal cancer. "I had the Word everywhere — everywhere." Declaring, believing, declaring God's Word — and supernaturally, that Word brought healing to her body.

A Word That Has Produced for Decades

Psalm 91, 1990 — "I am convinced that the promises of God in Psalm 91, and the power of God in those promises, delivered Pastor Chris and me from possible death."

The College Rhema (1977)

Meditating on the promises as a student, the Holy Spirit quickened it: "DJ, whenever you have a need, that need is already met." Because in Christ you've already been blessed with all spiritual blessings — and after all these years, that Word has produced.

Pray From Assurance

"And my God shall supply all your need according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus."

Philippians 4:19 (NKJV)

"I pray — but the prayer is one of thanksgiving, one of assurance. Not pleading, not begging. When I become aware of a need, I say thank You. I remind myself, I stand on the Word."

Joshua 1:8 Still Works

"This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth, but you shall meditate in it day and night… for then you will make your way prosperous, and then you will have good success."

Joshua 1:8 (NKJV)

Not a promise to make us all billionaires — a promise of success in doing God's will. None of us needs to fail at God's will, because everything we need to fulfill it is already in the Word.

Our Part

Make Room

You don't have to make the Word strong

The Farmer Doesn't Empower the Seed

If you are worried, anxious, thinking you're going to fail — pause and remind yourself: the power necessary to fulfill His will is in the Word He has spoken to you.

"The farmer does not give power to the seed — the seed is full of power. The farmer makes room for the seed to take root. That's all we're called to do."

Three Unhealthy Soils

1. The Closed Heart

The wayside — the birds come immediately. Closed to understanding, the Word never takes root.

2. The Casual Heart

No roots — pressure, persecution, and temptation collapse a shallow relationship with the Word.

3. The Crowded Heart

Thorns — cares of this life and the deceitfulness of riches leave the Word no room to grow.

The Closed Heart

An atheist scientist was asked in a debate: if Jesus Himself appeared before you — like He did with Thomas, "Touch My hand" — would you believe? He paused. "No. I would consider that a hallucination."

Such a person doesn't lack evidence — they're just closed-minded. The Word cannot produce life in anyone who refuses to understand it.

Believers Can Be Closed Too

The Gifts of the Spirit

Millions say the supernatural gifts ended with the apostles — and never experience words of knowledge, healing, or tongues. Not because the power isn't in the Word, but because their minds are closed to that truth.

Giving and Receiving

"I haven't experienced every promise in the Bible, but this one I know: God blesses giving. You reap more than you sow." Yet many close their minds every time the subject comes up.

Ask yourself: what truths in Scripture have you closed your mind to?

Construct, Don't Deconstruct

Many who are "deconstructing" their faith have never constructed — they're not grounded enough to deconstruct, because nothing was ever built. If I must choose between the man with ten PhDs and Jesus for my eternal soul, I'm trusting the One who died and rose.

"It is written: 'Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of God.'"

Matthew 4:4 (NKJV)

Jesus settled it: the Scriptures are the Word of God. If you begin to question the Scriptures, you can end up anywhere. Please don't end up anywhere — end up in heaven.

The Casual Heart

The word falls, but there are no roots. Pressure is real. Tests are real. Tribulation — Jesus said you will have it. When the storm comes, the trees that fall are those whose roots didn't go very deep.

The Storm Reveals the Roots

Trials reveal those with a shallow relationship with the Word. But those with a deep relationship will stand — they will go through the valley and come out on the other side, and what was meant to destroy them will actually work for them.

THE Way of Escape

"God is faithful, who will not allow you to be tempted beyond what you are able, but with the temptation will also make the way of escape…"

1 Corinthians 10:13 (NKJV)

Not a way — THE way. And the way of escape is the Word: the Word you put in your heart (Psalm 119:11), the Word you put in your mouth, the Word you feed on. "Thy rod and Thy staff, they comfort me" (Psalm 23:4).

Is Your Relationship Casual?

  • After a sermon, do you ever think about it again — or is "Amen, good word, Bishop" the end of it?
  • Do you ask: what is this saying to me that should change the way I think or behave?
  • Is the only time you open the Bible once a week?
  • If the Word isn't renewing your mind — changing your opinions — your relationship with it is shallow.

The Crowded Heart

The cares of this life, the deceitfulness of riches, and the desire for other things enter in and choke the word. They don't remove the Word — they just make it impossible for it to grow, because there's no room.

"The Word needs room. It needs space. Just like in a garden — your seeds need room."

The Problem Is Neglect

The problem is not with hearing the Word — it's neglect. And we neglect the Word not because we don't know it's important, but because we're just too busy. The priority of the Word slips to third, fourth, fifth — way down the list.

Even the Preacher

"How do I know all of this? Because I've experienced it. I'm not immune. I can become so busy doing things — good things — and neglect the time I need in the Word, meditating on the truths, the promises, the instructions, and acting on them."

The $200 Cable Bill

From Genesis to Revelation, God's people are to invest a significant portion of what they receive into the work of God. But so many other "important" things compete that at the end of the day we give little or nothing — hearers of the Word, but not doers.

"I'm paying $200-plus a month to watch the news and Family Feud? Surely I could put $150 of that into the work of the Lord." A crowded heart shows up in the calendar and the budget.

What You Need: A Cultivated Heart

"Therefore lay aside all filthiness and overflow of wickedness, and receive with meekness the implanted word, which is able to save your souls."

James 1:21 (NKJV)

Receive the word with meekness — that's the key. Meekness is the opposite of pride: humble, teachable, surrendered. It is meekness that prepares the heart for the word.

Softer, Not Stronger

"God is not asking us to be stronger — He's asking us to be softer. God is not asking you to be weaker — He's asking you to be meeker."

Then the next time God says forgive, you won't argue — you'll say, "Okay, Lord, let's do it." The next time He says give, you'll say, "Let's do it — I'm going to release the power of generosity in my life."

Prayer Points

Making room for the Word

1. The Word That Creates

Isaiah 55:11 • Jeremiah 1:12

Father, thank You that Your Word does not just inform me — it creates. No Word of Yours is void of power, and no Word You speak returns void. And thank You for the double guarantee: You are watching over Your Word to perform it, so every promise over my life, my family, and my future will surely come to pass.

2. Everything Already Inside

2 Peter 1:3-4

Father, Your divine power has already given me all things that pertain to life and godliness. I stop looking outside for what You have put inside. Lord, open my eyes — may I understand Your Word and its power to make me a partaker of Your divine nature, so that love, joy, peace, and hope flow as my true nature, not something I fight to produce.

3. Healing & Protection

Psalm 107:20 • Psalm 118:17 • Psalm 91

Father, You sent Your Word and healed — Your Word is the best medicine for my spirit, my emotions, my mind, and my body. I declare: I will live and not die, and I will declare the glory of God. I put the promises of Psalm 91 in my heart: You are my refuge and my fortress; Your protective power surrounds me and my household now.

4. My Need Is Already Met

Philippians 4:19 • Joshua 1:8

Father, I thank You — not begging, but believing — that my need is already met, for You supply all my needs according to Your riches in glory through Christ Jesus. Help me meditate on Your Word day and night and do it, and I declare: I will succeed in doing Your will and fulfilling Your purpose for my life, because the power is in the Word.

5. I Refuse a Closed Heart

Matthew 4:4 • Romans 12:2

Father, I refuse a closed mind. I will not let doubt, unbelief, or neglect keep me from Your truth. Like Jesus in the wilderness, let it be settled in me: "It is written." I choose to construct my faith, not deconstruct it. Open my mind and heart to every truth in Your Word — even the ones I have been tempted to leave for someone else to deal with.

6. I Refuse a Casual Heart

1 Corinthians 10:13 • Psalm 119:11 • Psalm 23:4

Lord, I refuse a casual relationship with Your Word. Root it deeply in me, so that trials and pressures cannot topple me like a shallow-rooted tree in a storm. Thank You that with every temptation You make THE way of escape — and the way of escape is the Word. I hide Your Word in my heart; Your rod and Your staff comfort me through every valley.

7. I Refuse a Crowded Heart

Mark 4:19 • James 1:21

Lord, I refuse a crowded heart. Show me plainly — even in my calendar and my spending — what must be laid aside so there is room for Your Word. I lay aside pride and receive Your Word with meekness: humble, teachable, surrendered. And the next time You say forgive, or give — I will not argue. I will say, "Okay, Lord, let's do it."

Declaration

I Make Room

Father, thank You for the power that is in Your Word — Your Word creates everything I need for life and godliness, everything I need to do Your will.

I refuse a closed mind.

I refuse a casual attitude.

I refuse a crowded calendar.

Lord, I make room for the power of Your Word in my life. In Jesus' name.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Making Room For The Word — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, scripture, and takeaways

Overview

Anchor texts — Luke 8:11 ("the seed is the word of God") and James 1:21 (receive with meekness the implanted word), continuing the Word Works series on the Parable of the Sower.

Setting — Bishop Darlingston Johnson at Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney, July 6, 2026. The soils revisited — and the believer's one assignment: make room.

Big idea — The Word of God does not merely inform, instruct, or teach doctrine — it creates: it created the new birth, it sustains the universe through Christ, and it already contains everything needed for life, godliness, healing, provision, and success. The seed is already full of power.

Aim — Since our part is not to strengthen the Word but to prepare the heart, refuse the three unhealthy soils — closed, casual, and crowded — and cultivate a heart marked by meekness, so the Word can take root and fulfill its purpose.

Summary 1 of 7

Key Scriptures (I)

Luke 8:11 — "The seed is the word of God." Same sower, same seed, four soils: the outcome depends solely on the preparation of the heart, not the power of the seed.

1 Peter 1:23 — Born again through the incorruptible Word that lives and abides forever; what the eternal Word produces partakes of its eternal nature — "I am living forever."

Jeremiah 1:12 — "I am watching over My Word to perform it." Not doubt but a double guarantee: the power already in the Word, plus God's personal backing (the $1,000-check illustration).

Luke 1:37 / Isaiah 55:11 — "No Word of Mine is void of power… no Word I speak will return void." Every word God speaks is compressed with omnipotence and omniscience, sufficient to fulfill itself.

Hebrews 1:2-3 — God has spoken by His Son, who upholds all things by the word of His power — the universe is sustained and expanding right now by the power in the word. "The Word — it's where the power is."

Romans 1:16 — The gospel, believed and understood, is the power of God that produces and creates salvation and all its benefits.

Summary 2 of 7

Key Scriptures (II)

2 Peter 1:3-4 — All things for life and godliness already given, accessed "through the knowledge of Him" — and the Word is how we partake of the divine nature.

Psalm 107:20 — "He sent His Word and healed them." The Word is the best medicine — for spirit, emotions, mind, and body — alongside, not instead of, medical care.

Psalm 118:17 — "I will live and not die" — Billy Bimba's refusal to let go; Psalm 91 — the promises Bishop Johnson is convinced delivered him and Pastor Chris from death in 1990.

Philippians 4:19 / Ephesians 1:3 — "God shall supply all your needs" — the ground of the 1977 college rhema: "Whenever you have a need, that need is already met."

Joshua 1:8 — Meditate day and night and do the Word: prosperity and success in doing God's will.

Matthew 4:4 / Deuteronomy 8:3 — "It is written": Jesus' own settled confidence in Scripture — the reason to construct, not deconstruct, your faith.

1 Corinthians 10:13, Psalm 119:11, Psalm 23:4 — God makes THE way of escape — and the way of escape is the Word hidden in the heart.

Mark 4:19, James 1:21 — Cares and the deceitfulness of riches choke the word; the answer is to receive the implanted word with meekness.

Summary 3 of 7

Main Teachings (I)

The Word creates — because the Word is alive, when it speaks of salvation, healing, faith, hope, or peace, it brings that reality into being — it does not merely describe it.

The double guarantee — God watching over His Word (Jeremiah 1:12) is not doubt but added assurance: the power in the Word, plus God personally seeing it through — two reasons to act on the Word without worry.

Jesus upholds the universe by His word — creation, sustenance, and cosmic expansion are happening now by the power in the word He has spoken.

Everything is already inside — love, joy, peace, faith, hope, wisdom, power, anointing: already deposited. Like a millionaire begging on the streets, believers lack not the deposit but the knowledge of it — and knowledge comes only through the Word.

Living forever — born of an incorruptible, eternal seed, the believer partakes of its nature; the fruit of the Spirit is your nature, not an achievement to fight for.

The heart decides the harvest — different outcomes among believers have nothing to do with the Word or the Sower; they trace to the receptivity and preparation of the heart.

Summary 4 of 7

Main Teachings (II)

The Word is the best medicine — Billy Bimba held "I will live and not die" through repeated brushes with death; Dodie Osteen saturated her home with the Word through terminal cancer; Psalm 91 delivered Bishop Johnson and Pastor Chris in 1990.

"Whenever you have a need, that need is already met" — the 1977 college rhema, rooted in what Christ has already given (Ephesians 1:3), turning prayer from pleading into thanksgiving and declaration.

Make room — don't empower the seed — "The farmer does not give power to the seed; the seed is full of power. The farmer makes room for the seed to take root. That's all we're called to do."

The closed heart — the wayside: understanding refused or neglected — the atheist who would dismiss the risen Christ as a hallucination; believers closed to the gifts of the Spirit or to giving and receiving.

The casual heart — the stony soil: a shallow, once-a-week relationship with the Word puts down no roots, and trials topple shallow-rooted trees.

The crowded heart — the thorns: busyness, competing priorities, and the deceitfulness of riches (the $200 cable bill) leave the Word no room — especially in prayer and giving.

The cultivated heart — prepared by meekness: humble, teachable, surrendered — the key text (James 1:21) to be developed the following week.

Summary 5 of 7

Principles & Warnings

Principles — "The Word does not just inform — it creates." "The Word — it's where the power is." "Everything I need for life and godliness is already inside of me, in Christ Jesus." "The nature of the Word is your nature now." "Whenever you have a need, that need is already met." "The farmer doesn't give power to the seed; he makes room for it." "The way of escape is the Word." "The Word needs room." "God is not asking us to be stronger — He's asking us to be softer. Not weaker — meeker."

Warning: the closed mind — whether outright unbelief or a believer's refusal to examine truths like the gifts of the Spirit or giving and receiving, closed-mindedness shuts the Word out before it can take root.

Warning: deconstruction — many who deconstruct never constructed; questioning the authority of Scripture itself can land you "anywhere." Construct your faith: the Bible is the Word of God — Jesus Himself endorsed it.

Warning: the casual hearer — "Amen, hallelujah" with no meditation afterward leaves no roots; the storm will reveal it.

Warning: the crowded calendar — busyness with even good things pushes the Word to third, fourth, fifth place; neglect — not rejection — is how most believers lose the Word's power.

Warning: the deceitfulness of riches — competing financial priorities leave believers hearers but not doers in giving.

Summary 6 of 7

Takeaways & Declarations

Meditate daily — a once-a-week Bible is a casual relationship; spend time in the Word daily until it renews your mind.

Interrogate every sermon — ask: "What is this saying to me that should change the way I'm thinking or behaving?"

Audit your closed places — name the biblical truths you've closed your mind to, and pursue understanding instead of leaving them to "others."

Audit calendar and spending — the cable-bill test: what is crowding out the Word, prayer, and giving?

Make the Word priority one — not third, fourth, or fifth among good things competing for your time.

Receive with meekness — humble, teachable, surrendered; when the Word says forgive or give, answer "Okay, Lord, let's do it."

Declarations — "I refuse a closed mind. I refuse a casual attitude. I refuse a crowded calendar. Lord, I make room for the power of Your Word in my life." "When [your name] has a need, his need is met." "I am living forever." "I will succeed in doing God's will, fulfilling His purpose for my life, because of the power in the Word." And the refrain: "The Word works."

Watching Over My Word. So let me say this right now. You know, in Jeremiah — Jeremiah chapter one, I believe — God says to Jeremiah the prophet, "I am watching over My Word to perform it." I'm doing what? Watching over My Word. So when I read that, I said, wait a minute, Lord — the Word has the power. Why do You have to watch over it? If you're not careful, you might think what God is saying is, "Well, you know, just in case the Word doesn't do what it can, I'm going to make sure it happens, because I question whether the Word will always deliver." Now we know that's not the case, because God has said no Word is void of power and no Word I speak will return void. So God has absolute confidence that His Word will perform what He says. He doesn't have one iota of doubt concerning the power of the Word to create peace and joy and hope and wisdom, success, prosperity — whatever it is that you need for life and godliness.

And this is what I felt He showed me. He says, listen — when I say I will watch, it's not because I doubt My Word; it's because I want to give Jeremiah, in this case, another reason not to doubt. I want to give Jeremiah another reason to be absolutely certain that the Word I speak is going to come to pass. Because you've got to understand, Jeremiah was young. He was just starting, and like all young prophets, when you get started you have questions. So a young Jeremiah doesn't quite have the confidence yet to speak the Word of the Lord with certainty, and yet God wants him to do it. So God is saying, "I will watch over it," to give him another reason to be sure that what he's saying is going to come to pass. In essence, God is saying, don't worry, because I promise you, not only is the Word going to come to pass, but I'm also going to be involved in the process. So you can count on Me that this Word will come to pass.

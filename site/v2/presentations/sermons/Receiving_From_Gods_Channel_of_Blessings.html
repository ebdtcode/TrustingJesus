Receiving From God's Channel of Blessings

God's Word — the Immediate Source of Every Blessing

Bishop Darlingston Johnson • Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney

Luke 8:11 • James 1:18, 21 • June 28, 2026

Say It Again: The Word Works

"Now the parable is this: The seed is the word of God."

Luke 8:11 (NKJV)

The Word has the power to reproduce — thirty, sixty, a hundredfold — in our lives. That is a spiritual law working in the kingdom.

Not Like Seed — It Is Seed

A seed doesn't look like it holds a tree — yet one seed carries enough life, information, and power to produce a forest. The same Creator works by the same principle in His kingdom.

A Container and a Carrier

The Word carries divine life, divine power, divine grace, divine wisdom, divine peace, divine healing — and it reproduces after its kind: God's life and God's fruit in us.

God's Channel of Blessings

God — the Original Source

He wills, He speaks, and He places His life and power in the word.

His Word — the Immediate Source

Going forward, it is the word that delivers that power. Every spiritual blessing God designed for us, He delivers via His Word.

Say It With Me

"God's word carries everything necessary to accomplish the purpose for which God sent it."

God's purpose for your life will be fulfilled by the Word of God working in and through you.

It Cannot Not Be Fulfilled

"So shall My word be that goes forth from My mouth; it shall not return to Me void, but it shall accomplish what I please…"

Isaiah 55:10-11 (NKJV)

Once it leaves His mouth, it's too late to stop it. It goes with the power to produce whatever He has purposed — and with His instructions already inside.

Programmed Like DNA

Your DNA carried every instruction needed to make you who you are — the reason you look the way you look is that the information was already inside.

In the Word of God is all the information required for you to fulfill your purpose — and all the power necessary to bring it to pass.

Absolutely Essential

"Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of God."

Matthew 4:4 (NKJV)

Child of God, do not neglect the Word. Make it an absolute priority in your life — it is essential for your growth and the fulfillment of your purpose.

The Seed Is Sure

The Blessing Is in the Seed

God doesn't have to add anything more to His Word

The Seed Is Complete

In the parable, Jesus spends almost no time on the seed — because the seed is sure. God doesn't have to add power to His Word or adjust it. Everything the Word needs to do what it's meant to do is already in it.

"The blessing is in the seed. The miracle is in the seed. My future is in the seed."

God Doesn't Have to Speak Again

God could create new trees every morning — but He chose to put the power to recreate inside the seed. In the same way, He doesn't speak a new blessing, new healing, or new prosperity over you every day.

Spoken Once, Working Forever

"Let there be light" — the power was in the word, and God never had to say it again. "It is finished" — the blessing and the ability are already released, already in the Word.

The Seed and the Soil

  • Seeds release what's in them when planted in soil
  • Good seed in good soil → abundance
  • Good seed in bad soil → produces poorly
  • Our part: work with the Holy Spirit to make the soil good

All that needs to happen now is that we create the right environment for the Word to fulfill itself in our lives.

The Heart of the Matter

Four Soils, Four Relationships

Four ways we relate to the Word of God

Soil 1: The Closed Heart

Ground so hard the seed never enters. "Yeah, that's good stuff — but let's be real." Their own ideas and philosophies keep the Word out, and without understanding, the Word cannot be planted.

The Devil's First Goal

He comes immediately to steal the word — because he knows what it can do if it gets in. His surest tactic: keep you from understanding it. That's why God gives apostles, prophets, pastors, and teachers — to help His people get understanding.

Soil 2: The Casual Relationship

Stony ground: they hear, get excited, say "amen, hallelujah" — but never let the word take deep root. When afflictions and pressures come, they quickly throw away their confidence and go back to worrying.

Roots Grow by Meditation

Don't just take the information — take the revelation. Stay with the word, meditate on it, ask the Holy Spirit to open it further. If the word isn't renewing the way you think, it hasn't taken root yet.

Soil 3: The Crowded Heart

Thorns spring up and choke the word: the cares of this life and the deceitfulness of riches. They absorb the time, energy, and effort that belong to the Word — too busy to pray, too busy to read.

The Real Competition

"You cannot serve God and mammon." He didn't say God and the devil — the competition is money. Money is not bad; it becomes bad when it becomes an idol, pursued as if it will meet the needs only God has promised to supply according to His riches in glory.

Soil 4: The Good Heart

  • The mind is open — understanding can be grasped
  • The relationship is deep — not casual
  • The heart has room — the word is given priority

Into that heart, the word reproduces the life of God, the purposes of God, the plans of God, the power of God, the wisdom of God — everything God wants operating in your life becomes possible through the Word.

Say It Out Loud

"Lord, I reject a closed heart"

My heart is open to the truth.

"Lord, I reject a casual relationship with the Word"

My relationship with the Word will be deep.

"Lord, I reject a crowded heart"

I'm making room for Your Word to take root and grow.

No Substitute for the Word

  • Prayer without the Word will not suffice
  • Praise and worship without the Word will not suffice
  • Church attendance without the Word will not suffice
  • Giving without the Word will not suffice

"Your word I have hidden in my heart, that I might not sin against You."

Psalm 119:11 (NKJV)

James 1:18-21

Born by the Word

The word that saved your spirit can save the rest of you

Salvation Was God's Idea

"Of His own will He brought us forth by the word of truth, that we might be a kind of firstfruits of His creatures."

James 1:18 (NKJV)

God willed it, God wanted to give birth to us — and the means He used was the word of truth. The new creation is the product of the Word of God.

The Power Is in the Gospel

"…having been born again, not of corruptible seed but incorruptible, through the word of God which lives and abides forever."

1 Peter 1:23 (NKJV)

"I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ, for it is the power of God to salvation…"

Romans 1:16 (NKJV)

God put the power to produce new creatures inside the gospel. When you believed it, it reproduced you. He didn't have to speak again.

The Implanted Word

"Therefore lay aside all filthiness and overflow of wickedness, and receive with meekness the implanted word, which is able to save your souls."

James 1:21 (NKJV)

Wait — James is writing to born-again people (v.18). Why tell saved people to receive the word that is able to save them?

Salvation in Three Phases

My spirit IS saved

The new birth — already accomplished by the word of the gospel.

My soul IS BEING saved

Mind, will, and emotions — the same word is saving them now.

My body SHALL BE saved

The same word will heal it now and glorify it at the trumpet.

The Rest of Me Needs Saving

"I'm born again — but I've got some attitudes that need saving. Some emotions that need saving. Some fears I need to be delivered from. Some bitterness that needs to be cast out. Some pride that needs to be broken."

That's Okay — the Power Is Still in You

The word that produced the salvation of your spirit is still inside you, ready to save the rest of you. "Let your word, O God, save the rest of me — my soul, my will, my mind, my emotions."

Carrying Resurrection Power

"In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trumpet… the dead will be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed… this mortal must put on immortality."

1 Corinthians 15:52-53 (NKJV)

The power to produce your glorified body is already inside you — in the gospel — just waiting for God to say, "It's time."

The Chinese Bamboo Tree

Planted, it barely grows for about five years — then, within weeks, it shoots up into a massive tree. The life to produce that tree was inside the seed all along, waiting for its time to manifest.

This body may be getting old and tired — don't worry. Inside you is a seed that lives forever, and when the time comes: totally, completely transformed, just like Jesus.

The Word Is Already Planted

"Receive… the implanted word." Not a word you must plant — a word already planted. When? The day you got saved. By whom? The Holy Spirit. You don't need another word; you need to receive the one already in you.

Planted Is Not Yet Received

In IVF, a seed is planted — then everyone hopes it takes. The environment must receive what was planted before it produces results. The gospel that got you born again must be received so it can save your soul.

If the President Called You Tonight…

"I'm coming to visit you tomorrow." You wouldn't just plan to open the door — you'd prepare to receive him. Cleaning becomes urgent. Old furniture disappears. Anything that would dishonor him gets taken out of the way.

Receiving someone means giving them the honor their position deserves — and the Word deserves the honor of God speaking.

Receiving Means Honoring

  • Honor it — treat it as the word of God, not of man
  • Respect it — give it first place
  • Submit to it — yield when it speaks
  • Clean house — take out anything that dishonors Him

Now that the word is in you, start cleaning house. When you make room, you allow the Word to do what it came to do.

A Command That Empowers

"Lay aside all filthiness" — like finally casting off clothes you've worn too long: I'm tired of smelling this way; take it off. But when God tells you to do something, He's not telling you to do it alone.

Don't See a Command — See an Enablement

God said "Let there be light" — and there was light. God says "Lay aside all filthiness" — and that word contains the power to give you the desire and the ability to lay it aside. He's empowering you.

When the Attitude Poisons the Soil

For some of us it's not behavior — it's the inward attitude that makes the soil toxic, acidic: constantly complaining, constantly criticizing, constantly angry, constantly bitter.

Nothing wrong with the seed — same seed. Nothing wrong with the sower — same sower, the Holy Ghost. These things don't diminish the power of the Word; they diminish the quality of the soil — and so they limit what the Word can do.

The Word Trumps Your Feelings

Never say, "The Bible says this, but this is how I feel." That is to dishonor the word.

Say instead: "I feel this way, but the Bible says this." The word of God has the final say — every time.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

Receiving From God's Channel of Blessings — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, scripture, and takeaways

Overview

Anchor texts — Luke 8:11 ("the seed is the word of God") and James 1:18-21 (the implanted word able to save your souls), continuing the Word Works series on the Parable of the Sower.

Setting — Bishop Darlingston Johnson at Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney, June 28, 2026. Part 2 of the series: the soils — and how to receive what God has already planted.

Big idea — God is the original source of every blessing, but His Word is the immediate source — His channel. The blessing is already in the seed: God doesn't have to speak again. What determines the harvest is the soil — our relationship with the Word.

Aim — Move from a closed, casual, or crowded heart to a good heart that honors and receives the implanted word — so it can save the soul, heal the body, and deliver every blessing God has purposed.

Summary 1 of 7

Key Scriptures (I)

Luke 8:11 — "The seed is the word of God." The Word is not like seed; it is seed — a container and carrier of divine life and power that reproduces thirty, sixty, a hundredfold.

Isaiah 55:10-11 — The word that goes out of God's mouth never returns void; its instructions are programmed inside it like DNA. "God's word cannot not be fulfilled."

Matthew 4:4 — "Man shall not live by bread alone." The Word is absolutely essential — make it an absolute priority.

Psalm 119:11 — "Thy word have I hid in my heart" — nothing substitutes for the Word: not prayer, praise, attendance, or giving.

Matthew 6:24 / Mark 8:36 — You cannot serve God and mammon; you can gain the whole world and lose your soul. The real competition with God is money, not the devil.

Philippians 4:19 — God supplies all our needs according to His riches in glory — the antidote to the deceitfulness of riches.

Summary 2 of 7

Key Scriptures (II)

James 1:18 — "Of His own will He brought us forth by the word of truth." Salvation was God's idea, accomplished through the Word.

1 Peter 1:23 — Born again of incorruptible seed, the word of God that lives and abides forever.

Romans 1:16 — The gospel is the power of God unto salvation — the power to produce new creatures is inside the word itself.

James 1:21 — "Lay aside all filthiness… and receive with meekness the implanted word, which is able to save your souls." Written to people already born again.

1 Corinthians 15:52-53 / 1 Thessalonians 4:16 — At the trumpet, in the twinkling of an eye, this mortal puts on immortality — resurrection power already carried within.

Genesis 1:3 / John 19:30 — "Let there be light" and "It is finished": God speaks once, and the power stays in the word forever — He never has to say it again.

Summary 3 of 7

Main Teachings (I)

The Word is God's channel — God is the original source; the Word is the immediate source. Every spiritual blessing is delivered via His Word.

Everything necessary is inside — God's word carries everything necessary to accomplish the purpose for which God sent it — life, power, grace, wisdom, peace, healing.

The blessing is in the seed — the seed is complete; God doesn't have to add anything, adjust anything, or speak again. As with trees, He put the power to recreate inside the seed.

Four soils = four relationships — closed (the word never enters; the devil steals it by blocking understanding), casual (no roots; collapses under trials), crowded (cares and the deceitfulness of riches choke it), and good (open, deep, prioritized — everything becomes possible).

Understanding is the entry point — without understanding the word cannot be planted; that is why God gave apostles, prophets, pastors, and teachers.

Summary 4 of 7

Main Teachings (II)

Salvation in three phases — my spirit is saved (new birth, accomplished); my soul is being saved (mind, will, emotions); my body shall be saved (healing now, glorified body at the trumpet). The same word does all three.

The word is already planted — the Holy Spirit implanted it the day you were saved. You don't need another word — you need to receive the one already in you (the IVF picture: planted must also be received).

Receiving means honoring — like preparing the house for a presidential visit: clean house, remove what dishonors Him, give the Word first place, submit to it.

Commands are enablements — "lay aside all filthiness" carries the power to obey it, just as "let there be light" carried the power to create light.

Attitudes poison soil — complaining, criticizing, anger, and bitterness make the soil toxic. They never weaken the Word — they diminish the quality of the soil.

The Chinese bamboo tree — years of no visible growth, then a massive tree in weeks: the life was in the seed all along, waiting for its time — like the resurrection power already in you.

Summary 5 of 7

Principles & Warnings

Principles — "God is the original source; His Word is the immediate source." "The blessing is in the seed; the miracle is in the seed; my future is in the seed." "God's word cannot not be fulfilled." "Prayer, praise, attendance, and giving without the Word will not suffice." "The word trumps your feelings every time." "Thorns don't diminish the Word — they diminish the soil."

Warning: the closed heart — when understanding is blocked, the devil steals the word immediately; his first goal is to keep it from ever getting in.

Warning: the casual hearer — a Sunday "amen" without meditation puts down no roots, and trials will uproot your confidence.

Warning: the crowded heart — the cares of this life and the deceitfulness of riches absorb the time and energy the Word deserves; busyness that kills prayer chokes the seed.

Warning: mammon, not the devil — Jesus named money, not Satan, as God's rival for your service. Money is not bad — until it becomes an idol.

Warning: never dishonor the word — never say "the Bible says this, but this is how I feel" or "let's get real." That is not receiving the word.

Summary 6 of 7

Takeaways & Declarations

Seek understanding — with the Holy Spirit's help and through the teachers God has given; never let a lack of understanding become permanent.

Meditate until the mind is renewed — stay with a word past Sunday; if it isn't changing how you think, it hasn't taken root yet.

Declutter the heart — identify and remove what crowds out the Word: worry, busyness, and the pursuit of riches.

Honor and receive the word — clean house as if the President were coming; give the Word the honor God speaking deserves.

Lay aside by grace — treat "lay aside all filthiness" as divine enablement; cast off what shouldn't be worn, trusting the power in the word.

Guard the attitude — in everything give thanks; refuse the complaining and bitterness that acidify the soil.

Declarations — "Lord, I reject a closed heart; my heart is open to the truth. I reject a casual relationship; my relationship with the Word will be deep. I reject a crowded heart; I'm making room for the Word." And standing: "Lord, You have put inside Your word the power, the desire, and the ability for me to lay aside filthiness and wickedness… I honor the word. I accept the word. I submit to the word. I yield to the word, in Jesus' name."

The Word Works. Say to your neighbor, the word works. One more time, the word works. Amen. We're looking at that theme based upon Jesus' teaching of the sower that went out to sow. He interpreted that for us and said the word is the seed, and that word has the power, according to that teaching, to reproduce and continue to reproduce thirty, sixty-fold and a hundred-fold in our lives. That is a spiritual law working in the kingdom, and so we need to understand the Word of God from this perspective — the Word of God is not like seed; the Word of the Lord is seed.

We can learn so much about seeds from natural observation. Seeds do not look like they have a tree in them. They don't look like they can produce a forest — but we know from experience that in one seed there's enough life, information, and power to literally produce a forest. What Jesus is teaching is that this principle we know and trust in nature is also the principle by which God works in his kingdom. When he wants to bless us, empower us, reproduce things, God uses — in the spirit realm — the same principle of seeds. The difference is that in the natural you've got physical seeds, but in the kingdom of God, Jesus said the word is the seed.

Transcript 1 of 14

Container and Carrier. That means the word is a container like seeds; the word is a carrier of life, a carrier of power like the seeds we know. And the word has the ability to produce and reproduce after its kind. If God has placed his life and his power into the word, then that word is going to reproduce after its kind — which means it's going to reproduce God, God's life, and God's fruit in our lives. Every spiritual blessing that God has designed for us to have, he delivers to us via his word. So God is the source — but you could say God is the original source, and the word of God is the immediate source. What God does is he wills something, he speaks, and he places his life and power in the word — and going forward, it is the word that delivers that power.

The same creator, the same system, the same pattern we see working in nature, we are also experiencing in the kingdom of God. The word is a container, a carrier — it carries divine life, divine power, divine grace, divine wisdom, divine peace, divine healing. It carries everything necessary to accomplish the purpose for which God sent it. Say it: God's word carries everything necessary to accomplish the purpose for which God sent it. So God's purpose for your life will be fulfilled by the word of God working in and through you.

Transcript 2 of 14

Never Returns Void. We talked about compression — how God compresses omnipotence and omniscience into his word. Every plan God has for us is going to be worked out in our lives through the word. So we absolutely need the word. Say: thank God that the word of God contains the life of God, the power of God, to produce and reproduce the plan of God, the promises of God, the purposes of God for my life.

God's word never returns void. He said it in Isaiah 55:10-11 — "so shall my word be that goes out of my mouth; it shall never return void." Say: God's word cannot not be fulfilled. Once it goes out of his mouth, it's gone — it's too late. If you wanted to stop that word, you should have kept him from speaking. Once it goes out of his mouth, it goes with the power and the ability to produce whatever he has purposed, and it has the instructions God wants it to follow already programmed inside.

Just like biology — DNA — everything programmed already. The reason I look the way I look is that my DNA had all the instructions for how I'm supposed to look. And the reason you look so beautiful, so handsome, is that your DNA contained the information. So in the word of God is all the information required for you to fulfill your purpose, and the power necessary to bring it to pass.

Transcript 3 of 14

Absolutely Essential. This is why Jesus said, "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by" what? We need the word. The word is absolutely essential for our spiritual growth and development, for the fulfillment of our purpose. So child of God, do not neglect the word of God. Make the word of God an absolute priority in your life.

The Seed Is Complete. Going back to Jesus' parable: Jesus says the seed is the word, and he doesn't spend much time talking about the seed. He spends time talking about the soil, because the word is sure. God doesn't have to add any more power to his word. God doesn't have to adjust his word. Everything that needs to be in the word to do what the word is supposed to do is already in the word. Say: the blessing is in the seed. The miracle is in the seed. My future is in the seed. God doesn't have to add anything more to his word. It's complete.

Seeds have the power, but seeds are designed by God to release what's in them when they're planted in the soil. There's a connection and a relationship between the seed and the soil. Good seed in good soil is going to produce abundance. Good seed in bad soil is going to produce poorly. So what Jesus is concerned about in the parable is that we work with him — with the Holy Spirit — to make sure the soil into which the word is planted is good soil.

Transcript 4 of 14

Four Soils — the Closed Heart. There are four different types of soils he describes, which really describe four different types of relationships we can have with the word. The first is soil so hard that the seed never really enters it — like taking good seed and throwing it on the floor; the ground is closed, so the seed will never get in and never release its ability. That describes the relationship some people have with the word. They're just closed to it. They've got their own ideas, their own philosophies. "Yeah, that's good stuff, but let's be real — that's not real life." So they're closed to the word, and the word cannot take root in those lives.

When people are closed to the word, they don't make enough effort to even understand it. Right now you may be here and your mind is somewhere else — the word is being sown, but your mind is closed. Without understanding, the word cannot be planted. Jesus says when people have this closed-minded attitude, so that they don't even get understanding, the devil immediately comes and steals it. The devil understands: if the word gets into you, he knows what it can do. His first goal is to prevent it from getting inside of you at all — and the surest way is to make sure you don't understand it.

So make it your goal, with the help of the Holy Spirit, to understand the word. That's one of the reasons God gives apostles, prophets, pastors, teachers — to help his people get understanding. I thank God for the teachers and pastors I have listened to in my life; they have helped me tremendously. So don't be closed-minded.

Transcript 5 of 14

The Casual Relationship. Then some people's relationship with the word is described this way: the sower sowed the word, and the seed fell on stony ground. Because the ground was stony, the seeds never became deeply rooted — and when circumstances changed and there were afflictions and pressures, quickly those persons gave up, turned away from the word, and went back to whatever they were doing before.

These people hear the word and they're excited. They understand it a little, but they don't make the effort to see that the word becomes deeply rooted in them so it can really produce fruit — so they quickly let go of it. That describes what happens many times on Sundays: you hear a word, you say amen, hallelujah, and that word genuinely blesses you — but then you don't take the steps to make sure it gets rooted in you, so that when tests and trials come you are not quick to throw away your confidence in the word and go back to worrying.

What you want to do when the word is preached and there's understanding: get deeper understanding. Stay with that word until it starts to actually renew your mind. If the word isn't renewing the way you think about something, it hasn't taken root. Don't just take the information — take the revelation, spend time meditating on that word, ask the Holy Spirit to open it up more, listen to it again. You're trying to make sure the word goes deep, so that when trials come, you don't throw away your confidence — because your mind has been renewed with that word. Say to your neighbor: don't have merely a casual relationship with the word. Let your relationship with the word be deep, because temptations, trials, and tests will challenge that truth in your life — and you need to be able to stand on it no matter how much pressure you're feeling.

Transcript 6 of 14

The Crowded Heart. Then Jesus said there's a third group: they hear the word, they have some understanding, they get excited, and they last a little longer — but then the thorns spring up and choke the word. The thorns represent the cares of this life. How many of you have cares? And the cares are many. We have concerns, things we worry about — though we shouldn't worry. He says the cares of this life, and then the deceitfulness of riches.

At some point somebody says, "Yeah, but what I really need is some money. If I can just get money, I won't have to pray for a lot of the things I'm praying for — money will take care of that." Riches can deceive you into thinking you really don't need the word, you just need more money — and if you get enough money, you'll be happy. Certainly money has its place, but money is not God. You can gain the whole world and lose your soul. You can have all the money in the world and still commit suicide — feel so unfulfilled, so unloved, depressed.

So don't let money, or the things money can buy, deceive you into thinking that what you should be living for and pursuing with all your might is money. The Bible says you can't serve God and mammon. He didn't say you can't serve God and the devil — because really, the competition is not the devil. The competition is money. And money is not bad in itself, but it becomes bad when it becomes an idol and replaces God — when we pursue it as though it will meet our needs, rather than the God who promises to supply all our needs according to his riches in glory.

Transcript 7 of 14

You can allow the desire for riches — the Bible says "the desire for other things" — to become so important in your life that it crowds your heart to such a degree that the word of God no longer has a place there. The thorns absorb the nutrients and the moisture that the seed needs. These things simply absorb the time, the energy, the effort that we should be putting into hearing, responding to, and obeying the word of God. You can get so busy you can't pray. You can become so busy you no longer have time to read the word — you know you're supposed to, but you're so busy: work, work, work, and you're tired like I don't know what. Jesus says be careful, because those things will literally cause your heart to become crowded — and a crowded heart doesn't have room for the seed to grow, bloom, and become all it has the potential to produce in your life.

Three Rejections. So say: Lord, I reject a closed heart — my heart is open to the truth. Say: Lord, I reject a casual relationship with the word — my relationship with the word will be deep. Say: Lord, I reject a crowded heart — I'm going to make room in my life for your word to truly take root and grow. The things in your life that limit what the word can do are the things you and I should be removing.

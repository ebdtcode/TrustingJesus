Honoring the Women

On Mother's Day at HIC, We Celebrate Women

Physical mothers, spiritual mothers, wives, aunts, sisters, daughters — we honor you. The role you play is so critical: if you were not here, we would have to close the doors of the church and restart.

"Ministry here depends so much upon the faithfulness of our female members."

The Anointing at Bethany

"A woman came in with a beautiful jar of expensive perfume made from essence of nard. She broke open the jar and poured the perfume over his head."

Mark 14:3 (NLT)

An expression of love. An expression of worship. An expression of gratitude — broken open and poured out at the end of Jesus' life.

"Why This Waste?"

"Some of those at the table were indignant. 'Why waste such expensive perfume?' they asked."

Mark 14:4 (NLT)

They considered her act unwise, foolish, wasteful — and they criticized her publicly.

"Leave Her Alone"

"But Jesus replied, 'Leave her alone. Why criticize her for doing such a good thing to me?'"

Mark 14:6 (NLT)

Women, Hear This

People can be critical of you — but Jesus comes to your defense. Jesus stands up for you. Jesus highly values the role you play in the kingdom.

The Verdict of Heaven

"She has done what she could."

Mark 14:8a (NKJV)

"She did what she could."

The common theme that runs through every woman in this story.

Context

The First-Century View

"Lord, I thank You that I was not born a woman."

How Society Saw Women

  • A common rabbinic prayer: "Lord, I thank You I was not born a Gentile or a woman."
  • Josephus: the testimony of a woman was legally inadmissible.
  • Even multiple women saying the same thing — not taken seriously.

Yet… God disagreed.

Heaven's Counter-Witness

The Holy Spirit placed these stories in scripture to repudiate the lie that women are lesser. At every stage of Jesus' life, women feature prominently.

At His Birth — Mary, Anna, Elizabeth
In His Ministry — Mary Magdalene, Joanna, Susanna, many more
At His Cross & Tomb — Mary, Mary Magdalene, Salome, & many more
Lesson 1 of 3

Surrender to His Will

Mary, the Mother of Jesus

"Behold the bondservant of the Lord."

A Teenager's Assignment

The Weight of Her Calling

Mary was probably 13 or 14 — a young teenager. Yet she was the one chosen to carry the Son of God, give birth to Him, and raise Him.

"There are some things in the kingdom only a woman can do."

What She Risked

In her culture, an unmarried pregnancy meant:

  • Public ridicule
  • Shame and rejection
  • Possibly even death by stoning

The angel did not ask her opinion. He just announced.

Her Response

"Behold the maidservant of the Lord! Let it be to me according to your word."

Luke 1:38 (NKJV)

"If that's what You want — yes sir. I am Your servant. I exist for one purpose: to do Your will."

The Word She Used

δουλη (doulē)

Translated "bondservant" — not just any servant. A bond-servant. A servant-slave. A born servant.

"I am Your born servant."

Servant or Employee?

Employee mentality

"I work for God for certain hours. Don't ask more than that."

Bond-servant mentality

"I have already given up my right to any other choice. Whatever You ask — yes, Lord."

How Do You Self-Identify?

"The day you confessed Jesus as Lord — that is what you should have done. Surrendered your will to His will once and for all."

— Bishop Darlingston Johnson

How you self-identify will influence how you respond to the will of the Lord.

The Risk of Following Jesus

Chuza managed Herod's palace — a high government official. Joanna had money, social standing, access to power. She put it all at the disposal of Jesus.

Herod killed John the Baptist. By supporting Jesus, Joanna risked Herod's displeasure and her husband's job — but she stayed faithful.

Lesson 3 of 3

Stay Available

The Women at the Cross

Just in case…

The Question of the Dark Day

When there are no more miracles. No signs. No wonders. Only the cross, darkness, weakness.

Who Will Stay?

Who will remain when exciting things are not happening? When dark days come? When there is trouble and pain? Who will stay where they should be? Who will stay close? Who will stay near? Who will stay just in case?

Where Were the Men?

  • John was there.
  • Peter followed at a distance — then left.
  • The other disciples were gone.

Perhaps because their lives were in greater danger. Perhaps from fear. But the men were gone.

The Paradox of Self-Preservation

"Whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for Me will find it."

Matthew 16:25 (NIV)

The men who fled to save their lives still lost them later. John, who was prepared to lose his life by staying, ended up keeping it — they tried, they tried, they tried, but they could not kill him.

Love When You Are Powerless

Sometimes the greatest way you show your love and loyalty is when you are powerless — as far as you know — to do anything that can change the circumstances. Yet because of love, because of gratitude, you stay nevertheless.

Stay close. Stay near. Stay available. Just in case.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

She Did What She Could — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, scripture, and takeaways

Overview

Anchor text — Mark 14:3-9. The unnamed woman breaks the alabaster jar and pours costly perfume on Jesus. The disciples call it waste; Jesus calls it worship: "She has done what she could."

Mother's Day frame — HIC honors physical and spiritual mothers. Bishop Johnson uses "she did what she could" as the common theme that links the women across Jesus' life: at His birth, in His ministry, and at His cross.

Big idea — God is not asking you to do everything. He is asking you to do everything you can, and to make doing what you can a way of life.

Three lessons — from Mary (Surrender), from the women in Luke 8 (Support), from the women at the cross (Stay).

Summary 1 of 6

Key Scriptures

Mark 14:3-9 — The anointing at Bethany. "She has done what she could." Anchor text for the whole message.

Luke 1:38 — Mary's response: "Behold the maidservant of the Lord; let it be to me according to your word." The pattern of surrender; the word she used is doulē — bond-servant.

Luke 8:1-3 — "Mary called Magdalene… Joanna the wife of Chuza… Susanna… provided for Him from their substance." The women financially backed the ministry.

The cross / tomb narratives — Mary the mother, Mary Magdalene, Joanna, Salome, "and many more" stayed near while the male disciples (except John) fled.

Matthew 16:25 (referenced) — "Whoever wants to save his life will lose it." Cited via the contrast between the men who fled and John who stayed.

Summary 2 of 6

Main Teachings

Jesus defends the women in His life — "Leave her alone." When society or the church criticizes, Jesus stands up for the women who serve Him.

Lesson 1 — Surrender (Mary) — A 13-or-14-year-old girl, facing public ridicule and possible death, calls herself "the bond-servant of the Lord." The day you confessed Jesus as Lord, you should have surrendered your will once and for all.

Lesson 2 — Support (Luke 8 women) — They were not bystanders. Mary Magdalene had been totally lost; Joanna risked her husband's job in Herod's palace. They funded the ministry from their substance.

Lesson 3 — Stay (women at the cross) — When there were no miracles, no signs, no wonders — just the cross — the women stayed close, near, available. The men, except John, fled.

The common theme — They could not do everything. But they always did everything they could. That is what got heaven's attention.

Summary 3 of 6

Principles

There are some things in the kingdom only a woman can do — God reserved the bringing of Christ into the world for a woman.

How you self-identify shapes how you respond — Bond-servant ≠ employee. The bond-servant has already given up the right to bargain.

Ministry requires funding — The gospel travels from town to town on real money.

Generosity is worship — Giving is not transaction; it is offering.

The kingdom advances through partnership — Some go; others support.

Love is most visible when you are powerless — Staying when you cannot fix anything is one of the highest expressions of love.

Awareness fuels devotion — The more you know what Christ has done for you, the more motivated you are to stay.

Summary 4 of 6

Warnings & Cautions

Do not celebrate sin while celebrating the child — Pregnancy out of wedlock is fornication. Always celebrate the child — never make a child feel inferior — but Christians cannot celebrate sin. Repent of fornication when convicted.

Do not mistake the employee posture for servanthood — If your relationship with God is "I work eight hours and get paid," you have misunderstood the call.

Tax-payer giving is not partner giving — If you give only because you're "supposed to," you are not personally invested in His work.

A faith that does not affect your giving may not be a real faith — A direct, sobering challenge.

Watch the self-preservation reflex — The men who fled to save their lives still lost them. John, who stayed, kept his.

Backsliding is normal — staying backslid is not — Renew the commitment every time you notice you have drifted.

Summary 5 of 6

Practical Takeaways

Re-confess Jesus as Lord today — And let it carry the full weight of "I am Your bond-servant."

Move from consumer to partner — Decide that ministry where you are fed has a financial backbone — and you are part of it.

Practice "just-in-case" presence — Be near to people in their dark days even when you cannot fix anything. Stay close. Stay near. Stay available.

End-of-day examination — Each night before sleep, ask: "Did I do everything I could today to honor Christ, support His work, do His will, stay available?"

Renew, don't despair — If you have backslid — from the surrender, from the giving, from the staying — renew. Today.

Honor the women in your life — Men, encourage rather than limit. See yourself in these women too — the lessons are not gendered.

Live for the final conversation — "Lord, I didn't do everything — but I did everything I could." And let Jesus say: "Yes. Amen."

Doulē — the bond-servant. She self-identified as the Lord's servant. Women, that's your example. How do you self-identify? Because how you self-identify will influence how you respond to the will of the Lord. Men, this is also for our example.

The word that Mary uses there is doulē. It's a word that is translated bond-servant — it's not just any kind of servant, it's a reference to a bond-servant. Mary said, "I am Your bond-servant," which really means, "I am Your servant-slave."

That's far different from the mentality many of us carry today when we say we are servants of God. Many of us see that as pretty much being employees. "I work for God for certain hours" — like you say, "I'm a servant of this company." In your mind, "I work for eight hours and you pay me." If the boss begins to ask you for much more than that, your response might be: "I work for this company, but I'm not your slave. You don't pay me enough to demand that I work all of these hours and weekends. And if you continue, you can take your job."

That's for your employer. That's not for God. Because when it comes to our relationship with God, we ought to learn from Mary that as Christians we are servant-slaves. And when you are a bond-servant, there's only one appropriate response when the Master lets you know His will — surrender to His will, and say, "Let it be so. By the way, the day you confessed Jesus as Lord — whenever you did — that is what you should have done: surrendered your will to His will once and for all.

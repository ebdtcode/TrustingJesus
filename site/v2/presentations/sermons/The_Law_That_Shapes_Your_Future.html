Kingdom Laws

The Law That Shapes Your Future

Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Galatians 6:6–10 · The Law of Sowing & Reaping

Harvest Intercontinental Church, Olney · May 17, 2026

You Can Influence Your Future

Not totally. Not in everything. But to a large degree, your future is in your hand.

There is a law

A law that can shape your future. We need to understand that law, cooperate with it, and make it work for us, not against us.

The Big Idea

"Do not be deceived. God is not mocked, for whatever a man sows, that he will also reap."

Galatians 6:7 (NKJV)

In the future, you will reap what you sow in the present. That is the law.

Part 1

The Foundation in Grace

Before the law of harvest, the gift of righteousness

The Moral Law Condemned Us

  • Thou shalt not steal, kill, commit adultery — the Ten Commandments
  • Every person has broken it — "All have sinned and fall short of the glory of God"
  • The penalty was clear — condemnation, judgment, eternal separation
  • Without the shedding of blood — there could be no remission of sin

Jesus Satisfied the Law

"It is finished."

John 19:30

He didn't soften it — He fulfilled it

Jesus took your place. Received the condemnation. Received the judgment. Received the sentence of death. The law was fully satisfied in our place.

From Minus 100 to Plus 100

Minus 100

Where the moral law left every one of us — condemned, judged, sentenced.

Zero

Jesus didn't just clear the debt and leave you neutral.

Plus 100

He clothed you in His own righteousness — treated as one who has never sinned and one who has perfectly obeyed.

I Am the Righteousness of God in Christ

"For He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him."

2 Corinthians 5:21 (NKJV)

Your job now is to renew your mind until you see yourself the way God sees you.

Illustration

The White Clothes

In dirty jeans, you sit on a dusty chair without thinking. In immaculate white, every surface matters. When you truly see yourself clothed in Christ's righteousness, sin loses its appeal — not by guilt, but by identity.

Illustration

The Daughter & the Beetle

A father is not a policeman

God Deals With You As Father, Not As Judge

  • She drove 50 in a 35 — the law broken
  • Daddy did not chase her or fine her — he was not her judge
  • He waited at home and had a conversation — love does discipline differently
  • He took the keys, not the relationship — "Until you grow up — not until you stop being mine"

Her status didn't change. His love didn't change. She did not become less.

Part 2

The Laws That Still Govern

The cross dealt with the moral law — not the laws of creation

Three Laws That Still Work

Gravity

It holds you to the earth. Step off a skyscraper — no sin, no judgment, just consequence. The law works the same for saint and sinner.

Newton's Third Law

For every action, an equal and opposite reaction. Studied precisely enough, it sends a spacecraft to the moon and brings it home to the exact landing spot.

The Parachute's 4 Seconds

800 feet, four seconds to pull. The paratrooper rehearses the steps before every jump — not because the law might fail, but because it never does.

Cooperation Produces Exploits

When you understand a law well enough to cooperate with it precisely, you can do extraordinary things. Cars. Airplanes. Telephones across the ocean. Spacecraft to the moon.

Salvation does not erase them — alignment with them benefits you

And the same is true of the laws that operate in the realm of the spirit.

Part 3

The Law of Sowing & Reaping

Both natural and spiritual

A Creation Ordinance

"While the earth remains, seedtime and harvest, cold and heat, winter and summer, day and night, shall not cease."

Genesis 8:22 (NKJV)

The earth still remains. The law is still in effect — in every realm.

Jesus — Power Is in the Seed

"The kingdom of God is as if a man should scatter seed on the ground, and should sleep by night and rise by day, and the seed should sprout and grow, he himself does not know how."

Mark 4:26–27 (NKJV)

God placed the power inside the seed. The farmer's job is to plant — not to produce the growth.

Every Harvest Begins With a Seed

  • The farmer doesn't pray for corn in an empty field — he plants corn first
  • He invests time, he invests money — because he trusts the law
  • He waters, he waits — and in due season, the harvest comes
  • Jesus said the same is true of the kingdom — the law operates identically (Mark 4)

Prayer Is Not a Substitute for Sowing

Stand in an empty field. Pray loudly. Add fasting. Declare a hundredfold harvest in the name of Jesus. You will wait forever.

The order matters

Sow the seed God has given you. Then pray — ask grace to multiply what your hands have already planted.

Zero × Grace = Zero

Zero × a hundred = 0

Zero × a thousand = 0

Zero × a million = 0

No matter how great and powerful the grace of God is, if what you give is zero — God can pull out all the grace there is — if that grace comes upon zero, it will remain zero.

Jesus Required Seed to Feed 5,000

The Son of God did not declare bread into existence from nothing. He began the miracle by asking: "What do you have?" He took the five loaves. He gave thanks — that word for thanksgiving is also the word for grace. He released grace upon the seed. The seed multiplied.

Grace multiplies the seed that is sown — not the seed kept in your hand.

God Supplies Seed to the Sower

"Now may He who supplies seed to the sower, and bread for food, supply and multiply the seed you have sown."

2 Corinthians 9:10 (NKJV)

He gives you bread for today — and seed for your future. Don't consume the seed.

Part 4

Applying the Law

Paul in Galatians 6:6–10

v6 — Bless Those Who Teach You

"Let him who is taught the word share in all good things with him who teaches."

Galatians 6:6

Be a blessing — physically, financially, otherwise — to those who take care of your soul. That is a seed. The law guarantees the return.

v7–8 — The Choice of Seed

Sow to the flesh

Living for yourself. Withholding from the kingdom. Refusing to give, serve, support — reap corruption: thorns and thistles, the things that steal, kill, destroy.

Sow to the Spirit

Give. Serve. Support. Bless. Build — reap everlasting life: the benefits of eternity, beginning in this life and continuing into the next.

v10 — The Household of Faith

"Therefore, as we have opportunity, let us do good to all, especially to those who are of the household of faith."

Galatians 6:10

The widest field is anyone you can reach. The most fertile ground is fellow believers.

Part 5

Don't Grow Weary

Due season is coming

v9 — The Warning Against Weariness

"And let us not grow weary while doing good, for in due season we shall reap if we do not lose heart."

Galatians 6:9

Different seeds. Different due seasons. The temptation to quit just before harvest is real — and costly.

Personal Story

The Rubber Farm

I invested money. Year one. Year two. Year three. Year four. Year five — nothing. When the harvest finally began, it was so small I said, "You mean I invested all this for that?" I became weary. I was about to walk away.

Thank God for the gentleman who said: "Bishop, don't do that. Give it one more year."

Weariness Is the Only Way to Lose It

  • The law is reliable — what you have sown will produce
  • Due season varies — some quickly, some after years
  • God surrounds you with encouragers — listen to the voice that says one more year
  • The harvest spans this life and the next — what you don't see now, you'll see in eternity
Part 6 · The Master Pattern

The Cross

The greatest application of sowing and reaping in history

God Wanted Many Sons

God had one Son. He desired many sons just like the first — brothers and sisters of Jesus.

He did not just declare it

To get a harvest of sons, the first Son had to become a seed. God Himself respected the law He established.

Jesus the Seed

"Most assuredly, I say to you, unless a grain of wheat falls into the ground and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it produces much grain."

John 12:24 (NKJV)

Jesus understood. He was speaking about Himself. If He refused to be planted — He remains alone. If He becomes the seed — many grain.

You Are the Harvest

The cross was not a tragedy — it was God applying the law of seedtime and harvest. The Father planted the Son. You and I are the harvest of that planted Seed.

Say it: "I am the harvest of the seed God planted."

The DNA of the Seed

Love — not a struggle
Peace — not a pursuit
Joy — not an achievement
Hope — not an aspiration

"Love is now in your DNA." — Seeds produce after their kind.

Prayer Points

Pray with Me

Receive the gift. Sow the seed. Don't grow weary.

Renewed Identity & Righteousness

  • Receive the gift of righteousness — not from minus to zero, but from minus to plus 100 (2 Cor. 5:21 / Romans 8:1)
  • Renew the mind to true identity — move the truth from head to heart (Romans 12:2)
  • Freedom from condemnation and spiritual exhaustion — His yoke is easy, His burden light (Matthew 11:30)
  • Righteousness as identity, not striving — dressed in immaculate white, attitudes change (Galatians 2:20)

Sowing & Stewardship

  • Choose the Spirit over the flesh in every seed — time, money, words, service (Galatians 6:7–8)
  • Bless spiritual teachers and the household of faith — this is fertile ground (Galatians 6:6, 10)
  • Refuse to confuse prayer with sowing — sow first, then pray for grace to multiply (2 Cor. 9:10 / Mark 4)
  • Trust God as the supplier of seed — bread for today, seed for tomorrow (2 Cor. 9:10)

Patience & Harvest

  • Perseverance through due season — the law is at work even when ground looks empty (Galatians 6:9 / Mark 4:26–29)
  • Faith of a farmer against weariness — settled confidence year after year (Galatians 6:9 / James 1:3–4)
  • Receive identity as the harvest of the planted Seed — love, joy, peace, hope are DNA (John 12:24 / Galatians 4:6–7)
  • Declare the law at work in this life and the next — benefits of everlasting life (Galatians 6:8–9 / 1 Tim. 4:8)

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

The Law That Shapes Your Future — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview

You can influence your future. Not totally, but to a large degree it is in your hand — because there is a law that shapes it: the law of sowing and reaping.

Before arriving at the law, Bishop lays a grace foundation. The moral law condemned every one of us. Jesus satisfied that law completely — received our condemnation, judgment, and death — and clothed believers in His own righteousness. The Father no longer relates to His children as a judge with a ticket, but as a Father who loves His daughter too much to let her wreck the car He gave her. He may take the keys; He never takes your status.

With grace secured, the sermon pivots: the cross did not cancel natural and spiritual laws that govern how creation works. Gravity still operates. Newton's third law still operates. The law of sowing and reaping — Genesis 8:22, Mark 4, Galatians 6 — still operates with the same reliability in the kingdom of God as on a rubber farm in Liberia.

Summary 1 of 7

Key Scriptures

Galatians 6:6 — "Let him who is taught the word share in all good things with him who teaches." The first practical seed: bless those who pour into you spiritually.

Galatians 6:7 — "Do not be deceived. God is not mocked, for whatever a man sows, that he will also reap." The anchor verse — the law stated plainly.

Galatians 6:8 — "He who sows to his flesh will of the flesh reap corruption, and he who sows to the Spirit will of the Spirit reap everlasting life." The choice embedded in the law.

Galatians 6:9 — "Let us not grow weary while doing good, for in due season we shall reap if we do not lose heart." The exhortation behind the rubber-farm story.

Galatians 6:10 — "Do good to all, especially to those who are of the household of faith." The widest field, most fertile ground.

Genesis 8:22 — "While the earth remains, seedtime and harvest...shall not cease." The law is a creation ordinance.

Mark 4 (parable of the growing seed) — God placed the power inside the seed; the farmer's job is to plant and wait.

John 12:24 — "Unless a grain of wheat falls into the ground and dies, it remains alone; but if it dies, it produces much grain." Jesus speaks of Himself as the seed.

2 Corinthians 9:10 — "He who supplies seed to the sower and bread for the eater will supply and multiply the seed you have sown." Bread for today, seed for the future.

2 Corinthians 5:21 — Christ was made sin so that we might become the righteousness of God in Him.

Romans 8:1 — No condemnation to those in Christ Jesus.

Summary 2 of 7

Main Teachings (Part 1 of 2)

The moral law condemned everyone — the Ten Commandments are the moral law; every person has broken it; the consequence is condemnation, judgment, eternal separation.

Jesus satisfied the law, not just softened it — He took your place and received the full execution the law required. "It is finished" means the law has been fully satisfied.

You are not just forgiven — you are righteous — Christ's righteousness credited to your account moves you from minus 100 to plus 100.

Renewing the mind is essential — God already sees you righteous; your job is to start seeing yourself the way God sees you.

The white-clothes illustration — when you truly see yourself clothed in Christ, sin becomes uncomfortable from the inside out — not by guilt, by identity.

God deals with you as Father, not as Judge — the daughter with the red Beetle: he may take the keys but never the status. Status doesn't change; love doesn't change.

The cross did not cancel natural laws — Jesus' death dealt with the moral law's judgment and condemnation, but did not alter the laws woven into creation.

Natural laws reward understanding and cooperation — NASA's moon mission and the paratrooper's four-second window: cooperate precisely and you can do exploits.

Summary 3 of 7

Main Teachings (Part 2 of 2)

Sowing & reaping is both natural and spiritual — what Paul applies in Galatians 6, Jesus applied in Mark 4 to the kingdom itself. The law is a reliable, repeatable mechanism.

Prayer is not a substitute for sowing seeds — no farmer prays a harvest into an empty field. "If what you give is zero, God can pull out all the grace there is — if that grace comes upon zero, it will remain zero."

The feeding of the five thousand required seed — even the Son of God did not bypass the principle. He began by asking what seed was available, then added grace.

God supplies seed to the sower — bread for today, seed for your future. God multiplies the seed that is sown, not the seed that remains in your hand.

Sowing to the flesh produces corruption — living for yourself produces a harvest of thorns and thistles. This is not judgment; it is the law working against you.

Sowing to the Spirit produces everlasting life — benefits begin in the present and extend into eternity. Godliness is profitable for this life and the life to come.

Due season requires patience — different seeds, different timelines. Bishop's rubber-farm story: five years of sowing nearly lost to one decision in discouragement.

The cross is the greatest application of sowing and reaping — God planted Jesus as the seed to produce a harvest of many sons. You are that harvest. You carry the nature of the Seed.

You don't have to struggle — you need to believe and declare — love, joy, peace, hope are not attainments but inheritances. The believer's task is acknowledging what the law has already produced.

Summary 4 of 7

Biblical Principles

The law of sowing and reaping is a creation ordinance, not a suggestion — while the earth remains, it operates without exception (Genesis 8:22).

Prayer is not a substitute for sowing — every harvest begins with a seed — plant first, then pray for grace to multiply.

What leaves your hand does not leave your life — it goes ahead of you — the seed released into the kingdom is invested, not lost.

Soil and seed together determine the harvest — sow to the Spirit in fertile ground (the household of faith) and reap righteousness and everlasting life.

Due season comes for every seed — but patience is required to receive it — weariness is the enemy of the harvest, not the absence of a law.

Zero times any amount of grace is still zero — God multiplies seed that is sown, not seed withheld.

The law works both for you and against you — impartial. Sow corruption, reap corruption. Sow to the Spirit, reap life. The choice of seed type is yours.

The cross is the master pattern of sowing and reaping — one Son planted, a harvest of many sons. You are that harvest. This is not aspiration — it is identity.

Summary 5 of 7

Warnings & Practical Takeaways

Warning — Don't be deceived. "God is not mocked." The law operates whether you believe in it or not.

Warning — Don't confuse prayer with sowing. A farmer who prays over an empty field will wait forever.

Warning — Don't sow to the flesh. Living for yourself produces a harvest of corruption.

Warning — Don't grow weary before due season. Five years of sowing can be lost in a single decision made in discouragement.

Warning — Don't drag yourself back under condemnation. Christ has satisfied the moral law completely.

 

Takeaway — Declare daily: "I am the righteousness of God in Christ." Renewing the mind to your true identity is foundational.

Takeaway — Be intentional about what you are sowing. Every act of generosity, every act of service is a seed. Know what you are planting.

Takeaway — Prioritise the household of faith (Galatians 6:10) — local church, spiritual teachers, fellow believers in need.

Takeaway — Sow first, then pray. "Lord, send grace upon what I have planted — bring forth a hundredfold."

Takeaway — Stay in the field through due season. Plant. Water. Give thanks. Refuse to throw away your faith.

Takeaway — Receive your identity as the harvest. Love, joy, peace are in your DNA. Believe it, declare it, walk in it.

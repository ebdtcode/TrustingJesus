The Power of the Seed · Part 2

The Power of The Seed

Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Galatians 6:6–8 · The Law of Sowing & Reaping

Harvest Intercontinental Church, Olney · May 25, 2026

Transformation, Not Information

The goal of preaching is not information — it is transformation. But information only transforms when it moves from your head into your heart.

How the word reaches your heart

By hearing it over and over again. "Once the word gets into your heart, it changes you into the image of Jesus in that area." Hear last week's message; hear this one again and again.

Hear It — Then Share It

God didn't let us discover the internet for the world — He did it so this gospel could reach the whole earth. When the word blesses you, don't keep it. Don't just say amen — share it.

One shared message may lead someone to Christ, stop a suicide, or turn a life around — and you may never know it on this side.

The Law That Shapes Your Future

Last week's foundation: the things you do today determine what your future looks like. We don't control everything — but our choices, decisions, and seeds shape what is coming.

A natural and a spiritual law

Not every law of nature works in the kingdom — but the law of sowing and reaping operates in both. Today we go deeper into its spiritual dimension.

Part 1

Sowing in Galatians 6

The seed of gratitude and the choice of seed

v6 — Honor Those Who Teach You

"Let him who is taught the word share in all good things with him who teaches."

Galatians 6:6 (NKJV)

If you are fed spiritually through the ministry of those God has chosen, gratitude says: be a blessing to them.

Gratitude Is a Seed

If someone cooked you a meal every week and you never once said thank you — something would be wrong. Some have sat under the word for years and never once said, "Pastor, thank you for that word."

"It's important for God's people to show gratitude." Saying thank you is itself a seed.

v7 — You Will Reap What You Sow

"Do not be deceived, God is not mocked; for whatever a man sows, that he will also reap."

Galatians 6:7 (NKJV)

Sow gratitude into your pastors, and you will reap — more than you sowed. You sow one, you don't get one back.

Be Careful How You Hear

"Therefore take heed how you hear. For whoever has, to him more will be given..."

Luke 8:18 · cf. Mark 4:24 (NKJV)

The value you place on the word determines what you receive. Treat it as the greatest thing this week, and it will bless you beyond imagination.

v8 — Two Kinds of Seed

Sow to the flesh

"He who sows to his flesh will of the flesh reap corruption." You cannot sow to the flesh and reap life — that's sowing oranges and expecting mangoes.

Sow to the Spirit

"...but he who sows to the Spirit will of the Spirit reap everlasting life." The Spirit is life — in the present and in the future (Galatians 6:8).

You Have a Choice

"Que sera, sera — whatever will be, will be" is humanistic philosophy. It is not true.

You are not helpless about your future

God has given you a lot of control. You can decide: "I will not sow to the flesh, because I don't want to reap corruption. I will sow to the Spirit."

Spiritual Seeds Are Words & Deeds

  • Seeds are words and deeds — you sow with what you speak and what you do
  • Obey the flesh and you sow to the flesh — "cuss her good" reaps death and corruption
  • Obey the Spirit's prompting and you sow to the Spirit — words and deeds that follow His leading
  • You already know the difference — you don't need a theological degree to tell flesh from spirit

When Cursed, Bless

Jesus said: when they curse, you bless. When someone harms you, do them good.

Why? Because returning a curse only sows seeds for a harvest you don't want. Use the law to your advantage.

Part 2

Isaac: A Harvest in Famine

Genesis 26 — sowing where it makes no sense

God Said: "Stay"

"Do not go down to Egypt; live in the land of which I shall tell you. Dwell in this land, and I will be with you and bless you."

Genesis 26:2–3 (NKJV)

There was famine. The soil was dry, the natural law could not produce. Yet God said: stay right here.

The Reasonable Choice Was to Leave

Abraham faced famine and moved to Egypt. Isaac could reason, "When my father faced this, he moved — let's go before things get worse." Nothing wrong with that; that's wisdom.

But Isaac chose to hear the word of the Lord and obey it — staying where, naturally, it did not make sense. That obedience was itself a seed sown to the Spirit.

"Because I'm Blessed, I Will Plant"

What he did not say

"Because I am blessed and have God's promise, I don't have to plant. I'll just believe God."

What he did say

"Because I am blessed and favored, I will plant — and trust God's blessing to come upon my seed and multiply it beyond what is normal."

Prayer is not a substitute for sowing — but knowing you are blessed is the reason to sow.

A Hundredfold in the Year of Famine

"Then Isaac sowed in that land, and reaped in the same year a hundredfold; and the Lord blessed him. The man began to prosper, and continued prospering until he became very prosperous."

Genesis 26:12–13 (NKJV)

He sowed the physical seed and sowed his faith. He tapped into both realms.

Natural + Spiritual = Supernatural

Natural — he tilled and planted seed in dry ground
Spiritual — he acted in faith, obeying God's word to stay
Supernatural — a hundredfold where there should have been nothing

Sometimes the natural law isn't enough — combine it with the spiritual.

Your Harvest Is Not Limited by Your Environment

Child of God — you can still prosper during times of famine. Your harvest is not dependent upon, nor limited by, your environment.

Take the extra step

Do what you must in the natural — but don't stop there. Sow seeds in the kingdom: do what the Spirit says, believe what the Spirit says, and there will be a harvest of life.

Part 3

The Seed & the Soil

A good harvest needs both

What Are the Kingdom Seeds?

The seeds you sow in the kingdom are your words and your works:

  • Words of love, kindness, and faith
  • Works of love and works of faith
  • Giving, prayer, and service done in the name of the Lord

What Is the Soil?

God provides the seed and the soil. Soil =

Your heart — where you receive the word
God's church — the gathered body
God's work — the mission and ministry
God's people — especially those in need

Sow into the right soil, and you sow seeds God can multiply.

Part 4

The Widow of Zarephath

1 Kings 17 — sowing the last meal

Fed by Ravens

Elijah — the man of God, representing the work of God — was supplied supernaturally by ravens. But ravens are predators; they come to steal and take.

"God can take your enemies and make your enemy a blessing." If He can use a raven, our needs have a solution too.

Illustration

The Gambler's Gift

Oral Roberts was mocked for saying God would take him if he didn't finish the City of Faith. Then a rich gambler flew from Florida to Tulsa to give him millions — not out of love, but to "shut him up."

God used a man who didn't even like him. "God has many ways to fulfill His promises." When one door closes, He still has His own way.

Why the Brook Dried Up

Couldn't God have kept the brook flowing? Yes. But the water dried and the ravens stopped because that is how God wanted it — to move the work of God to where the widow was.

She was about to eat her last meal and die. God created a need in the prophet's life so she could have an opportunity to sow a seed and receive her miracle.

Why God Allows Lack in the Kingdom

The needs are an opportunity

Why does God allow lack in the church and in His work? So that you have an opportunity to plant seeds that become the harvest for the future you are trusting Him for. "But you've got to give Him the seed He can work with."

God could have said "Bread, multiply." He created the world. But He chose a pattern: the law of sowing and reaping, to supply needs supernaturally for His glory.

"Make Me a Small Cake First"

"Do not fear... but make me a small cake from it first... For thus says the Lord God of Israel: 'The bin of flour shall not be used up, nor shall the jar of oil run dry...'"

1 Kings 17:13–14 (NKJV)

A seemingly "wicked" command — until you see it was God working a miracle through the law of the seed.

Don't Be Manipulated — Don't Forsake the Law

The caution

Charlatans use this story to exploit people. Don't be pressured, manipulated, or forced. Be wise.

The balance

"Just because people abuse it doesn't mean the law intended to bless you is no longer there." Don't forsake it.

Every Harvest Begins With a Seed

The widow had enough revelation and faith to act on the word. In the natural you'd question it — but she was working with God.

She understood: "Every harvest begins with a seed."

Part 5

This Is Not Karma

The law of Christ is also at work

More Than a Mechanical Law

Karma says: every bad you do, you must reap bad. But the kingdom's law of sowing and reaping is not karma — because it involves a Person.

"Gravity works, but the law of lift can cause an airplane to fly." The law of seedtime and harvest is real — but there is also the law of Christ.

Mercy Uproots the Harvest You Deserve

You planted seeds to the flesh; you deserve that harvest; it's growing, about to manifest. Then, by grace, Jesus steps in — and just before the harvest, He aborts it, uprooting the seeds.

"Mercy withholds that which you deserve." Say: "Jesus, there's a harvest on the way — please uproot it by Your grace."

Grace: Vineyards You Did Not Plant

"I have given you a land for which you did not labor... you eat of the vineyards and olive groves which you did not plant."

Joshua 24:13 (NKJV)

Mercy withholds what you deserve; grace blesses you with what you did not plant. This does not cancel the law — it is added to it.

"Neither Do I Condemn You"

"Neither do I condemn you; go and sin no more."

John 8:11 (NKJV)

The woman caught in adultery sowed the seed; the harvest of stones was about to fall. Jesus uprooted it — but added a warning: "go and sin no more." Don't presume on grace; the law still works.

Part 6

Never Eat Your Seed

A season of supernatural supply

Bread to Eat — Seed to Sow

The widow didn't sow only once. Every day through the famine, God supplied enough bread to eat and enough seed to sow. The bin of flour and the jar of oil did not run dry.

"They ate the bread, and planted the seed by feeding Elijah." Sowing became a way of life.

A Season of Supernatural Supply

A proclamation

"I'm proclaiming it now upon your life, upon your family, upon this church — a supernatural supply that comes through the law of sowing and reaping."

Eat your bread. Sow your seed. Then declare the blessing and combine sowing with prayer — and watch God take something small and usher you into supernatural supply.

Consuming Your Seed = Consuming Your Future

It is tempting when what you have is small. But what you consume can never multiply.

Eat your bread — but never eat your seed.

Part 7

Supply Is More Than Money

The richest harvest is peace

A Trillion Dollars — or Peace?

The richest man — approaching a trillion dollars. Imagine the good you could do. But offered a trillion dollars to use however you wish, or the peace that Jesus gives…

"Please — take your trillion dollars and give me peace."

What good is a trillion dollars if you don't have peace?

Godliness Profits Both Lives

"...godliness is profitable for all things, having promise of the life that now is and of that which is to come."

1 Timothy 4:8 (NKJV)

Spiritual riches are far more valuable. Live this way even if you never saw a material harvest — yet the harvest does include financial and material blessings too.

Wisdom Nugget

Use the Law on Purpose

Before we let you go

Sow Intentionally to the Spirit

  • Do not ignore the law of sowing and reaping — use it to your advantage
  • Use it intentionally and purposefully — let it work for you, not against you
  • Always sow to the Spirit — never to the flesh
  • Teach your children and grandchildren — by word and by example

Stop

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

The Power of The Seed — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview

Part 2 of the law of sowing and reaping. Where Part 1 established that the law shapes your future, this message presses into its spiritual dimension and how to cooperate with it deliberately.

From Galatians 6:6–8, Bishop shows that the seed of gratitude toward those who teach you returns multiplied, that the value you place on the word determines your harvest, and that you always have a choice — sow to the flesh and reap corruption, or sow to the Spirit (with your words and deeds) and reap life.

Two Old Testament witnesses anchor the message: Isaac, who prospered a hundredfold in famine by combining natural planting with spiritual faith (Genesis 26), and the widow of Zarephath, who sowed her last meal and entered a season of unfailing supply (1 Kings 17). The kingdom's law is not karma: the law of Christ adds mercy (uprooting the harvest you deserve) and grace (blessing you with what you never planted). The closing charge: never eat your seed, treasure peace above riches, and teach the next generation to sow.

The goal of preaching is transformation, not information — the word only changes you once it moves from head to heart, by hearing it repeatedly. Share it so it can transform others.

Gratitude to those who teach you is a seed — honor and bless your pastors and teachers (Gal 6:6); the seed of thanks returns multiplied (Gal 6:7).

The value you place on the word determines your harvest — "take heed how you hear." Treasure it and you receive more.

You have a choice — you are not helpless — "que sera sera" is false. You can refuse to sow to the flesh and intentionally sow to the Spirit.

Spiritual seeds are words and deeds — obey the flesh and you sow corruption; obey the Spirit's leading and you sow life. When cursed, bless.

Isaac prospered in famine — he obeyed God's word to stay (a spiritual seed) and still planted physically; combining natural and spiritual produced a supernatural hundredfold.

"Because I'm blessed, I will plant" — the blessing is not a reason to stop sowing; it is the reason to sow with confidence. Prayer is not a substitute for sowing.

Your harvest is not limited by your environment — the spiritual law can produce where the natural law cannot.

Summary 4 of 8

Biblical Principles

Every harvest begins with a seed — nothing comes from sowing nothing.

Prayer is not a substitute for sowing — faith plants first, then asks God to multiply.

Natural + spiritual = supernatural — do what you must in the natural, then add faith and obedience to tap the spiritual.

The harvest matches the seed — sow to the flesh, reap corruption; sow to the Spirit, reap life. Mango seeds never grow apple trees.

The law of Christ is greater than karma — mercy withholds what you deserve; grace gives what you never earned.

Never eat your seed — what you consume cannot multiply; the seed is your future.

Your environment does not set the limit of your harvest — God's spiritual law operates above your circumstances.

Value determines reception — the worth you assign the word governs how much of it you receive.

Summary 5 of 8

Warnings & Cautions

Don't be deceived. "God is not mocked" — the law operates whether or not you believe it (Gal 6:7).

Don't sow to the flesh. Flesh-seeds (words and deeds from the flesh) always reap corruption.

Don't be ungrateful to those who feed you spiritually. Years of being fed without a single thank-you is a missed seed.

Don't let charlatans manipulate you — the seed principle is abused by some, so be wise; do not be pressured or forced.

But don't forsake the law either — abuse by some does not cancel a law meant to bless you.

Don't presume on grace. Jesus uproots a deserved harvest — but says "go and sin no more"; the law still works.

Don't eat your seed. Consuming it — however small — consumes your future.

Summary 6 of 8

Practical Takeaways

Hear the word repeatedly — then share it. Subscribe, listen again, and pass it on; one share may save a life.

Say thank you. Express gratitude to those who teach and pastor you — it is a seed.

Audit your seeds. Identify what you are sowing with your words and deeds; stop the flesh-seeds.

Sow even in famine. Don't wait for ideal conditions; plant, add faith, and trust God's blessing.

Sow into good soil. Prioritize God's church, God's work, and God's people.

Bless when cursed. Respond to harm with good to keep sowing life.

Keep bread, protect seed. Eat what you need, but never consume your seed; sow as a way of life.

Teach the next generation. Train your children and grandchildren to sow to the Spirit, by word and example.

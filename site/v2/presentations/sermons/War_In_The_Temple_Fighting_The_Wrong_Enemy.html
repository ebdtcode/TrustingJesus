Jehovah Nissi — Our Banner

The Lord My Banner

The name Moses gave God after deliverance from the Amalekites. In medieval warfare, the banner was raised before battle to rally the troops. Jehovah Nissi goes before us in every fight.

"He comes with His Word to make you stand.
He always has a better plan when He comes."

The Wound of Offense

"An offended friend is harder to win back than a fortified city."

Proverbs 18:19 (NLT)

To offend = to cause anger, resentment, upset, or hurt feelings. Offense fortifies. The longer it stays, the harder the wall becomes.

Identity

You Are a Temple

Individually. Collectively. The dwelling place of God.

The Spirit Dwells — Not Visits

"Don't you realize that your bodies are the temple of the Holy Spirit, who lives in you and was given to you by God? You do not belong to yourself..."

1 Corinthians 6:19 (NLT)

Not "live" — dwell. He didn't come to visit. He's staying.

Together, One Holy Temple

"In him the whole building, joined together, rises to become a holy temple in the Lord. And in him you too are being built together to become a dwelling in which God lives by his Spirit."

Ephesians 2:21-22 (NIV)

"You temple, I temple — all of us together, a temple, and the Spirit of the Lord lives inside us."

Spiritual History

A History of Coups

Heaven. Eden. Calvary. The ongoing insurgency.

Three Coups, One War

1. Heaven — Failed Coup

Lucifer attempted, defeated, cast down (Rev. 12:7-9)

2. Eden — Successful Coup

Devil deceived, seized dominion over earth

3. Calvary — Counter-Coup

"Friday He was crucified... but He rose. Counter-coup complete."

The defeated one has never given up his desire for dominion.

God Wrote Your Days

"You saw me before I was born. Every day of my life was recorded in your book. Every moment was laid out before a single day had passed."

Psalm 139:16 (NLT)

The Enemy's Determination

By any means necessary — sin, your family, those who love you most — he is determined that you will not live the days God wrote.

The War

Three Things Every War Requires

Opposing sides. A battleground. Weapons.

Opposing Sides

"For we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities, against powers, against the rulers of the darkness of this world, against spiritual wickedness in high places."

Ephesians 6:12 (KJV)

"There is no on the fence."

You are either fighting for, or fighting against.

The Battleground

Inside The Temple

The battle is fought in you, in me, in the body of Christ. The enemy wants to depose the One on the throne — by any means necessary.

He's a master counterfeiter. You can only counterfeit what's real. His weapons (thoughts, words) are imitations of God's.

The Name

Knowledge. Remembrance. Stay.

Fighting in the Temple Is Not New

"I implore Euodia and I implore Syntyche to be of the same mind in the Lord. And I urge you also, true companion, help these women..."

Philippians 4:2-3 (NKJV)

Two women laboring together had a disagreement serious enough that Paul named them in an epistle. The model: acknowledge, refuse to escalate, seek reconciliation, invite wise counsel.

The Apology Rule

Don't Say:

"I'm sorry, but..."

Say:

"I'm sorry."

Full stop. No deflection. No restatement of the offense.

Study Notes

Sermon Summary

War In The Temple — Pastor Gledy Wariebi

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview

Pastor Gledy Wariebi exposes offense as the enemy's "greatest weapon of mass destruction" against the Body of Christ.

Drawing from Proverbs 18:19 — an offended friend is harder to win back than a fortified city — she traces Satan's primary tactic: infiltrating the temple (both individual and corporate) and shifting the focus of war so that God's people fight each other instead of him.

Her central charge: be a lover and a fighter, but fight the right enemy. Refuse offense as an act of will. Stand behind God. Deploy the four weapons He has provided: the Blood, the Name of Jesus, the Word, and the Spirit.

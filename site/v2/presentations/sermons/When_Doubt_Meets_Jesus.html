Study Notes

Sermon Summary

When Doubt Meets Jesus — Bishop Darlingston Johnson

Key points, principles, and takeaways

Overview & Key Scriptures

In this Easter Sunday message, Bishop Johnson uses the story of Thomas (John 20:24–29) to show how Jesus patiently moves an honest skeptic from categorical unbelief to total personal surrender. Organized around four movements — Proof of Truth Required, Revealed, Received, and Rewarded — the sermon demonstrates that Christianity does not demand blind faith; it offers a risen, living Savior who provides evidence to the sincere seeker, then calls for a personal decision to surrender.

Key Scriptures:

John 20:24–29 — The primary text. Thomas demands physical proof; eight days later Jesus appears and provides it. Thomas responds: “My Lord and my God.” Jesus declares: “Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed.”

1 Corinthians 15:14, 17 — “If Christ has not been raised, your faith is futile.” The foundation: Christianity rises and falls on the resurrection.

John 14:6; John 10:10 — Jesus came not to teach philosophy but to give life. “I am the way, the truth, and the life.” “I have come that they may have life abundantly.”

Hebrews 10:25; Matthew 18:20 — Don’t forsake the assembling. Jesus reserves certain revelations for the corporate gathering.

Romans 8:35–39 — Nothing — not even shut doors — can separate us from God’s love.

Proverbs 18:21 — Death and life are in the power of the tongue. Jesus is always listening — speak words of life.

John 8:36; Romans 5:8 — Surrendering to Jesus is surrendering to love and freedom, not bondage.

John 11:25–26 — “I am the resurrection and the life.” Jesus demonstrated every claim He made.

1 Corinthians 2:9 — Eyes have not seen all that God has in store for those who love Him.

Summary 1 of 5

Main Teachings: Truth Required & Revealed

1. Christianity Is Based on a Living Person — Unlike other religions founded on teachings, Christianity is based on a divine person who conquered death. Jesus didn’t come to give a philosophy to dead people; He came to give life to the spiritually dead. If He is not risen, He cannot help anyone and our faith is in vain (1 Corinthians 15:17).

2. Thomas Was an Honest, Intelligent Skeptic — Bishop Johnson reframes Thomas not as “Doubting Thomas” but as “Intelligent Thomas” and “Honest Thomas.” Thomas understood that if he believed, he would have to preach — and preaching this truth would likely cost him his life. He was too intelligent to die for something unverified. His demand for physical proof was reasonable given the stakes.

3. The Gathering Is Non-Negotiable — Thomas missed Jesus’ first appearance because he was absent from the believers’ gathering. Jesus intentionally waited eight days and appeared only when Thomas was present with the church. He could have visited Thomas privately but refused. Principle: there are things Jesus reserves exclusively for the corporate gathering (Hebrews 10:25; Matthew 18:20).

4. Shut Doors Cannot Shut Jesus Out — The Bible intentionally notes the doors were shut, yet Jesus appeared. No barrier — physical, circumstantial, or spiritual — can keep Jesus from you or separate you from His love (Romans 8:35–39).

5. Jesus Is Always Present and Listening — Jesus quoted Thomas’s exact words from eight days earlier, though He was not physically present when Thomas spoke them. Jesus hears everything — complaints and praise alike. Since He responds to our words as He responded to Thomas’s, we should speak words of life, faith, and blessing (Proverbs 18:21).

Summary 2 of 5

Main Teachings: Truth Received & Rewarded

6. Jesus Demonstrates Every Claim — Jesus does not require blind faith. He backs His claims with evidence: “I am the light of the world” → He opened blind eyes; “I am the bread of life” → He fed 12,000+ from five loaves and two fish; “I am the resurrection and the life” → He raised Lazarus after four days (John 11:25–26). He gives sincere seekers enough evidence to believe.

7. The Resurrection Evidence Is Overwhelming — More than 512 eyewitnesses saw Jesus alive. Ten disciples saw Him simultaneously (ten people don’t hallucinate the same thing). The Romans and Jewish leaders could not produce the body. The disciples were transformed from hiding cowards to bold preachers who all died for their testimony — nobody dies for a lie they know they created.

8. From “I Will Not Believe” to “My Lord and My God” — Thomas’s response was deeply personal: not “You are Lord” but “my Lord and my God.” He went from having a question to having a Savior. This is the proper response to the evidence: personal surrender. Thomas preached this truth for the rest of his life, eventually dying for it in India.

9. Many Refuse to Follow the Evidence — The problem is not insufficient evidence but unwillingness to surrender. People keep asking questions to avoid the conclusion the evidence demands. They love their sin and their independence more than salvation. The real question is not “Is there enough evidence?” but “Will you surrender?”

10. Surrendering to Love, Not Bondage — The enemy deceives people into thinking surrender means bondage, slavery, and lost happiness. The opposite is true: the greatest freedom comes from surrendering to Him who came to set you free (John 8:36). “I surrender to love” (Pastor Glennie). God commended His love while we were yet sinners (Romans 5:8).

11. The Reward: Inclusion in the Heavenly Family — Those who believe receive: membership in the heavenly family, inheritance of the family’s fortune (“all that the Father has is yours in Christ”), the gift of righteousness (God sees the perfection of His Son in you), and eternal life. Eyes have not seen all that God has in store (1 Corinthians 2:9).

Summary 3 of 5

Principles & Warnings

Timeless Principles:

• Christianity stands or falls on the resurrection — if Christ is not risen, faith is futile (1 Corinthians 15:17).

• Jesus does not require blind faith — He provides evidence to the honest, sincere seeker.

• The corporate gathering is irreplaceable. Some things Jesus only reveals when the church is assembled together.

• No door of circumstance can separate you from God’s love or keep Jesus from reaching you.

• Jesus is always present and always listening — our words carry the power of life and death.

• The evidence demands a personal response — not just intellectual acknowledgment, but personal surrender (“my Lord and my God”).

• Surrendering to Jesus is surrendering to love and freedom, never to bondage.

Warnings:

• Against neglecting the gathering — If you have the habit of not being present, you have the habit of missing what Jesus wants to say to you. Thomas’s absence cost him eight days of doubt.

• Against careless speech — Jesus hears everything. Don’t fill your mouth with gossip, complaining, or unbelief when He is listening. He responds to your words like He responded to Thomas’s.

• Against perpetual skepticism — Don’t hide behind “one more question” when the evidence is already sufficient. The real issue may be unwillingness to surrender, not lack of evidence.

• Against loving sin more than salvation — Many refuse the evidence not because it’s weak but because they love their independence and their sins more than they love freedom in Christ.

• Against the lie that surrender is bondage — The enemy’s deception: “If you surrender you’ll lose your joy and freedom.” The truth: whom the Son sets free is free indeed (John 8:36).

Summary 4 of 5

Takeaways & Call to Action

Practical Takeaways:

• Be faithful to the corporate gathering — don’t miss what Jesus reserves for the assembled church.

• Guard your tongue — since Jesus is always present and listening, speak words of life, faith, and praise rather than doubt and complaint.

• Follow the evidence to its conclusion — don’t keep asking questions when the evidence is enough. Let it lead you to personal surrender.

• Make it personal — move from “He is Lord” to “my Lord and my God.” Christianity is not a theory to accept but a person to receive.

• Surrender today — for those who have never received Christ, stop postponing the decision. For believers, identify where you’ve been holding back and make a fresh surrender.

• Rejoice in your inheritance — you are part of the heavenly family, an inheritor of the family’s fortune, clothed in the righteousness of Christ.

Call to Action from Bishop Johnson:

• For non-believers: “Surrender to faith. Surrender to love. Receive the gift of eternal life.” Christ brought you to this message to invite you to surrender to Him whose only purpose and desire for you is to love you.

• For believers: “Since you have believed and received, begin to live and act like it’s true. Surrender to His love and become obedient to His word. In what areas of your life have you not surrendered to Him?”

• For all: “On this resurrection morning, experience a resurrection in your relationship with Christ.” The time is now. Stop doubting and start believing. Do what Thomas did — surrender.

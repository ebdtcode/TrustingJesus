# Connecting To God's Power And Supply
**Speaker:** Bishop Darlingston Johnson
**Church:** Harvest Intercontinental Church (Olney)
**Date Preached:** April 27, 2026
**Series:** Part 2 — "God Is Your Source of Supply"
**Part 1 Foundational Text:** Philippians 4:19

---

## 1. Overview

Building on Part 1, which established Philippians 4:19 as a divine decree and eternal law of supply, Bishop Johnson turns to the critical question of human responsibility: what must a believer do to experience this promise in its fullness? The sermon's central answer is that the promise is dependent on power, and that power has already been delivered to every believer — the same power that raised Christ from the dead. The believer's responsibility, therefore, is not to acquire power but to connect to the power already within, and that connection happens through one word: agreement. Agreement — aligning one's will, words, ways, and work with God's — is the spiritual equivalent of plugging into the power source. Where promises are not manifesting, the problem is never God; it is always a connection problem.

---

## 2. Key Scriptures

### Philippians 4:19 — Foundational Decree
> "And my God shall supply all your need according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus."

**Application:** Bishop Johnson reaffirms this is not merely a promise but a decree — a divine law established by God, using the language of "shall" (as in the Ten Commandments: "Thou shalt"). It is an eternal, covenantal commitment to every believer. It covers every geography, every economic season, and every era — including eternity. Jesus is identified as the source, the channel, and the qualifier of this supply. The promise applies specifically to those in covenant with God through Christ, not universally.

---

### Matthew 7:11 — The Father's Desire to Give
> "If you then, being evil, know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father who is in heaven give good things to those who ask Him!"

**Application:** Used to establish why God wants our needs supplied. God is a Father, and it is entirely natural — not even supernatural — for a loving father to want his children's needs met. Jesus' argument from the lesser to the greater: if even sinful, selfish human fathers provide for their children, God's desire to provide is incomparably greater. This reframes need-meeting as an expression of God's fatherly love, not just power.

---

### Matthew 6:33 — Kingdom Priority Over Personal Provision
> "But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things shall be added to you."

**Application:** God knows what His children need before they ask. Because He is a Father who sees and knows, believers can orient their lives around His kingdom rather than around their own provision. The promise of supply frees us to prioritize kingdom work. One of the chief reasons God wants all our needs met is so we are free to participate in His work on earth without distraction.

---

### Ephesians 3:20 — The Power That Is Already In You
> "Now to Him who is able to do exceedingly abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that works in us."

**Application:** The key phrase is "the power that works in us." This confirms that God's supply-producing power is not located in heaven waiting to be summoned — it has already been delivered to the believer at salvation. The believer is not praying for power to come; the power is already present and operational within them. The responsibility is to connect to that indwelling power.

---

### Amos 3:3 — Agreement as the Condition of Partnership
> "Can two walk together, unless they are agreed?"

**Application:** God spoke this to Israel to explain why they were not experiencing His blessings at the level He desired. The disconnection was not God's failure but a lack of agreement. God declares He will not move from His perfect will, word, and ways to align with ours. We must be the ones to move. Agreement is the biblical term for what we would call "connection" in modern language. To walk with God — to experience the flow of His power and provision — requires alignment on four levels: will, words, ways, and work.

---

### Luke 11:2–3 — The Lord's Prayer: Surrender Before Petition
> "When you pray, say: Our Father in heaven, hallowed be Your name. Your kingdom come, Your will be done on earth as it is in heaven. Give us this day our daily bread."

**Application:** The structure of the Lord's Prayer is itself a teaching on connection. Jesus deliberately orders the prayer so that declaration of surrender ("Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done") precedes petition ("Give us this day"). Verse 2 is in the imperative mood — it is not a request; it is a declaration of alignment and surrender. This must come first. To request provision before declaring surrender is to attempt to draw on power without being connected to the source.

---

### Matthew 26:39 — Christ's Example of Surrender in Gethsemane
> "O My Father, if it is possible, let this cup pass from Me; nevertheless, not as I will, but as You will."

**Application:** Jesus modeled in Gethsemane the very pattern He taught in the Lord's Prayer. His natural will was to avoid the cross, but He deliberately surrendered that will to the Father's. Bishop Johnson draws the line from that surrender on Friday to the resurrection on Sunday: the power that raised Christ was released precisely because surrender and alignment had been established. Surrender is the gateway to resurrection power.

---

### James 4:7 — The Sequence: Submit First, Then Resist
> "Therefore submit to God. Resist the devil and he will flee from you."

**Application:** The sequence is non-negotiable — submit first, then resist. Many believers attempt to resist the enemy without first coming into submission and alignment with God. Submission (alignment, agreement, connection) is what makes resistance effective. Once a believer is surrendered to God's will, they carry the authority to demand that contradicting circumstances, sickness, lack, or the enemy's work flee. The power to enforce the demand flows from the prior act of surrender.

---

### Hebrews 3:1 — Jesus as High Priest of Our Confession
> "Therefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our confession."

**Application:** This is one of the most critical applications in the sermon. "Confession" literally means "to say the same thing" — it means agreement. Jesus is the High Priest of our agreement with the Father's will and words. His intercessory, advocating ministry on our behalf is directly tied to whether we are speaking in agreement with what God has declared. When we confess doubt, fear, or words that contradict God's promises, we are not giving Jesus anything He can advocate for. We tie His hands. To speak the Word — to agree with what God has said — is to activate the full power of Christ's high priestly ministry on our behalf.

---

### 2 Corinthians 4:13 — The Spirit of Faith: Believe and Speak
> "And since we have the same spirit of faith, according to what is written, 'I believed and therefore I spoke,' we also believe and therefore speak."

**Application:** The spirit of faith operates in two movements: first believing, then speaking. Agreement with God must be expressed verbally. It is not enough to believe internally while speaking doubt externally. Speaking the Word — declaring God's promises aloud — is the act of alignment that connects the believer to the power behind those promises.

---

### 2 Corinthians 1:20 — "Yes" and "Amen" as Acts of Agreement
> "For all the promises of God in Him are Yes, and in Him Amen, to the glory of God through us."

**Application:** Every promise of God carries a "Yes" in Christ. Our "Amen" is our stated agreement with that "Yes." Bishop Johnson calls believers to say "Amen" to God's promises — not as a liturgical habit, but as a conscious declaration of alignment. To say "Amen" to Philippians 4:19 is to connect to the power behind that decree. Refusing to say "Amen" while circumstances look bleak is precisely the kind of disconnection that prevents the promise from manifesting.

## 3. Main Points / Teachings

### Point 1 — Recap: Philippians 4:19 Is a Decree, Not Just a Promise

The sermon opens by reinforcing Part 1's foundation: Philippians 4:19 uses the word "shall" — the language of divine law and royal decree. When God used "Thou shalt" to establish the Ten Commandments, He was establishing laws, not making suggestions. Philippians 4:19 carries that same force. It is a law God has established that operates in the earth, that He is Himself committed to enforcing when the conditions are met.

This decree is:
- **Eternal** — it covers both time on earth and eternity in heaven, because believers are eternal beings.
- **Universal in geography** — whether you are in America, Africa, Asia, or Europe, the law applies.
- **Covenantal and relational** — this is not a universal promise to all humanity. It is a covenantal promise to those who are in Christ Jesus, who have the legal right to call God their Father.
- **Mediated by Christ** — "by Christ Jesus" means Jesus is the source, the channel, and the qualifier of this supply. He is responsible for making this provision happen.

Believers can walk in peace regardless of economic circumstances because this law is a constant even when circumstances fluctuate.

---

### Point 2 — Why God Wants Our Needs Supplied

Two reasons are given:

**Reason 1 — God Is a Father Who Loves His Children.**
It is entirely natural — not even supernaturally extraordinary — for a loving father to want his children's needs met. Jesus makes the argument from lesser to greater (Matthew 7:11): if even fallen, selfish human fathers provide for their children, how much more does the perfect Father desire to supply His children's needs? God's supply is not reluctant; it is His pleasure, His joy, His delight.

**Reason 2 — So We Are Free to Participate in His Kingdom Work.**
God's ultimate purpose in meeting all our material and physical needs is to free us from anxiety about provision so we can serve Him without distraction (Matthew 6:33). As Bishop Johnson stated in the closing: "One of the reasons God wants all your physical material needs to be met is so you can be free to serve and to push forward His work." Need-meeting is not an end in itself; it is the enabling foundation for kingdom participation.

---

### Point 3 — The Promise Is Dependent on Power; My Responsibility Is to Connect

The critical transition of the sermon: the promise requires power to be fulfilled, and that power does not operate automatically. My responsibility — which God has left squarely in my hands — is to connect to the power behind the promise.

"Behind this promise is power that will bring it to pass. For God to supply all of our needs is going to take the power of God to do so. It is my responsibility and your responsibility to connect to that power behind the promise. And if we connect to the power behind the promise, the power behind the promise will make the provision."

---

### Point 4 — The Power Company Illustration

The sermon's central, extended illustration: Every house with electricity has been wired by the power company. The power company has delivered the power — it is already present in the wires within the walls. But the power company did not take for itself the authority to decide whether the TV works or the lights shine. That authority rests entirely with the homeowner. You decide whether to plug in.

The power is present. The benefit is available. But only when the connection is made does the power operate.

Bishop Johnson then shares the personal anecdote: he called Verizon, convinced they were the problem, only to discover that the cable box had been unplugged (by Pastor Chris). "The problem was not Verizon. The problem was not the television. It was not the cable box. The problem was simply a connection problem."

**The spiritual application:** God has delivered the power that raised Christ from the dead to your address. It lives in you. It is not in heaven waiting to be summoned. At salvation, He placed that power in every believer. The issue, therefore, is never God (the power company). The issue is never a shortage of power. The issue, when promises are not manifesting, is a connection problem.

"No connection, no operation."

---

### Point 5 — The Power Is Already in You (Ephesians 3:20)

This is a critical doctrinal point: "God is able to do exceedingly, abundantly above all we ask or think, according to the power that is at work inside of us." The power is already inside the believer. Believers are not praying for power to descend from heaven — they are connecting to power already resident within them. The greatest joy is not that the power is in heaven; it is that it is already inside you.

---

### Point 6 — Check the Connection; Don't Assume It Is Strong

Just as a dropped cell signal requires you to find a stronger location, believers must periodically check their spiritual connection. Don't assume the connection is strong. Subtle things break connection without notice — unresolved anger that hardens into bitterness, for instance, can disconnect without the believer realizing it ("click — nothing"). When you sense the power is not flowing as it should, don't blame God. Instead, diagnose: Where is the disconnection? What happened? And move to reconnect.

The cell phone signal illustration: when the connection weakens, you don't throw away the phone (there's nothing wrong with the phone). You move to find a stronger signal. Likewise, when God's power seems blocked, you don't abandon the promise. You move yourself into alignment.

---

### Point 7 — How to Connect: AGREEMENT — Will, Words, Ways, Work

The Bible's word for connection is "agreement" (Amos 3:3). Agreement is alignment on four levels:

**1. Will** — My desires, my wants, my agenda must align with God's will. "Thy will be done — not mine." This is the act of surrender that precedes all else.

**2. Words** — My speech must align with God's Word. I cannot speak everything that is in my mind if my mind contradicts God's declaration. "You speak your mind, and your mind will get you in real trouble." Instead, I align my mouth with what God has said. "My God shall supply all my need" — regardless of what I see.

**3. Ways** — My habits, temperament, conduct, and character must align with God's ways: ways of truth, love, generosity, and kindness. Natural temperament (Bishop Johnson humorously references "September people" having quick tempers) must yield to the character of God.

**4. Work** — My daily activity and life's work must align with God's kingdom work. Am I building my kingdom, or am I building His? This is the question of ultimate life alignment.

When these four are in alignment, you are connected to the power. When any of them is misaligned, the connection is weakened or severed.

---

### Point 8 — God Will Not Move; We Must Move (Faucet and Cup Illustration)

"In your kitchen, there's a faucet. That faucet has water coming out of it. In your hand is a cup. If you want water in that cup, that faucet is not moving to the cup. The cup will have to move to the faucet."

God's will is perfect. His word is perfect. His ways are perfect. He has no reason and no intention of adjusting Himself to align with our imperfect will, words, or ways. The movement required is entirely ours. When promises are not being experienced, we are not the faucet — we are the cup. We must move. God's expectation is communicated plainly in Amos 3:3: He told Israel, "I am not going to change. You must come into agreement with Me."

---

### Point 9 — The Lord's Prayer Pattern: Surrender Before Petition

The structure of the Lord's Prayer (Luke 11:2–3) is a teaching model on connection. Jesus designed it so that:

- **Verse 2** = Declaration of surrender and alignment ("Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done") — this is imperative mood, a declaration, not a request.
- **Verse 3** = Petition for provision ("Give us this day our daily bread") — this is the request, and it comes second.

"Before you request power, you must first surrender to power."

To say "Thy kingdom come" is to surrender your right to your own kingdom. To say "Thy will be done" is to surrender your will to His. This is the connection that makes the petition effective. Many believers habitually skip the surrender and go straight to the petition — and then wonder why the power does not flow.

---

### Point 10 — Declare Surrender, Then Demand Surrender

Once you have declared your surrender to God's will, you are now empowered to make demands of opposing circumstances. Bishop Johnson draws the distinction:

- **Declaration of surrender** = "Your kingdom come, Your will be done" (directed to God — alignment).
- **Demand for surrender** = "Lack, leave. Sickness, go. Circumstances, line up" (directed at contrary realities — authority).

The first empowers the second. You cannot effectively command circumstances to align with God's will if you have not first aligned yourself with that will. But once you have surrendered, you can demand that anything in your life contradicting His will must submit to His will — to which you have already submitted.

This is the exact pattern of James 4:7: "Submit to God" (first) — then — "Resist the devil, and he will flee." The sequence is not reversible.

---

### Point 11 — Jesus Is High Priest of Our Confession (Agreement)

Hebrews 3:1 reveals that Jesus holds the office of High Priest specifically in relation to our "confession." Confession (Greek: homologia) means "to say the same thing" — agreement. Jesus is the High Priest of our agreement with the Father's words and will.

His high priestly ministry — intercession, advocacy, applying His blood on our behalf, standing before the Father and saying "I advocate for them" — is activated by and dependent upon our agreement. When we speak in alignment with God's Word, Jesus takes that agreement into the very presence of the Father and advocates powerfully on our behalf.

But when we speak doubt, fear, complaint, or words that contradict God's promises — "I don't know how my needs are going to be met" — we give Jesus nothing to advocate for. He cannot be High Priest of disagreement. He cannot advocate for doubt. He cannot apply His blood to words that contradict the Father.

"When you speak words that contradict what God has said, you are in essence disconnecting from the high priestly ministry of Jesus. You are tying His hands."

Speaking doubt or fear in an area of promise does not cost you your salvation, but it does disconnect you from the power of Christ's priestly ministry in that specific area. "You're on your own."

---

### Point 12 — The Primary Purpose of Prayer Is to Overcome OUR Resistance

One of the most theologically significant statements of the sermon: "The primary purpose of prayer is to overcome our resistance, not to overcome His. The primary purpose of prayer is to bring our lives in alignment."

Prayer is not the means by which we persuade a reluctant God. God is not the resistant party. We are. Prayer — rightly understood — is the process by which we bring our will, words, ways, and work into alignment with His. When that alignment is achieved, requests are answered quickly, and the power to produce results is able to operate freely.

## 4. Principles

The following are timeless biblical principles drawn from the sermon:

1. **The Decree Principle** — Philippians 4:19 is not a hope but a law: a divine decree that God is committed to enforcing for every believer in every geography and for all eternity.

2. **The Delivery Principle** — The power of God to supply all your needs has already been delivered to your address. It is resident within every believer. There is no shortage; there is no supply problem.

3. **The Connection Principle** — "No connection, no operation." The availability of power does not guarantee the experience of power. Connection is required. Connection is the believer's responsibility.

4. **The Agreement Principle** — Connection to God's power is established through agreement: aligning your will, words, ways, and work with His. Agreement is the biblical word for connection.

5. **The Movement Principle** — God will not move to align with you. You must move to align with Him. You are the cup; He is the faucet. Move the cup.

6. **Surrender Precedes Supply** — You must declare surrender before you can request provision. "Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done" must always precede "Give us this day."

7. **Surrender Empowers Authority** — Once you have surrendered to God's will, you are authorized to demand that contradicting circumstances submit to that will. Submission to God is what makes resistance of the enemy effective (James 4:7).

8. **The Confession Principle** — Confession simply means to say the same thing. To agree with God's Word verbally is to activate the high priestly ministry of Jesus Christ on your behalf.

9. **The High Priest Principle** — Jesus is High Priest of our confession (agreement), not of our doubt. His advocacy is activated by our alignment and rendered inoperative by our disagreement.

10. **The Purpose of Prayer** — Prayer is not to overcome God's resistance. It is to overcome our own. Prayer's primary function is alignment, not persuasion.

11. **Kingdom Freedom Principle** — God supplies all our needs so we are free from anxiety over provision and free to focus on advancing His kingdom purposes on the earth.

---

## 5. Warnings and Cautions

1. **Do not blame God when promises seem unfulfilled.** The problem is never God. His supply is perfect, His power is perfect, and His will to provide is perfect. If the promise is not manifesting, look at the connection, not at the power company.

2. **Do not allow circumstances to determine your outlook.** Circumstances are variable. The decree of Philippians 4:19 is constant. "Job or no job, my God shall supply all my need." The believer's outlook must be anchored in the law, not in the present condition.

3. **Do not speak your own mind out of alignment with God's Word.** "Some of you should not speak your mind, because your mind is crazy." Speaking fear, doubt, complaint, or contradiction of God's promises is the spiritual equivalent of pulling the plug. It directly deactivates Christ's high priestly advocacy in that area.

4. **Do not allow anger to harden into bitterness.** Unresolved anger that becomes bitterness is a silent connection-breaker. It can happen without the believer noticing it — like a cable being unplugged in the night. Regular connection checks are necessary.

5. **Do not attempt to resist the enemy without first submitting to God.** The sequence of James 4:7 is fixed: submit first, then resist. Attempting to exercise spiritual authority without alignment is operating on severed connection.

6. **Beware of "Crowd Christianity" — handling things yourself.** Some believers are capable people who convince themselves, "I don't need God. I can figure this out." God will not force Himself on you. But this self-reliance is a deliberate disconnection from the power that would supply far more than self-effort ever can.

7. **Do not skip surrender and go straight to petition in prayer.** Beginning prayer with "Give me" before "Thy will be done" is an attempt to draw provision without first establishing connection. The Lord's Prayer pattern must be honored: alignment before asking.

8. **Do not confuse infrequent or weak results with God's inability.** When the signal is weak, the problem is the connection, not the network. Do not lower your expectation of God; raise the standard of your alignment with Him.

---

## 6. Practical Takeaways

1. **Periodically check your connection.** Do not assume it is strong. Audit your will, words, ways, and work against God's. Ask: "Am I aligned? Is there anything in my speech, my temperament, my conduct, or my daily work that is not in agreement with God?"

2. **Align your will with God's will.** In prayer and in daily decision-making, intentionally surrender your preferences, plans, and desires to God's will. "Not my will, but Yours."

3. **Align your words with God's Word.** Stop speaking doubt and start declaring the promises. "My God shall supply all my need" — say it, regardless of circumstances. The act of speaking the promise is an act of connection.

4. **Align your ways with God's ways.** Where your conduct, temperament, and relational patterns contradict God's character — truth, love, generosity, kindness — intentionally adjust. God's ways are ways of love.

5. **Align your work with God's kingdom work.** Evaluate whether what you are spending your strength and energy building serves God's kingdom purposes or solely your own agenda.

6. **In prayer, always declare surrender before making petition.** Pray "Thy kingdom come, Thy will be done" as a genuine act of alignment before presenting any request. Make the surrender real, not merely liturgical.

7. **Once surrendered, exercise your authority.** After aligning yourself with God's will, speak directly to the contradicting circumstances in your life. Demand that lack, sickness, and every opposing force submit to the will of God to which you have already submitted.

8. **Hold fast to your confession.** Do not allow fluctuating circumstances to dislodge you from the declaration of faith. "I don't care what my circumstances look like. I don't care how bad the economy is. This is what I know: My God shall supply all my need." Hold it. Don't let go.

9. **Say "Amen" to God's promises as a deliberate act.** Every time you hear or read a promise of God, respond with an intentional "Amen" — a stated agreement. You are activating the High Priestly ministry of Jesus every time you do so.

10. **Move toward God; don't wait for God to move toward you.** The cup moves to the faucet. Position yourself under the flow by drawing near to God in worship, in His Word, and in yielded prayer.

## 8. Prayer Points

### Prayer Point 1 — Surrender of Will (Alignment with God's Will)
**Focus:** Personal alignment — surrendering my will to God's

Father, in the name of Jesus, I come before You today and lay down my own will, my own agenda, and my own plans. Like Jesus in Gethsemane, I say to You, "Not my will, but Yours be done." Your kingdom come in my life. Your will be done in my family, my finances, my health, and my future — on earth as it is in heaven. I choose to move my cup to Your faucet. I will not wait for You to align with me. I move to align with You. Establish Your will as the governing reality of my life, in Jesus' name.
**Scripture:** Matthew 26:39 — "Not as I will, but as You will." / Luke 11:2 — "Your kingdom come, Your will be done."

---

### Prayer Point 2 — Repentance for Disconnection
**Focus:** Repentance for anger, bitterness, doubt, complaint, and self-reliance

Lord, I acknowledge that I have not always kept my connection to You strong. There are areas of my life where I have allowed unresolved anger to become bitterness, where I have spoken doubt and fear instead of faith, where I have complained instead of declared Your promises, and where I have handled situations in my own strength without consulting You. I repent. I ask You to reveal every broken connection in my life. Cleanse me from every root of bitterness, every habit of doubt, and every tendency toward self-reliance. Restore my connection to the power behind Your promises, in Jesus' name.
**Scripture:** James 4:7-8 — "Submit to God... draw near to God and He will draw near to you. Cleanse your hands... and purify your hearts."

---

### Prayer Point 3 — Activating the Resurrection Power Within
**Focus:** Awakening to the indwelling power of God already resident in every believer

Holy Spirit, I thank You that at salvation You delivered the power that raised Jesus Christ from the dead to my address — and that power lives in me right now. I do not need to beg for power to come. I need to connect to the power that is already present. I declare that the same Spirit who raised Christ from the dead is alive and active in me. I receive and release that power today. Let it operate in every area of my life — spirit, soul, body, and circumstance — according to Your will and purpose, in Jesus' name.
**Scripture:** Ephesians 3:20 — "According to the power that works in us." / Romans 8:11 — "He who raised Christ from the dead will also give life to your mortal bodies through His Spirit who dwells in you."

---

### Prayer Point 4 — Aligning Speech with Scripture (Confession of Faith)
**Focus:** Declaring God's promises rather than circumstances

Father, I choose today to align my words with Your Word. I will no longer speak my mind when my mind contradicts Your declaration. I will not speak fear, doubt, or complaint in the areas where You have made promises. I declare in faith: My God shall supply all my need according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus — regardless of what I see, regardless of the state of the economy, regardless of my current circumstances. As I speak this in agreement with You, I activate the High Priestly ministry of my Lord Jesus Christ on my behalf. I hold fast to this confession and I will not let it go.
**Scripture:** Philippians 4:19 / 2 Corinthians 4:13 — "I believed and therefore I spoke." / Hebrews 3:1 — "Consider the...High Priest of our confession."

---

### Prayer Point 5 — Standing Firm on the Decree of Philippians 4:19
**Focus:** Corporate intercession for the congregation standing on this promise amid specific pressures

Lord, we stand together as Your people on the decree of Philippians 4:19. We say "Amen" — we are in agreement with this promise. For every family in this congregation facing financial pressure, job loss, unexpected expense, or uncertainty about the future: we declare that You, our God, shall supply every need according to Your riches in glory by Christ Jesus. Not according to the economy. Not according to what the world offers. According to Your riches. In glory. By Christ Jesus. We are in agreement with this decree. We hold fast to it as a community of faith, and we refuse to let circumstances move us from this foundation.
**Scripture:** Philippians 4:19 / 2 Corinthians 1:20 — "All the promises of God in Him are Yes, and in Him Amen."

---

### Prayer Point 6 — Alignment of Words, Ways, and Work
**Focus:** Holistic alignment — a prayer of consecration across all four dimensions

Father, I present myself to You as a living sacrifice today. I align my will with Your will — I surrender my desires and embrace Yours. I align my words with Your Word — let the meditation of my heart and the words of my mouth be acceptable in Your sight and in agreement with what You have declared. I align my ways with Your ways — let truth, love, generosity, and kindness mark my walk. And I align my work with Your kingdom work — let everything I do with my strength, my time, my resources, and my ability serve Your kingdom purposes. I am connected. Let the power behind the promise now flow freely in my life, in Jesus' name.
**Scripture:** Amos 3:3 — "Can two walk together unless they are agreed?" / Psalm 19:14 — "Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in Your sight."

---

### Prayer Point 7 — Freedom from Anxiety to Serve the Kingdom
**Focus:** Receiving supply so we are free to participate in God's kingdom work

Lord Jesus, You said "Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things will be added to you." I receive Your provision today not just for my own comfort, but so that I am freed from anxiety and preoccupation with my own needs — so I can fully participate in what You are doing on the earth. Let Your supply release me into kingdom service. Let provision flow so that I am not distracted by lack, but empowered by abundance to be a blessing to others and to advance Your purposes. I trust You as my supply so I can give myself fully to Your kingdom.
**Scripture:** Matthew 6:33 / Philippians 4:19 — "according to His riches in glory by Christ Jesus."

---

### Prayer Point 8 — Holding Fast to the Confession of Faith
**Focus:** Corporate declaration — corporate intercession for steadfastness

We pray, Lord, for the grace to hold fast — to not let go of the confession of our faith when circumstances are dark, when the economy fluctuates, when the report is bad, or when provision seems delayed. Give us the spiritual tenacity to keep saying "My God shall supply" when the bank account says otherwise. Give us the courage to agree with You publicly when the world around us speaks fear. We are a people of the decree. We will not be moved. We will hold fast to our agreement, because You are faithful and You are committed to this law You have established. We trust You.
**Scripture:** Hebrews 10:23 — "Let us hold fast the confession of our hope without wavering, for He who promised is faithful." / Hebrews 3:1 — "Consider the Apostle and High Priest of our confession."

---

### Prayer Point 9 — Activating Christ's High Priestly Ministry Through Agreement
**Focus:** Intercession — agreeing with God's Word to release Christ's advocacy on our behalf

Lord Jesus, You are the High Priest of our confession. You are advocating for us at the right hand of the Father. Today we choose to give You something to advocate for. We align our words with the Father's Word. We agree with His will. We say "Amen" to every promise He has made. Advocate for us, Lord Jesus. Apply Your blood to our needs. Stand before the Father and say, "I advocate on their behalf." As we align our confession with His Word, we release Your full High Priestly ministry in our lives, our homes, our church, and our nation. Work on our behalf, Lord Jesus. We are in agreement with the Father.
**Scripture:** Hebrews 3:1 / Romans 8:34 — "Christ Jesus...is at the right hand of God and is also interceding for us."

---

### Prayer Point 10 — Breaking Patterns of Disconnection in the Congregation
**Focus:** Congregational intercession — breaking cycles of disconnection

Father, we intercede for every member of this congregation who has experienced a pattern of disconnected prayer — where requests seem to go unanswered, where promises feel distant, where the power seems blocked. By Your Spirit, reveal the precise point of disconnection: is it unsubmitted will? Is it unaligned words? Is it ways that contradict Your character? Is it work that builds a personal kingdom rather than Yours? Reveal it, Lord — and grant the grace to move. Today we break every pattern of disconnection in this church. We choose agreement. We choose alignment. We plug back in to the power behind every promise, in Jesus' name.
**Scripture:** Amos 3:3 / James 4:7-8 — "Submit to God...draw near to God and He will draw near to you."

---

### Prayer Point 11 — Prayer of Thanksgiving and Adoration
**Focus:** Adoration — thanking God for the decree of supply and the gift of indwelling power

Father, we thank You. We thank You that You did not merely issue a vague promise — You established a decree. You said "shall," and Your word does not fail. We thank You that You are a Father who takes joy in supplying the needs of Your children. We thank You that You did not leave the power in heaven but delivered it to our address — that the power that raised Jesus from the dead lives in us right now. We thank You for Jesus, our High Priest, who stands ever to advocate for us. We thank You that nothing about our situation surprises You, and that You have already made provision. We praise You, Father, for Your unfailing faithfulness to Your own decree, in Jesus' name. Amen.
**Scripture:** Philippians 4:19 / Ephesians 3:20 / Matthew 7:11 — "How much more will your Father...give good things to those who ask Him."

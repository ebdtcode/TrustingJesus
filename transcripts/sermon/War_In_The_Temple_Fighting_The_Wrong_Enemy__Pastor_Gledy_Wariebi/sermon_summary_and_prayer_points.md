# War In The Temple: Fighting The Wrong Enemy

**Speaker**: Pastor Gledy Wariebi (Gledy Badu Wariebi)
**Church**: Harvest Intercontinental Church-Olney (HICC-Olney)
**Date**: May 3, 2026 (Sunday)
**Foundational Text**: Proverbs 18:19 (NLT)

---

## 1. Overview

Pastor Gledy Wariebi delivers a spiritually incisive message on offense as the enemy's "greatest weapon of mass destruction" against the Body of Christ. Drawing from a rich tapestry of Scripture, she exposes Satan's primary tactic: infiltrating the temple — both the individual believer and the corporate church — and shifting the focus of the war so that God's people fight each other instead of fighting the true enemy. The sermon calls every believer to refuse offense as an act of will, not by feeling, to know their identity as temple-dwellers indwelt by the Holy Spirit, and to stand behind God armed with the four weapons He has provided: the Blood, the Name of Jesus, the Word, and the Holy Spirit. Pastor Gledy's central charge is clear — be a lover and a fighter, but fight the right enemy.

## 3. Main Points / Teachings

### Opening: Jehovah Nissi Is Our Banner

Pastor Gledy opens with the song "Jehovah Nissi Came." Jehovah Nissi — "The Lord My Banner" — is the name Moses gave God after deliverance from the Amalekites (Exodus 17). In medieval warfare, the banner was raised before battle to rally the troops. The lyrics declare: "He comes with His Word to make you stand. He always has a better plan when He comes." The sermon is addressed specifically to someone struggling with offense — whether they have been offended or have offended another — and Jehovah Nissi is the assurance that God's presence goes before them in that fight.

---

### Definition: What Does It Mean to Offend?

"To offend" means to cause anger, resentment, upset, or hurt feelings in someone through what we say or do to them. The sermon deliberately addresses the one who has been offended — not the offender — because we have no control over the offender, only over ourselves. The preacher acknowledges her friend who always asks, "Why don't you talk about the person who offends?" Her answer: "You are a good Christian sister — then act like one and forgive." The sermon's scope is the offended person, described in the foundational text as "harder to win back than a fortified city."

---

### Identity: We Are Temples — Individually and Collectively

Based on 1 Corinthians 6:19 and Ephesians 2:21-22, Pastor Gledy establishes that:

- **Individually**: Each believer's body is the temple of the Holy Spirit. He does not merely "visit" — He DWELLS. He has taken up permanent residence because of what Jesus did.
- **Collectively**: Together, the church is a single holy temple for the Lord. You temple, I temple — together, one dwelling place.
- **Mutual indwelling**: "God lives in me, and I live in Him." Pastor Gledy calls this a "seeming contradiction," comparing it to the other paradoxes of God's nature: Prince of Peace and Yahweh Sabaoth (Lord of Hosts), Beginning and Ending, Judge and Advocate. We do not need to fully understand it — we receive it by faith.

---

### The History of Coups

Pastor Gledy frames spiritual history as a series of coup attempts, counter-coups, and ongoing insurgency:

**a) The First Coup Attempt — Heaven**
Lucifer attempted a coup in heaven (Revelation 12:7-9). He was defeated and cast down to earth with his angels. The one who deceives is now on earth among us — and when he lies, you cannot detect the difference between truth and the lie without God's help, because he is the father of lies.

**b) The Coup d'Etat — Eden**
The devil succeeded in deceiving Adam and Eve, seizing dominion over the earth. This was the coup in Eden.

**c) The Counter-Coup — Calvary**
Jesus executed the counter-coup at Calvary. On Friday He was crucified; Satan thought he had won. Saturday, Sunday — demons were still celebrating. But on the third day He rose. Counter-coup complete. "He rose and gave us heaven's open door."

**d) The Defeated One Who Never Gives Up**
Though defeated, the enemy has never surrendered his desire for dominion. He wants insurrection. He wants rebellion. He is determined — by any means necessary — that you will not live the days God wrote in His book (Psalm 139:16). He does not care what means he uses: sin outside of marriage, lying, cheating, drugs, your children, your spouse, those who love you most. His only intent is to stop you from fulfilling God's purpose for your life.

---

### The Required Elements of War

**1. Opposing Sides**
In any war, there must be two sides. Our sides:
- Us: the temple-dwellers — those in whom God's Spirit dwells.
- Them: Satan, principalities, powers, rulers of darkness, spiritual wickedness in high places (Ephesians 6).

There is no "on the fence." You either fight for God or against. Fence-sitting is not a neutral position — it is surrender to the wrong side.

**2. The Battleground**
The battle is fought in the temple — in you, in me, in the body of Christ, in the local church. The enemy's goal is to depose the One who sits on the throne of your life. He wants to infiltrate and instigate rebellion from within.

**3. Weapons — Provided by God (2 Corinthians 10:3-5)**
God has equipped His temple-dwellers with four essential weapons:

- **The Blood**: It is through the blood of Jesus that we are enlisted — that we become temples at all. It is the foundation of everything.
- **The Name of Jesus**: This name reminds the enemy of his defeat. At the name of Jesus, every knee must bow — principalities, powers, and even those who practice dark arts say "if God agrees," because they know this name carries supreme authority (Philippians 2:8-11).
- **The Word of God**: The Word overcomes the thoughts the enemy plants. When fear thoughts came about a second surgery, reading 2 Timothy 1:7 gave the weapon: "God did not give me this spirit of fear — why would I take it?" The Word must be brought inside to displace what the enemy puts in the mind.
- **The Holy Spirit**: The Spirit speaks through wise counsel — the person who says "leave it, it's okay" — and through His own voice inside us, pointing us back to God's truth. When someone offers wise counsel and we reject it because we are feeling bad, we are choosing feelings over the Spirit's voice.

---

### The Master Counterfeiter

Satan is a master counterfeiter. You cannot counterfeit something that does not exist — there is no counterfeit $3 bill. Every counterfeit exists because something real preceded it. The enemy's two primary weapons are counterfeits of God's own weapons: **words** and **thoughts**. He uses thoughts to infiltrate the battleground of the mind. He uses words to wound, divide, and stir offense. Because he counterfeits God's tools, we must not be surprised that the battle comes through language and thoughts — but we are equipped with the real versions of those same weapons.

---

### The Battle of the Mind: Pastor Gledy's Testimony

Walking with a cane following a surgery that had not gone well, Pastor Gledy faced fear thoughts about a second necessary surgery. The enemy came with thoughts: "The first time didn't go well — the second time will be worse. Just leave it." These were rebel thoughts — fear thoughts.

Then she read 2 Timothy 1:7: "God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power, love, and a sound mind."

Her response: "He didn't give me this spirit of fear. Why would I want to take it?" She then addressed the thought directly: "Please, go and sit down in the back row. God has not given me a spirit of fear. I am not taking it."

This testimony directly applies to offense: offense lives in the mind exactly the way fear thoughts live in the mind. They come from the same source. They must be dealt with the same way — by pulling them down with the Word (2 Corinthians 10:3-5), refusing to receive what God did not give, and meditating on what He DID give instead.

---

### Pull Down Strongholds

Any thought that exalts itself against the knowledge of God must be pulled down. When offense thoughts come — "See how she talked to you. See how Pastor Gledy didn't speak back to you. See the kind of pastors we have here" — tell the enemy directly: "Go and sit behind me. Back row of the church." God has given us mighty weapons precisely for this purpose.

---

### "You Are Not Your Feelings"

Bishop Johnson once declared from this pulpit: "You are not your feelings." Pastor Gledy testifies that this single sentence changed her life. The key insight:

- You do not have control over how you feel.
- You DO have control over how you react to your feelings.

The supreme example is Jesus in the Garden of Gethsemane. He was not "feeling it" at all — He was sweating drops of blood, asking if the cup could pass from Him. But He did not act on those feelings. He reached the place where He said, "Not My will, but Yours." He submitted His feelings to the Father's purpose. We are called to do the same.

You are not your feelings. Stand up and fight against your feelings. This is the same act of will required to drop offense — not because you feel like forgiving, but because you choose to.

---

### How the Enemy Shifts the War

The enemy's most dangerous move is infiltrating the temple and shifting the focus of the war. Instead of fighting him, we end up fighting each other — and in so doing, we fight the war FOR him. He has his weapons (rebel thoughts, counterfeit words) and his greatest weapon of mass destruction is offense. When believers become offended with one another, they redirect their spiritual energy away from the real enemy and onto their brothers and sisters. The battle has been shifted. We are fighting the wrong enemy.

---

### The Pattern of Judas (John 13:2, 27)

The enemy first put a thought in Judas's mind: betray Jesus. Judas did not immediately resist it or cast it down. He let it stay. He "mused on it," mixed it in — added, as Pastor Gledy illustrates, some "Maggi cubes" to the pot. By John 13:27, Satan had not just planted a thought — he had entered Judas entirely.

This is the pattern of unaddressed rebel thoughts:
1. The enemy plants the thought.
2. You allow it to remain.
3. You meditate on it (in the wrong direction).
4. The enemy gains entry.

Apply this to offense: the thought begins — "See what they did to you." If you do not cast it down immediately, it grows. You revisit it. You elaborate on it. Eventually, offense has taken up full residence — and the enemy now has a foothold in the temple.

---

### The Enemy's MO: John 10:10

The enemy does not come to check how things are going. He does not come just to see the church. Every time he comes, his motive is singular: "to steal, kill, and destroy" (John 10:10). When offense comes, it comes to steal your joy, kill your relationships, and destroy the unity and witness of the church.

His greatest weapon of mass destruction in the temple is offense — because it accomplishes all three simultaneously without the offended person recognizing the real aggressor.

---

### The Answer: Refuse to Be Offended — Stand Behind God

Pastor Gledy gives the direct prescription: REFUSE to be offended. Know who you are. Know why you came to this church. Know who brought you here.

The song "Stand Behind God" encapsulates the posture:

> "Stand behind God... For the battle is His, and He gives you the victory. Every Goliath is killable. Every battle is winnable by His power and might when you stand behind God."

God says, "The battle belongs to Me — but the victory is yours." He asks us to "go up and meet them" not so we fight alone, but so that when the enemy is defeated in our presence, we remember what he looks like in defeat. The next time he comes, we can say: "I saw you the last time, defeated. I know how this ends."

---

### The Enemy Returns at a More Opportune Time

The enemy's most subtle tactic is patience. He did not get you the first time — so he left for a season (as he did with Jesus in the wilderness, Luke 4:13) and returned at a more opportune moment. He counterfeits God's weapons of words and thoughts. He comes back. He tries again. This is why we must remain fully armed and vigilant, not assuming a victory in one battle means the war is over.

---

### House Rules for the Temple

These are the standing rules God requires of His temple-dwellers:

**a) Knowledge — Know God's Word**
You need to know what God says, because the enemy fights with thoughts and words. The only way to recognize a counterfeit is to know the real thing. Fill your mind with the Word so you have something to fight back with.

**b) Remember Who Brought You to This Church**
Pastor Gledy testifies: she was in Liberia with a group of women praying for God to send them a teacher. Bishop Darlingston Johnson came and preached on the Amalekites. She wept from beginning to end. She recognized the answer to her prayer. It was not Bishop Johnson who brought her — it was God who brought her through him. Therefore, "Even Bishop Dr. Darlingston G. Johnson cannot make me leave." The one who called you here knew in advance who you would meet, who would offend you, what you would face. He is not surprised. Don't let offense make you forget the Author of your assignment.

**c) Don't Leave Because of Offense**
If you leave a church because of offense, when you arrive at the new church you start at the back of the platoon. Nobody knows you. You lose your place, your spiritual deposit, and your investment in that community. If God legitimately calls you to leave, your pastor writes a letter of commendation and you arrive with your reputation intact. But leaving in offense sends you to the back of the line. The Liberian reference to the last platoon in a parade captures the humiliation and loss of ground.

---

### Be a Lover and a Fighter

The closing charge summarizes the sermon's dual call:
- **Be a FIGHTER** — fight the actual enemy (Satan, principalities, powers, rebel thoughts, offense itself).
- **Be a LOVER** — love your brothers and sisters in the temple with you.

Do both simultaneously. The two are not contradictory — they are the very combination that defeats the enemy's strategy. He wants us to fight each other and fail to love. We defeat him by reversing his plan: fight him, love each other.

---

### Fighting in the Temple Is Not New

Philippians 4:2-3 shows that conflict in the church predates the 21st century. Euodia and Syntyche — two women who had labored together in the gospel — had a disagreement serious enough that Paul named them publicly in an epistle. He did not take sides. He appealed to them: "Settle your disagreement." He then asked a trusted partner to help them reconcile. The model is clear: acknowledge the conflict, refuse to escalate it, seek reconciliation, and involve wise counsel when needed.

---

### Apology Rules

To those who offend because they "have big mouths" — who say things quickly, impulsively, the way of their tribe or temperament — Pastor Gledy gives a direct rule:

When you apologize, do NOT say "I'm sorry, but..." The word "but" cancels the apology and restates the grievance. Just say: "I'm sorry." Full stop. Nothing more.

---

### Closing: Salvation Invitation and Giant Slayer Declaration

Pastor Gledy closes with an invitation — to those who are not yet temple-dwellers, to receive Jesus as Lord and Savior, and to those who carry offense, to come for prayer. Offense is real. She does not minimize it. She testifies: a woman came and took her husband. "If that's not a big offense, I don't know what is." She knows offense. She knows forgiveness. She knows what it costs.

Her declaration: "My name is Gledy Badu Wariebi — **giant slayer**. I went to the land of unforgiveness, and I slew the giants in that land."

The invitation to all: You can leave today — not by feeling, but by an **act of your will** — and say, "God, I am dropping this thing." He will help you. That is the promise.

## 4. Principles

These timeless biblical principles are drawn directly from the sermon:

1. **"You are not your feelings."** — You have no control over how you feel; you only have control over how you react to your feelings. (Bishop Johnson / Gethsemane model)

2. **"Refuse to be offended."** — Refusing offense is not a feeling; it is an act of the will. It is a decision made in obedience to God.

3. **"Stand behind God."** — When offense comes and the battle is hot, get behind God. The battle is His; the victory is yours.

4. **"Don't fight the wrong enemy."** — Satan's goal is to get temple-dwellers fighting each other. Identify the real enemy and direct your warfare correctly.

5. **"Be a lover and a fighter."** — Fight the enemy with everything you have, and love your brothers and sisters in the temple with equal passion.

6. **"He comes with His Word to make you stand."** — Jehovah Nissi always comes with His Word. The Word is the weapon that makes you stand when your back is against the wall.

7. **"He is a master counterfeiter."** — Satan can only counterfeit what is real. His weapons (thoughts and words) are imitations of God's weapons (the Word and the Spirit). Know the real thing so you can recognize the fake.

8. **"The greatest weapon of mass destruction is offense."** — Offense is not just a personal wound; it is a strategic weapon deployed by the enemy against the unity and effectiveness of the Body of Christ.

9. **"There is no on the fence."** — In spiritual warfare, neutrality does not exist. You are either a temple-dweller fighting for God or you are not.

10. **"The enemy is determined you will not live the days God wrote."** — Psalm 139:16 declares your days were written before you lived them. The enemy's entire campaign is to prevent their fulfillment.

11. **"Don't let rebel thoughts dwell."** — Thoughts that are not taken captive become strongholds. The Judas pattern is the warning: a thought planted, permitted, and pondered becomes a full possession.

12. **"Drop it by an act of will, not by feeling."** — Forgiveness is not a feeling. It is a decision. God will help you after you decide.

## 5. Warnings and Cautions

1. **Do not fight the wrong enemy.** Fighting your brothers and sisters in the church is exactly what Satan wants. When you do, you are fighting the war for him.

2. **Do not let rebel thoughts stay.** The Judas pattern is the warning: a thought that is permitted and pondered eventually opens the door to full demonic entry. Address thoughts immediately (2 Corinthians 10:5).

3. **Do not let offense take you over.** Once offense is fully seated in the mind, you become "harder to win back than a fortified city" (Proverbs 18:19). Act before it fortifies.

4. **Do not leave your church because of offense.** If you leave in offense, you start at the back of the platoon at the new church. You lose spiritual ground, community investment, and your God-ordained assignment in that local body.

5. **Do not sit on the fence in spiritual war.** There is no neutral ground. Fence-sitting is effectively fighting against God, because the enemy will use your passivity as an opening.

6. **Do not speak your mind when your mind contradicts God's Word.** The battleground is the mind. When rebel thoughts arise, do not give them voice — give your tongue the Word of God instead.

7. **Do not apologize with "but" attached.** "I'm sorry, but..." is not an apology — it is a reopening of the wound. Just say, "I'm sorry."

8. **Do not act on your feelings.** Jesus did not act on His feelings in Gethsemane. You are not your feelings. Your feelings are not your authority; God's Word is.

9. **Do not forget who called you to your local church.** Offense can make you forget your assignment. The enemy uses offense to uproot you from your God-appointed place of growth and service.

10. **Do not receive what God did not give you.** Fear, offense, bitterness — these are not gifts from God. "God has not given us a spirit of fear." If He didn't give it, don't take it.

## 6. Practical Takeaways

These are specific, actionable steps drawn from the sermon:

1. **Bring every thought captive to the obedience of Christ (2 Corinthians 10:5).** Identify rebel thoughts as they arise — fear, offense, resentment — and take them captive immediately before they grow into strongholds.

2. **Tell rebel thoughts to "go sit in the back row."** Use your words to address thoughts directly. Pastor Gledy's model: "Please, go and sit down in the back row. God has not given me a spirit of fear. I am not taking it." Apply the same declaration to offense thoughts.

3. **Deploy the four weapons in every battle:**
   - The Blood — rest in your identity as a temple purchased by the blood of Jesus.
   - The Name of Jesus — speak it over every situation, reminding the enemy of his defeat.
   - The Word — find the specific Scripture that addresses your specific battle and meditate on it.
   - The Holy Spirit — listen to the wise counsel He sends. When someone says "let it go," consider that it may be the Spirit speaking.

4. **Meditate on Philippians 4:8 things.** Do not just read the Word — ponder it. Sit with it. Let it unfold. Meditating on 2 Timothy 1:7 showed Pastor Gledy two sides: what God did NOT give (fear) and what He DID give (power, love, sound mind). Meditation reveals both sides of the truth.

5. **Stand behind God when offended.** Resist the impulse to retaliate, defend yourself, or rehearse the offense. Get behind God and let the battle be His.

6. **Refuse offense as an act of will, not by feeling.** You may not feel like forgiving. Forgive anyway. Decide. "God, I am dropping this thing." He will help you carry out what you decide.

7. **Be a lover of your brothers and sisters in the temple.** Actively and intentionally cultivate love for those in your local church, especially those who are most difficult.

8. **Settle disagreements — follow the Philippians 4:2-3 model.** When there is conflict, acknowledge it, seek reconciliation, and do not let it fester. Invite wise counsel to help mediate when needed.

9. **When you apologize, just say "I'm sorry."** Remove the "but." A genuine apology stands alone without qualifications or deflections.

10. **Remember who called you to your local church and stay.** Trace your assignment back to God — not to any person who may have hurt you — and let that remembrance anchor you in your place.

11. **Go up and meet the enemy in battle.** Do not avoid spiritual confrontation out of passivity. Engage, so that when the enemy is defeated in your presence, you know his face in defeat and are not intimidated the next time he comes.

12. **Know the enemy's MO.** He steals, kills, and destroys (John 10:10). He returns at a more opportune time (Luke 4:13). He counterfeits God's weapons. Knowing his tactics strips him of the element of surprise.

## 8. Prayer Points

### Prayer Point 1 — Repentance for Fighting the Wrong Enemy
**Focus**: Personal repentance for misdirected warfare and harboring offense

Heavenly Father, we confess that we have allowed offense to shift our focus from the real enemy to our brothers and sisters. We have fought the wrong enemy and carried wounds that were never ours to keep. Forgive us, Lord, for every moment we have directed our spiritual weapons against flesh and blood rather than against principalities and powers. We repent of every fortified wall of offense we have allowed to build in our hearts. Restore our focus to the true enemy and cleanse us of all bitterness and resentment. As Ephesians 6:12 declares, our battle is not against flesh and blood — Lord, help us live like we believe it. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 2 — Activating the Four Weapons
**Focus**: Fully deploying the Blood, the Name, the Word, and the Spirit

Lord, we thank You that You have not sent us to war unarmed. Today we activate every weapon You have provided. We plead the blood of Jesus over our minds, our families, and this local church — the blood that made us temples and enrolled us in Your army. We lift the name of Jesus over every principality and power assigned against us — for at that name, every knee must bow and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord (Philippians 2:10-11). We take up Your Word as our sword, declaring that the weapons of our warfare are not carnal but mighty in God for pulling down strongholds (2 Corinthians 10:4). And Holy Spirit, speak through wise counsel, speak through Your Word, and give us ears to hear what You are saying. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 3 — Bringing Every Thought Captive
**Focus**: Warfare over the mind — casting down rebel thoughts, offense, and fear

Father, we take authority right now over every rebel thought in our minds. Every thought of offense that exalts itself against the knowledge of God — we pull it down. Every fear thought, every thought of resentment, every voice that says "see how they treated you" — we bring it captive to the obedience of Christ (2 Corinthians 10:5). We declare: we will not let rebel thoughts dwell. We refuse to be a Judas who lets the enemy's suggestion sit until it becomes a possession. Lord, guard our minds with Your peace that surpasses all understanding. Give us the discernment to recognize the enemy's counterfeit thoughts and the courage to deal with them immediately. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 4 — Power, Love, and Sound Mind Over Fear
**Focus**: Rejecting the spirit of fear; receiving what God actually gave (2 Timothy 1:7)

Lord, Your Word is clear: You have NOT given us a spirit of fear — but You have given us power, love, and a sound mind (2 Timothy 1:7). Right now, every person here who has been carrying fear thoughts — about health, about the future, about relationships, about what people think — we declare: God did not give you this. Do not take it. We reject the spirit of fear in every form. We receive what You DID give: power to overcome, love that casts out fear, and a sound and disciplined mind. Let this Word drop deep into every heart that has been tormented by fear, and let it be the weapon that sends those thoughts to the back row — far from the throne of our minds. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 5 — Healing from Offense; Releasing Forgiveness as an Act of Will
**Focus**: Deep healing from past and present offenses; releasing forgiveness by decision

Father, we acknowledge that offense is real. You are not asking us to pretend we were not hurt. The wound is real — but so is Your power to heal it. Right now, every person carrying an offense — whether it happened years ago, months ago, or this week — we invite Your healing into that place. We choose by an act of our will — not by feeling, but by decision — to drop this offense. Lord, we are dropping it. As Pastor Gledy declared, we go to the land of unforgiveness today and we slay the giants there. We release every offender into Your hands. We bless those who hurt us. We declare that no person has the power to steal the days You wrote for us in Your book (Psalm 139:16). Heal us completely. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 6 — Standing Behind God in Current Battles
**Focus**: Posturing ourselves behind God in the battles we are currently facing

Lord, we take our position right now — behind You. Every battle we are facing: the battle of the mind, the battle in our families, the battle in this church, the battle for our health and purpose — we stand behind You. The battle belongs to You, and the victory belongs to us. We go up to meet the enemy not in our own strength but in the power of Your might. We declare: every Goliath we are facing is killable. Every battle we are in is winnable — by Your power and might, as we stand behind God. Lord, let us see the enemy defeated in our presence, so that the next time he comes we recognize him and remember that we have seen this before — and he lost (Ephesians 6:13). In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 7 — Unity in the Temple (The Euodia and Syntyche Prayer)
**Focus**: Reconciliation and unity among believers in the local church

Father, just as Paul appealed to Euodia and Syntyche — "Please, because you belong to the Lord, settle your disagreement" (Philippians 4:2) — we appeal to You now on behalf of every unresolved conflict in this temple. We pray for every strained relationship in this church. We pray for those who have been hurt by leadership, by fellow members, by misunderstandings, by careless words. Lord, let the same Spirit who inspired Paul to write that appeal now move in every heart that is fortified behind offense. Send true partners and wise counsel to help mediate where walls have been built. Restore relationships. Restore trust. Make us one — the way You and the Father and the Holy Spirit are one — so that the world will know we are Your disciples by the love we have for one another (John 13:35). In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 8 — Grace for Apology and Reconciliation
**Focus**: For those who have offended — humility to apologize fully and correctly

Lord, we pray for every person who knows they have offended a brother or sister and has not yet made it right. Give them the humility to go. Give them the words — the right words, not "I'm sorry, but..." but simply "I'm sorry." Full stop. No deflection, no restatement of the offense, no self-justification. Let the power of a genuine apology do what no explanation can do — break down walls and restore fellowship. And for those who receive the apology, give grace to receive it fully, to let the matter rest, and to move forward in genuine restored relationship. Let reconciliation flow in this temple. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 9 — Recognizing and Heeding Wise Counsel
**Focus**: Discerning the Holy Spirit's voice through those who say "let it go"

Holy Spirit, we thank You that You speak through people. You get inside the mouth of a trusted friend, an elder, a pastor, a spouse, and You say, "Leave it. It's okay." Forgive us for the times we have dismissed wise counsel because we were too deeply in our feelings to hear You. Give us ears to hear when You are speaking through another person — especially when what they are saying is the last thing our flesh wants to hear. Let us not mistake the voice of the Spirit for weakness. Give us the discernment to know that the one who tells us to forgive is not siding with our offender — they are siding with our destiny. We receive wise counsel today, in Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 10 — Knowing Why God Brought You to This Local Church
**Focus**: Reclaiming divine assignment in the local body

Father, just as You led Pastor Gledy from Liberia to HICC-Olney — not through the arm of flesh but through answered prayer — remind every member of this congregation why You brought them here. Let every person recall the moment of clarity when they knew: "This is where God wants me." Let that remembrance be an anchor when offense comes and the enemy whispers "leave." Lord, You knew who we would meet here. You knew what we would face. You wrote it in the book. You are not surprised. Help us to stay in our lane, in our post, in our platoon — and to complete the assignment You gave us in this house. Let no offense steal the deposit that has been growing in us here. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 11 — Standing on the Days God Wrote (Psalm 139:16)
**Focus**: Protecting God's written purpose against every plan of the enemy

Lord, Your Word declares that every day of our lives was recorded in Your book before a single one came to be (Psalm 139:16). We stand on that Word today. The enemy is determined — by any means necessary — that we will not live those days. He comes through sin, through our closest relationships, through fear, through offense, through distraction. But we declare: the days You wrote will be lived. The purpose You ordained will be fulfilled. No weapon formed against us will prosper (Isaiah 54:17). No Amalekite will steal our assignment. No offense will derail our destiny. We lift the name of Jehovah Nissi over our lives and declare: He comes with His Word to make us stand. He always has a better plan. In Jesus' name, Amen.

---

### Prayer Point 12 — Speaking Life: Replacing Rebel Thoughts with God's Word
**Focus**: Training the tongue and mind to declare God's truth over every lie

Father, we confess that too often we have spoken our minds when our minds contradicted Your Word. We have given voice to offense, to fear, to resentment — and in doing so we have agreed with the enemy rather than with You. Today we make a covenant with our mouths. We will give our tongues something to do: declare Your Word. We will meditate on what is true, honorable, right, pure, lovely, and worthy of praise (Philippians 4:8). We will say, "God has not given me a spirit of fear." We will say, "Every Goliath is killable." We will say, "The battle is the Lord's." We will say, "I am dropping this offense by an act of my will." Lord, let the Word of Christ dwell richly in us, so that when the enemy comes with his counterfeits, we already have the real thing — and we know the difference. In Jesus' name, Amen.
